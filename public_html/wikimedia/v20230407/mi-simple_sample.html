Ionuț Silaghi de Oaș, Count de Szilágyi-Oaș[1] (i whanau i te ra o Maehe 16, 1990) he kaikaiata Roma[2][3][4], kohikohi o nga waiata tangata, kaituhi, me te ingene.
Ionuț Silaghi de Oaș, Count de Szilágyi-Oaș[1] (born March 16, 1990) is a Romanian artist[2][3][4], collector of folk music, writer and engineer.
Ko ia te upoko o te whanau o Szilágyi-Oaş me te Tumuaki Tuhituhi o Ansamblul Oaşul.[5][6][7] He kaitohutohu ia o te Whare Whanui Hangarau o Cluj-Napoca me te Babeş-Bolyai University.
He is the current head of the Szilágyi-Oaș noble family and the current Secretary General of Ansamblul Oaşul.[5][6][7] He is a graduate of the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca and of the Babeş-Bolyai University.
I oti ano ia ia te tohu paetahi i te Whare Wananga Hangarau o Cluj-Napoca.[1][8]
He also completed a master's degree at the Technical University of Cluj-Napoca.[1][8]

Ko te taitara o te taitamariki o Satu Mare County, i whakawhiwhia e te Kaunihera Kaunihera o Satu Mare me te Poari Kaute o Satu Mare mo te Tiaki me te Whakatairanga i nga Ahurea Tangata (April 19, 2013).[17] Tohu Paetahi o te Whakahonore o Nanning International Folk Song Art 2012, CAEXPO 2012, Republic of People o China.
Title of Young Artist of Satu Mare County, granted by Satu Mare County Council and Satu Mare County Center for Preservation and Promotion of Traditional Culture (April 19, 2013).[17] Diploma of Honor of Nanning International Folk Song Art Festival 2012, CAEXPO 2012, People's Republic of China.
Ko te taitara LAUREAT o te putanga tuatahi o te Gala o Young Creators, i whakawhiwhia e te Satu Mare Local Council, Northwest Writers Association me Alfazet Cenacle Satu Mare, i te Pipiri 5, 2008.[18] Ko te tohu i whakawhiwhia e te Negreşti-Oaş Local Council me te Negreşti-Oaş Town Hall, hei tiaki i nga tikanga i Oaş Country.[19]
The LAUREAT title of the first edition of the Gala of Young Creators, granted by Satu Mare Local Council, Northwest Writers Association and Alfazet Cenacle Satu Mare, on June 5, 2008.[18] Diploma granted by Negrești-Oaș Local Council and Negrești-Oaș Town Hall, for preserving traditions in Oaș Country.[19]

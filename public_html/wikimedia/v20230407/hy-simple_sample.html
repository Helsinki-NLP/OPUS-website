William Lloyd Garrison (December 13, 1805 – May 24, 1879) was an American abolitionist, meaning he wanted to end slavery in the United States. He wrote a newspaper called The Liberator. He also worked to allow women to vote.

Norbert "Ned" Locke (December 25, 1919 – February 4, 1992) was an American television personality and writer, radio announcer and politician. He was born in Red Wing, Minnesota. He was best known for the role of "Ringmaster Ned" on WGN-TV's Bozo's Circus from 1961 to 1976.[1] He moved to Kimberling City, Missouri where he was mayor until 1992.
Լոքը մահացավ լյարդի քաղցկեղից Քիմբերլինգ քաղաքում 1992 թվականի փետրվարի 4-ին՝ 72 տարեկան հասակում [1]
Locke died of liver cancer in Kimberling City on February 4, 1992 at the age of 72.[2]

Hiag Akmakjian (July 17, 1926 – January 10, 2017) was an American writer, painter and photographer. He wrote several books including Snow Falling from a Bamboo Leaf: The Art of Haiku (1979), 30,000 Mornings (1999) and Cleo (2016).
Աքմաքճյանը ծնվել է Նյու Ջերսի նահանգի Ջերսի Սիթի քաղաքում: Նրա ծնողները փախչել են Հայոց ցեղասպանությունից: Ամուսնացած էր Մարգարեթ Աքմաքճյանի հետ։ Նրանք ունեցել են մեկ որդի՝ Նիկոլասը։
Akmakjian was born in Jersey City, New Jersey. His parents were refugees of the Armenian Genocide. He was married to Margaret Akmakjian.
Աքմաքճյանը մահացավ թոքերի քաղցկեղից Ուելսում՝ 90 տարեկան հասակում [1]
Akmakjian died from lung cancer in Wales, at the age of 90.[1]

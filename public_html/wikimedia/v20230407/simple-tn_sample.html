mola o o kgabaganyang computer monitor
Diagonal is a kind of straight line.
Daegonale ke mola o o tlhamaletseng o o kgabaganyang go tshwaraganya dihuku.
A diagonal line does not go straight up, down, or across.
Mola o o kgabaganyang ga o ye thwi kwa godimo, kwa tlase, kgotsa go kopana.
It is a line that connects two corners of a shape.
Ke mola o o tshwaraganyang dihuku tse pedi tsa popego.
In mathematics, "diagonal" has different meanings.
Mo dipalong mathematics, "mola-kgabaganyo" o na le bokao jo bo farologaneng.
For example, in geometry a diagonal is any line that goes between the corners of a polygon.
Sekao, mo geometry daegonale ke mola mongwe le mongwe o o yang magareng a dihuku tsa polygon.
So a square has two diagonals, and a five-sided shape has five diagonals.In general the count of diagonal can be identified by a formula [n(n-3)]/2 where n is the total number of sides of shapes.
Ka jalo khutlo-nne, square , e na le didaegonale tse pedi, mme popego e e matlhakore-tlhano, five-sided shape, e na le didaegonale tse tlhano. Ka kakaretso palo ya daegonale e ka supiwa ka formula [n(n-3)]/2 moo n e leng palo yotlhe ya matlhakore a popego.

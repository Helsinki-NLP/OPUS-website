Janez Bernik (6 September 1933 – 15 July 2016) was a multiple-time awarded and internationally acclaimed Slovenian painter and academic.
Janez Bernik, slovenski slikar, akademik, 6. september 1933, Gunclje pri Ljubljani,† 15. julij 2016, Prebold[2]
After finishing the Crafts School in Ljubljana, from 1951 until 1955 Bernik attended the Academy of Fine Arts in Ljubljana, where he was a pupil of Maksim Sedej.
Janez Bernik se je po končani šoli za umetno obrt leta 1951 vpisal na Akademijo za likovno umetnost v Ljubljani, kjer je leta 1955 tudi diplomiral, in sicer, iz slikarstva pri profesorju Maksimu Sedeju.
From 1956 to 1958 he continued his studies with the professors of graphic art Božidar Jakac and Riko Debenjak, and then on a number of study travels (Italy, France...).
Od 1955 do 1957 je pri profesorju Sedeju obiskoval še dvoletno slikarsko specialko in pa od 1956 do 1958 dvoletno specialko za grafiko pri profesorjih Božidarju Jakcu in Riku Debenjaku.
From 1958 to 1969, he worked as a freelance artist, and in 1970 started teaching on the Academy of Fine Arts in Ljubljana as a professor of drawing and painting.
Leta 1959 prejme štipendijo francoske vlade za študijsko izpopolnjevanje v grafičnem ateljeju Johnyja Friedlaenderja v Parizu. Znanje si je nabiral tudi na študijskih potovanjih (Italija, Francija …).
From 1979 to 1996, when he retired, he was a full professor and the head of the Painting Department of the Academy.
Od 1958-1969 deluje kot svobodni umetnik, dokler leta 1970 ne začne poučevati na Akademiji za likovno umetnost v Ljubljani kot profesor risanja in slikanja, od 1979 pa je bil tudi redni profesor in predstojnik oddelka za slikarstvo, do leta 1996, ko se je upokojil.
He was member of the Group 69 and participated at all its exhibitions.
Bil je član grupe 69 in je sodeloval na vseh njenih razstavah.
In 1989, he was named an associate member of the Slovenian Academy of Sciences and Arts, and in 1993 became its full member.
Leta 1989 postane izredni član Slovenske akademije znanosti in umetnosti v Ljubljani, leta 1993 pa je izvoljen za rednega člana.
He was an outer corresponding member of Accademia di san Luca in Rome, and from 1996, a regular member of the European Academy of Sciences and Arts.
Od 1992 je tudi zunanji dopisni član Accademiae Nazionale di san Luca v Rimu in od 1996 redni član Academiae Scientiarum et Artium Europaea v Salzburgu.
Janez Bernik was a versatile artist, who reached excellent results in painting, sculpture, graphic design, illustration, tapestry, book design, and poetry.
Janez Bernik je vsestranski umetnik in je dosegel odlične rezultate v slikarstvu, kiparstvu, kiparstvu, grafiki, ilustraciji, tapiseriji, knjižni opremi in poeziji.
His graphic prints are kept in numerous worldwide-known galleries and collections.
Njegovi grafični listi so v mnogih znanih svetovnih galerijah in zbirkah.
He had over 60 indepent exhibitions, including the main galleries of the former Yugoslavia, Klagenfurt, Milan, Rome, Paris, Berlin, New York... Several times, he also participated at the Venice Biennale, as well as a number of group exhibitions at home and abroad.
Imel je več kot 60 samostojnih razstav v Sloveniji in po svetu, med drugim v vseh glavnih galerijah nekdanje Jugoslavije, v Celovcu, Milanu, Rimu, Parizu, Berlinu, New Yorku … Večkrat je razstavljal tudi na Beneškem bienalu, sodeloval pa je tudi na mnogih skupinskih razstavah doma in v tujini.
He published four independent graphic folders.
Izdal je štiri samostojne grafične mape, zadnja Katharsis ad infinitum z 19 sitotiski (MGC, Lj.
His poems were published in two collections; the first one in the monograph Lines ("Črte"), and the second one, Triptych, in the 1985 catalogue Exhibition ("Razstava 1985") and the New Magazine ("Nova revija").
1997). Svoje pesmi je objavil v dveh zbirkah, prvo v monografiji Črte (1977), drugo z naslovom Triptih pa v katalogu Razstava 1985 ter v Novi reviji. Za svoje delo je prejel več domačih in mednarodnih nagrad.
Of the domestic awards, of particular importance are the Prešeren Fund Award for painting and graphic arts (1963), Jakopič Award for painting (1971), and Prešeren Award (1981), whereas from the international awards, he received a number of honorary awards and golden medals for painting and graphic arts.
Od domačih nagrad so pomembne predvsem nagrada Prešernovega sklada za slikarstvo in grafiko (1963), Jakopičeva nagrada za slikarstvo (1971) in Prešernova nagrada (1981), od mednarodnih pa je veliko častnih nagrad in pa zlatih medalj za slikarsvo oz. grafiko.

The Polhov Gradec Hills (Polhograjsko hribovje, also Polhograjski Dolomiti, Polhograjci and Pograjci) are a pre-Alpine hilly region of northwestern Slovenia.
Polhograjsko hribovje, tudi Polhograjski Dolomiti, Polhograjci ali Pograjci, so slovenska geografska regija z osrednjim naseljem Polhov Gradec (397 mnm).
To the north, they border the Škofja Loka Hills, whereas to the south, they border the Ljubljana Basin. Their central settlement is Polhov Gradec (397 m a.s.l.).
Polhograjsko hribovje je alpsko predgorje, ki na severu meji na v povprečju nekoliko višje Škofjeloško hribovje, na jugu pa na Ljubljansko kotlino oz. Ljubljansko barje.
The largest current is the river Gradaščica.
Največji vodotok na območju je reka Gradaščica.

KUD Banda Ferdamana (in translation "Damn Bandits") is a Slovenian improvisational theatre group, located in Maribor, Slovenia.
KUD Banda Ferdamana je mariborsko društvo, ki se ukvarja z gledališko improvizacijo že od leta 1998.
They've been performing since 1998 and are the largest improvisational theatre in Southeastern Slovenia.
So največja gledališko-improvizacijska skupina v Severovzhodni Sloveniji.
They're active in the largest Slovenian improv project, Impro League and they coach high-school teams in School Impro League.
Aktivno sodelujejo kot tekmovalci v projektu impro liga in kot mentorji srednješolskim skupinam v projektu Šolska impro liga.

Nigeria,[lower-alpha 1] officially the Federal Republic of Nigeria, is a country in West Africa.[9] It is situated between the Sahel to the north and the Gulf of Guinea to the south in the Atlantic Ocean.
Nigeria a Federal Republic e Nigeria, pu West Africa. A nanu no Sahel tu north e Gulf of Guinea tu south.

It covers an area of 923,769 square kilometres (356,669 sq mi).
A tomom e 923,769 square kilometres.
With a population of more than 230 million, it is the most populous country in Africa, and the world's sixth-most populous country.
A pakaw tu 230 million a san a populous tu Africa, e sixth-most populous tu world.
Nigeria borders Niger in the north, Chad in the northeast, Cameroon in the east, and Benin in the west.
A nono Niger tu north, Chad tu northeast, Cameroon tu east, e Benin tu west.
Nigeria is a federal republic comprising 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory, where its capital, Abuja, is located.
Nigeria e federal republic a tomom e 36 states e Federal Capital Territory, a nanu Abuja.
The largest city in Nigeria is Lagos, one of the largest metropolitan areas in the world and the largest in Africa.
A tayal a nanu Lagos, a largest city tu Nigeria e a largest metropolitan area tu Africa.
Nigeria has been home to several indigenous pre-colonial states and kingdoms since the second millennium BC, with the Nok civilization in the 15th century BC marking the first internal unification.[10] The modern state originated with British colonialization in the 19th century, taking its present territorial shape with the merging of the Southern Nigeria Protectorate and the Northern Nigeria Protectorate in 1914.
Nigeria a namahongay a tamakerayan a avai vevekelan a pre-colonial tu 2000 BC. A **Nok civilization** tu 15th century BC e mahanatju a masulungan. A pangaakuan e tu British colonial tu 19th century, e masi niyaka a lima na Southern Nigeria e Northern Nigeria tu 1914.
The British set up administrative and legal structures while practicing indirect rule through traditional chiefdoms.[11] Nigeria became a formally independent federation on 1 October 1960.
A namasakanu ya **British** tu pamalung a miadju a makamahekal a chiefdoms.
It experienced a civil war from 1967 to 1970, followed by a succession of military dictatorships and democratically elected civilian governments until achieving a stable government in the 1999 Nigerian presidential election, with the election of Olusegun Obasanjo of the Peoples Democratic Party.
A 1 October 1960 e **independent federation** a Nigeria. A natineng a ku civil war tu 1967-1970, no angar a makatajudju military tamadrino i demokrasi tu 1999 a **Olusegun Obasanjo**.
However, the country frequently experiences electoral fraud, and corruption is rampant in various levels of Nigerian politics.
Avava, a Nigeria i pamuli a makahema a pamalung a electoral fraud e **corruption**.
Lagos (/ˈleɪɡɒs/ LAY-goss;[10][11] also US: /ˈlɑːɡoʊs/ LAH-gohss;[11][12] Yoruba: Èkó), or Lagos City, is a large metropolitan city in southwestern Nigeria. With an upper population estimate of 21 million, it is the largest city in Nigeria, and the most populous urban area on the African continent.[13][14][15][16] [17] Lagos was the national capital of Nigeria until the government's December 1991 decision to move their capital to Abuja in the centre of the country.[18][19][20] Lagos is a major African financial centre and is the economic hub of Lagos State and Nigeria at large. The city has a significant influence on commerce, entertainment, technology, education, politics, tourism, art, and fashion in Africa.
Lagos (Èkó) nanmu drekalaveh a Nigeria i.
Lagos is also among the top ten of the world's fastest-growing cities and urban areas.[28][29] In 2024, Time Out (magazine) ranked Lagos as the 19th Best City to visit in the World.
Sangaw ni mepatseka, tanedju 21 kiya imalu a mekalulu a Africa i. Namudjini a pataw ni Nigeria inyan sangaw ngala 1991, asi nangale a drekalaveh a Abuja.
A megacity, it has the fourth-highest GDP in Africa,[4][30] and houses one of the largest and busiest seaports on the continent.[31][32][33] Due to the large urban population and port traffic volumes, Lagos is classified as a Medium-Port Megacity.[34]
Ningaveh a sudumangan ni Nigeria, nanmu a tarakelaveh ni kalasakut, aravak, atemazale, aziayai, tamazadeh, asadaljiki ni Nigeria na Africa.

It is the largest state in the country by area.[5][6][7] The state capital is Minna.

Yorubaland (Yoruba: Ilẹ̀ Káàárọ̀-Oòjíire) is the homeland and cultural region of the Yoruba people in West Africa. It spans the modern-day countries of Nigeria, Togo and Benin, and covers a total land area of 142,114 km2 (54,871 sq mi).
Yorubaland (Yoruba: Ilẹ̀ Káàárọ̀-Oòjíire) namu a kayuravangan a Yoruba ma ni West Africa.
Of this land area, 106,016 km2 (74.6%) lies within Nigeria, 18.9% in Benin, and the remaining 6.5% is in Togo.
Kuuma sa Nigeria, Togo, tamaya Benin, makua a 142,114 km².
Prior to European colonization, a portion of this area was known as Yoruba country.
Ni ni Togo 6.5%, Benin 18.9%, tamaya Nigeria 74.6%.
The geo-cultural space contains an estimated 55 million people, the majority of this population being ethnic Yoruba.
Tanedju ka maludjemu kadrengadrek a Yoruba naku 55 milyun na.

The Ẹgbado (Morphology: Ẹgba l'odo), now Yewa, are a subgroup of the Yoruba people and mostly inhabit the eastern area of Ogun West Senatorial District, Ogun State, in south-west Nigeria, Africa.
Ẹgbado (ngata so iyo naẸgbado) so sub-tribe na Yoruba, a ngiyan na ka eastern Ogun West, Ogun State, Nigeria.
In 1995, the group's name was changed to Yewa after the Yewa River, the river (odo) they foraged towards.
Sinakauka kadjenganan so Yewa na Yewa River ma 1995.
The name of this river is derived from the Yoruba goddess Yewa.
Kaputsenganan so Yewa na ma nga Yewa ka pulima-Yoruba.
Yewa/Ẹgbado mainly occupy four Local Government Areas in Ogun State, Yewa South, Yewa North, Imeko-Afon, and Ipokia, while the Ado-Odo/Ota LGA forms the fifth Awori part of the senatorial district. Other Yewa/Ẹgbado are located in Lagos West, Lagos East, Oyo North, and Oyo South senatorial zones.
Yewa so takupalka ma ka ma Yewa South, Yewa North, Imeko-Afon, tamaya Ipokia ka Ogun State.
Ipokia is the metropolitan headquarters of Anago kingdom. It is an ancient kingdom in old Oyo empire.
Ipokia so metropolina ka puziya so Anago.
Unlike the other kingdoms, which had at one time or the other engaged in the internecine wars that ravaged Yorubaland in the nineteenth century, Ipokia stood out as an unconquered sanctuary city-state throughout the period. The year it was founded was not known but can be traced to 13th to 14th century when some prince and princess of Oramiyan, the 6th Ooni of ife and founder of Oyo and Benni kingdom migrate from Oyo ile and settled in Ajase ipo in present-day kwara state due to a little misunderstanding among the prince, then move downward toward west,Lagos with the ancient crown gotten from their forefathers, Oduduwa. settled briefly in Lagos shores, Isale Eko before coming to Badagry axis and finally settled down and formed a small town named Ipokia meaning (Opo ko ara e sile, I.e Opo rejected its people) with the help and guidance of Ifa Oracle. Ipokia came to be a Local Government in 1996 carved from formally known as"Egbado south local government"but now "Yewa South local government in the west of Ogun State, Nigeria bordering the Republic of Benin.
So kavaisan na Ipokia simakatiana ma dakal a puruma na Oyo empire ya dina a masau ditutu ka mapuliman a Yorubaland ka 19th century.
Its Capital is in Ipokia town. It is an exit route from Nigeria to outside world through road and water. It is also regarded as main gate to the gateway state due to its proximity and the boundary between Nigeria and Benin Republic.

Ilaro is a town in Ogun State, Nigeria. Ilaro town houses about 57,850 people.
Ilaro a dapit ku Ogun State, Nigeria, a tinrekel nu 57,850 a cudad.
Ilaro[1] is the headquarters of the Yewa South Local government, now known as YEWALAND which replaced the Egbado division of the former Western State, and later became a part of Ogun State of Nigeria.
Tiya a Ilaro so tjuangki nu Yewa South a Panguluan (YEWALAND), ku pangukien nu Egbado tu Western State kanganatu tu Ogun State, Nigeria.
Ilaro town is about 50 km from Abeokuta, the Ogun State capital, and about 100 km from Ikeja, the capital city of Lagos State.
Matja nu Ilaro su 50 km tu Abeokuta, nu masitju nu Ogun, ya su 100 km tu Ikeja, nu masitju nu Lagos.
Osata was an Ancient Ilaro ruler in the 19th century who sacrificed his own son for his people to enjoy an abundance of rainfall at a time when Ilaro was plagued with drought.[2] The dialect spoken in Ilaro is the Egbado dialect.
Osata a Ilaro a matja nu 19-sekelan a masiakal a lalakayan. Na bayak nu ljemay nu tjaljak nu anak na tua marayaw a imudungan nu ljemay, tu kadua nu katadal nu Ilaro ku matumalay a tanubung. Ya masabal nu Egbado a salaitan ku Ilaro.
When Ilaro indigenes meet outside of the home, the shout of “Omo Oluwewun” has the power of unifying the "Ilu Aro" people.
Paka matjaw a tinongnu nu Ilaro ka nu masanpuan sun pa tsatjeng, ya “Omo Oluwewun” a pangajan ku pangarakupay tua "Ilu Aro."
Lagos State (Yoruba: Ìpínlẹ̀ Èkó, Gun: Ayìmátẹ̀n Awọnlìn tọ̀n) is a state in southwestern Nigeria.
Lagos State (Yoruba: Ìpínlẹ̀ Èkó, Gun: Ayìmátẹ̀n Awọnlìn tọ̀n) a nu tsukuy nu nuyaw ku kaval a Nigeria.
Of the 36 Nigerian states, it is the second most populous state but the smallest in area.
Ya so nu 36 a Nigeria, ya a ikadan kaval a makuapan a marayaw, masi nakuaw nu bini.
Bounded to the south by the Bight of Benin and to the west by the international border with Benin for 10 km, Lagos State borders Ogun State to the north for about 283 km, making it the only Nigerian state to border only one other state.
Ya malay kaval nu ya nu Bight of Benin ku ya nu maliya nu, a bayat ya ya, Lagos State a tsukuy ku Ogun State ku ljemay, a ikad a 283 km. Ya a ikad a kaval a Nigeria a malay ku siko so ya.
Named for the city of Lagos—the most populous city in Africa—the state was formed from the Western Region and the former Federal Capital Territory on 27 May 1967.[8][9]
A bayaw nu isad a Lagos a masi marayaw a kaval a Afrika, ya a matsukuy ku Western Region ku faku a Federal Capital Territory nu 27 May 1967.
Kamala Devi Harris (pronounced /ˈkɑːmələ ˈdeɪvi/ ⓘ KAH-mə-lə DAY-vee;[1] born October 20, 1964) is an American politician and attorney who has been the 49th and current vice president of the United States since 2021, serving under President Joe Biden.
Kamala Devi Harris (pronounced /ˈkɑːmələ ˈdeɪvi/ ⓘ KAH-mə-lə DAY-vee; yuwra Kuo 20, 1964) a nu nu a Amerikan ku avuncu a ru 49 a a doku a ru Amerika taji a ru 2021, a mu prezident Joe Biden.
Harris is the Democratic Party's nominee for president in the 2024 election.
Harris a nu nu a Democratic Party a ru doku a ru prezident a nu 2024.
She is the first woman, the first African American, and the first Asian American to be vice president, due to her Afro-Jamaican and Indian American descent.
A mu nu a kabun, a mu nu a African American, ku a mu nu a Asian American a nu vice president, ya so a kabun Afro-Jamaican ku Indian American.
She is the highest-ranking female official in U.S. history.
A mu nu a ikada a mu iayawan a kabun a U.S. a matakuwan.
From 2017 to 2021, she represented California in the United States Senate.
Ya mu a 2017 to 2021, a mu kavar California a ru U.S. Senate.
Before that, she was the attorney general of California.

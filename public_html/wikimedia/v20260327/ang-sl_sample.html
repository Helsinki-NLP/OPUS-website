The Minimum Wage Act determines the right to the minimum wage, its amount (in gross amount), and the method of its determination and publication.A worker is entitled to payment for work performed at least in the amount of the minimum wage if he is employed full-time by an employer in the Republic of Slovenia.
Zakon o minimalni plači (Uradni list RS, št. 13/10 in 92/2015) določa pravico do minimalne plače, njeno višino (v bruto znesku) ter način njenega določanja in objave. Delavec je upravičen do plačila za opravljeno delo najmanj v višini minimalne plače, če je pri delodajalcu v Republiki Sloveniji zaposlen za poln delovni čas.

In the case of working less than full-time hours, the employee is entitled to at least a proportional part of the minimum wage.This does not apply if the employer pays the employee a salary compensation (either due to excused absence from work or for other reasons), which may therefore be lower than the minimum wage.
V primeru krajšega delovnega časa od polnega delavcu pripada najmanj sorazmerni del minimalne plače. Slednje pa ne velja, če delodajalec delavcu izplačuje nadomestilo plače (bodisi zaradi opravičene odsotnosti z dela bodisi iz drugih razlogov), ki je torej lahko nižje od minimalne plače.
The first minimum wage was introduced in Slovenia in 1937.
V Sloveniji je bila uvedena prva minimalna leta 1937[1].
The minimum wage includes the gross salary of the worker, specified in the employment contract; a length of service bonus; a bonus for special working conditions resulting from the distribution of working hours; a bonus for special working conditions resulting from special work loads or hazards at work; the worker's work performance; a bonus for business performance or the so-called collective incentive.
Minimalna plača vključuje bruto plačo delavca, določeno v pogodbi o zaposlitvi; dodatek za delovno dobo; dodatek za posebne pogoje dela, ki izhajajo iz razporeditve delovnega časa; dodatek za posebne pogoje dela, ki izhajajo iz posebnih obremenitev pri delu ali nevarnosti pri delu; delovno uspešnost delavca; dodatek za poslovno uspešnost ali t.i. kolektivno stimulacijo.
The Amendment to Minimum Wage Act exempted three additional allowances for work under special working conditions resulting from the distribution of working hours from the minimum wage: allowance for night work, allowance for work on Sundays, and allowance for work on holidays and statutory holidays.
Zakon o dopolnitvi Zakona o minimalni plači (Uradni list RS, št. 92/2015) iz minimalne plače izvzel še tri dodatke za delo v posebnih pogojih dela, ki izhajajo iz razporeditve delovnega časa, in sicer: dodatek za nočno delo, dodatek za delo v nedeljo ter dodatek za delo na praznike in dela proste dneve po zakonu.
In addition, the minimum wage does not include costs that the employer is obliged to pay to the employee (e.g. reimbursement of costs for meals during work, transportation to and from work, business travel costs), as well as other benefits from the employment relationship (e.g. annual leave allowance, severance pay, jubilee awards).
Poleg tega v minimalno plačo niso vključeni stroški, ki jih je delodajalec dolžan izplačati delavcu(npr. povračilo stroškov za prehrano med delom, za prevoz na delo in z dela, stroškov na službenem potovanju), kot tudi ne drugi prejemki iz delovnega razmerja (npr. regres za letni dopust, odpravnina, jubilejne nagrade).
If, according to the employment contract, the amount of the basic salary is lower than the amount of the minimum wage, the employer must cover the additional payment of the difference to the minimum wage. That applies to the state only if it is the direct employer.
Če je po pogodbi o zaposlitvi znesek osnovne plače nižji od zneska minimalne plače, krije doplačilo razlike do minimalne plače delodajalec, država pa samo v primeru, da je neposredni delodajalec.[1]

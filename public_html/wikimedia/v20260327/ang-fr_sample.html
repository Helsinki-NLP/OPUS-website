The Corvin-Krukovsky family include the Russian sisters Anna Jaclard and Sofia Kovalevskaya, daughters of General Vasily Korvin-Krukovsky, Zoia Corvin-Krukovsky, Russian actor Yuri Corvin-Krukovsky, Hollywood actor Charles Korvin and American entrepreneur and socialite, Jan David Corvin:
La famille Korvine-Kroukovski comprend notamment les sœurs russes Anna Jaclard et Sofia Kovalevskaïa, filles du général Vassili Korvine-Kroukovski :

Anna Vasilyevna Corvin-Krukovsky (married name: Anna Jaclard; Russian: Анна Васильевна Корвин-Круковская) (1843-1887), was a Russian socialist and revolutionary feminist. Her husband Victor Jaclard (1840-1903), was a French socialist. Like his wife, he was a member of the International Worker's Association and a figure in the Paris Commune of 1871.
Anna Vassilievna Korvine-Kroukovskaïa (nom d'épouse : Anna Jaclard ; en russe : Анна Васильевна Корвин-Круковская) (1843-1887), socialiste et féministe révolutionnaire russe. son époux Victor Jaclard (1840-1903), socialiste français.
Sofia Vasilyevna Corvin-Krukovsky (married name: Sofia Vasilyevna Kovalevskaya, also Sonia, Sofa; in Russian: Со́фья Васи́льевна Ковале́вская; in French and German, she was known as Sophie Kowalevski) (1850–1891), was a mathematician active in Russia, Germany and Sweden. Her husband Vladimir Kovalevski (1842–1883), was a Russian paleontologist of Polish origin. Zoia Korvin-Krukovsky (18 January 1903 - 22 November 1999), also known as Zoia Krukovskaya Lagerkrans, was a Russian-Swedish artist known for landscapes and portraits painted against a gold background.
Comme sa femme, il est membre de l'Association internationale des travailleurs et est une figure de la Commune de Paris en 1871.
He is the son of German-Australian entrepreneurs Karl (Charles) Korvin, with possible partial ancestry from the royal Corvin family of Hungary, and Jana Korvinova. His paternal grandparents were Hungarian Jewish immigrants. As of 2024, he is a citizen and/-or permanent resident of Hungary, Germany, Hong Kong (S.A.R), China (P.R.C), the USA and Australia.
Sofia Vassilievna Korvine-Kroukovskaïa (nom d'épouse : Sofia Vassilievna Kovalevskaïa, également Sonia, Sofa ; en russe : Со́фья Васи́льевна Ковале́вская ; en français et en allemand, elle signe Sophie Kowalevski) (1850-1891), une mathématicienne active en Russie, en Allemagne et en Suède. son époux Vladimir Kovalevski (1842-1883), paléontologue russe d'origine polonaise.

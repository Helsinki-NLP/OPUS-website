Billijs Boids (dzimis 1968. gada 28. Augustā) ir Skotu dziedātājs un aktieris.
Billy Boyd (born August 28, 1968 in UK) is an American movie actor of German descent.
Viņa tēlotās lomas iekļauj Peregrinu “Pippinu” Tuku Pētera Džeksona episkajā triloģijā “Gredzenu Pavēlnieks” (2001 – 2003), Barretu Bondenu “Kapteinis un Komandieris: Pasaules Tālajā Pusē” (2003) un Glenu “Čakija Sēkla” (2004).
He is best known for playing Peregrin Took in the Lord of the Rings movie trilogy (2001-2003) and Barrett Bonden in Peter Weir's movie Master and Commander: The Far Side of the World (2003).
Boids piedzima Glāzgovā, Skotijā, Viljamam un Marijai Boidiem, kuri, ar gada starpību, mira, kad Boids bija pusaudzis.
As well as being an actor, he can sing, and play the guitar, bass, and drums.
Viņu un māsu Margaretu uzaudzināja vecmāte. 1984. gadā, 16 gadu vecumā, Boids uzstājās vietējās mūzikas kompānijas Teātra Ģildes, Glāzgova, ražojumā par Hansu Andersenu.
He wrote and sang a song in Peter Jackson's The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King.
Septiņus gadus pirms nodošanās aktiera karjerai, viņš strādāja par grāmatu sējēju. Viena no sietajām grāmatām bija “Gredzenu Pavēlnieks”.
He worked as a book-binder for 6 years before becoming an actor.
Boids absolvēja Skotijas Karaliskās Mūzikas un Teātra Akademiju, kur ieguva diplomu Teātra Mākslās.
One of the books he bound was The Lord of the Rings.

Mary Morrissey Born 1949 (age 73–74) Beaverton, Oregon, United States Notable works Building Your Field of Dreams (book) No Less Than Greatness (book) New Thought: A Practical Spirituality (book)
Mērija Moriseja (Mary Morrissey; dzimusi 1949. gadā) ir amerikāņu jaunās domas rakstniece [1] [2] un starptautiskā miera aktīviste . [3] Viņa ir sarakstījusi grāmatu "Būvējot savus sapņu laukus" (Building Your Field of Dreams), kas stāsta par viņas dzīvesstāstu. [4] [5] Tāpat viņa ir pazīstama kā grāmatas "Ne mazāk kā diženi" (No Less Than Greatness) autore, kurā stāsta par mieru.
Mary Morrissey (born 1949) is an American New Thought writer[1][2] and an activist for international peace.[3] She is the writer of Building Your Field of Dreams, which tells of her life story.[4][5] She is also the writer of No Less Than Greatness, a book about making peace.[6][7] In 2002 she wrote the book New Thought: A Practical Spirituality.[8] American writer Wayne Dyer called her "one of the most thoughtful teachers of our time."[9]

Sapņu lauka veidošana (Building Your Field of Dreams) (1996)
Building Your Field of Dreams (1996)
[7] Grāmata “Esības māksla” autors Deniss Merits Džounss ( Dennis Merritt Jones) raksta, ka “Sapņu lauka veidošana” ir viena no ieteicamajām lasāmvielām tiem lasītājiem, kurus interesē pašattīstība. [8] Rakstniece Tesa Kīna (Tess Keehn) grāmatā "Alķīmiskais mantojums" (Alchemical Inheritance) raksta, ka Sapņu lauka veidošana bija svarīga, palīdzot viņai izveidot "vēlmju kartes". [9] Rakstniece Sīdžeja Beneta (Sage Bennet) grāmatā "Gudrības gājiens" rakstīja, ka Morisejas grāmata var palīdzēt rast jaunas zināšanas par "Jauno domu".
Building Your Field of Dreams tells of Morrissey's difficulties as a teenage mother.[38] Publishers Weekly called the book "sincere"(kind) [38] The book was accepted by the self-development community,[2] with Wayne Dyer writing that the book "sparkles"(shines brightly)[40] and author Gay Hendricks calling the book a "wellspring of spiritual wisdom."(a place to gain knowledge)[41][5][6] The Peninsula Daily News magazine called the book "a metaphysical classic."(a wonderful book)[44] In his book The Art of Being, writer Dennis Merritt Jones writes that Building Your Field of Dreams was among the recommended reading for readers interested in self-development.[45] Writer Tess Keehn, in Alchemical Inheritance writes that Building Your Field of Dreams was important in helping her create "vision boards."(dreams)[46] Writer Sage Bennet in Wisdom Walk wrote that Morrissey's book can help learn about New Thought.[47] [48] Its Spanish version is known in the field of spirituality.[49][50]
[6] [7] Rakstnieks Gerijs Zukavs (Gary Zukav )grāmatu nosauca par "praktisku un iedvesmojošu", [8] savukārt rakstniece Marianna Viljamsone (Marianne Williamson) pauda, ka grāmatai jābūt katra pāra draugam (ceļvedis pāriem). [9] Roberts Lakross (Robert LaCrosse) rakstīja, ka "Ne mazāk kā vareni" (No Less Than Greatness) bijis rekomendēts resurss viņā grāmatai "Mācīties no šķiršanās" (Learning From Divorce). [10] Rakstnieks Deniss Džonss (Dennis Jones) ieteica Morisejas grāmatu savā 2008. gada grāmatā "Māksla būt" (The Art of Being).
Relationships were important in Morrissey's teachings, speaking of male and female characters.[51] Over the years Morrissey wrote articles for newspapers and magazines, writing on relationships.[52][3][4] In her book, No Less Than Greatness: Finding Perfect Love in Imperfect Relationships, Morrissey wrote about "relationship-building."(making friends) [54] The book was taught worldwide.[55][7] Writer Gary Zukav called the book "practical and inspiring,"[57] and writer Marianne Williamson wrote that the book "should be every couple's" friend(a guide for couples).[58] Robert LaCrosse wrote that No Less Than Greatness was a recommended resource in his book Learning From Divorce.[59] Writer Dennis Jones recommended the book in his 2008 book The Art of Being.[11].[60]
Jaunā doma: praktiskais garīgums (2002)
New Thought: A Practical Spirituality (2002)
Moriseja savās mācībās izmantoja teikumus no Bībeles, [1] kā arī Talmuda, [2] Tao Te Čing, [3] Thoreau [4] un citus. Vēlēdamies parādīt "Jaunās domas" (New Though) kustību vienkāršā veidā, Moriseja uzrakstīja grāmatu Jaunā doma: praktiskais garīgums (New Thought: A Practical Spirituality).
Morrissey used sentences from the Bible in her teaching,[1] as well as the Talmud,[53] the Tao Te Ching,[61] Thoreau[62] and others.
Grāmatu izdeva "Pingvīns" (Penguin) 2002. gadā un tā tika veidota no 40 Jaunās domas vadītāju īsām esejām.
Wishing to show the New Thought movement in a simple nature, Morrissey wrote the book New Thought: A Practical Spirituality.
[5] Grāmata tika izmantota akadēmiskajam darbam: grāmatā "Alternatīvās psihoterapijas" Žans Mersers (Jean Mercer) to minēja kā galveno grāmatu, lai izprastu "saistību ar garīgo pasauli". [6] Džonsa un Bārtleta 2009. gada grāmatā "Garīgums, veselība un dziedināšana" autori Jangs un Kūpsens rakstīja, ka Morisejas Jaunā doma bija svarīga, lai izprastu atšķirību starp Jaunās domas un Jaunā laikmeta kustībām, rakstot, ka "Jaunā doma nav jauns laikmets". [7] Daudzās pētniecības grāmatās, tostarp "Oksfordas Universitātes prese: Mūsdienu jogas guru" (Oxford University Press' Gurus of Modern Yoga), rakstīts, ka Morisejas grāmata "Jaunā doma" bija svarīga, lai izprastu Jaunās domas grupu.
Published by Penguin in 2002, the book was made of short essays from 40 New Thought leaders.[63] The book was used for academic work: in the book Alternative Psychotherapies Jean Mercer referred to it as a key book for understanding the "engagement with the spiritual world."[64] In Jones & Bartlett's 2009 book, Spirituality, Health, and Healing, authors Young and Koopsen wrote that Morrissey's New Thought was important for understanding the difference between the New Thought movement and New Age, writing that "New Thought is not New Age".[65] Many research books, including Oxford University Press' Gurus of Modern Yoga, write that Morrissey's New Thought book was an important book in helping to understand the New Thought group.[66][67]
Kopš bērnības aktīvi iesaistījusies starptautiskajās aktivitātēs, kas ļāvis Morisejai 1995. gadā izveidot "Jaunās globālās domas asociāciju" un būt tās pirmajai prezidentei. [1] [2] 1997. gadā viņa strādāja kopā ar Mahatmas Gandija (Mahatma Ghandi) mazdēlu Arunu Gandiju (Arun Ghandi) un uzsāka starptautisko Pret vardarbības sezonas organizāciju (Season for Nonviolence organization). [3] [4] No 2019. gada janvāra šī organizācija ir atpazīstama visā pasaulē.
Active from young age in international activism, Morrissey started the Association for Global New Thought in 1995 and was its first president.[1][10] In 1997 she worked with Mahatma Gandhi's grandson, Arun Gandhi, in starting the international Season for Nonviolence organization.[11][4] As of January 2019, Season for Nonviolence was celebrated around the world.[12]

[22] [23] Simonas un Šustera (Simon & Schuster) grāmatu sērijas "Vistas zupa dvēselei" nodaļas dažkārt tiek atklātas ar viņas citātiem.
Over the years Morrissey wrote articles for newspapers,[68] magazines,[2][3] and books.[69][5] She wrote for Success Magazine.[71] Sentences taken from her books were published in magazines internationally,[7][73] as well as in books.[74][75] Her writings are written in self-help books,[76][77][78] Christian teachings books,[79][80][81] books about rights,[82][83][84] work,[85][86] and happiness.[87][88] Simon & Schuster's Chicken Soup for the Soul series sometimes open chapters with her sentences.[89][90]
Kā skolotāja viņa tiek cienīta par to, ka viņa iedvesmojusi rakstīt vairākas grāmatas, tostarp “Apzinātā sirds”, [1] [2] “Esības māksla”, [3] “Iedvesmotā dzīve”, [4] “Mazie prieki”, [5] “Divpadsmit nosacījumi Brīnumam", [6] "Dziedināšana no depresijas", [7] "Pozitīvā enerģija", [8] "Deviņdesmit sekundes līdz dzīvei, kuru tu mīli" [9] [10] "Uz elli un atpakaļ", [11] kā arī citas. [12] Morisejas rakstīšanas vieglums, saskaņā ar Alana Koena grāmatu Handle With Prayer, padarīja viņu par "vienu no cienījamākajiem dalībniekiem Jaunās domas kustībā". [13] Viņas mācības parādījās grāmatās visā pasaulē.
As a teacher she is respected for inspiring the writing of several books, including The Conscious Heart,[91][92] The Art of Being,[93] The Inspired Life,[94] Small Pleasures,[95] The Twelve Conditions of a Miracle,[96] Healing From Depression,[97] Positive Energy,[98] Ninety Seconds to a Life You Love[9][99] To Hell and Back,[100] and others.[101] Morrissey's ease of writing made her, according to Alan Cohen's book Handle With Prayer, "one of the most respected ministers in the New Thought movement."[102] Her teachings appeared in books around the world.[103][104] She is well-respected in Russia,[105][106] as well as in the Far East, with her teachings taught in Indonesia[107] and China.[108][109]
Radio Moriseja ir izmantojusi, lai "mainītu pasauli". [1] [2] [3] Viņa ir ierakstījusi daudzas audio programmas, tostarp "11 aizmirstie likumi" (The Eleven Forgotten Laws) ar Bobu Proktoru. [4]
In radio, Morrissey used broadcasting in order "to make a difference in the world."[1][111][3] She recorded many audio programs, including The Eleven Forgotten Laws with Bob Proctor.[113]
Viņa parādījās divu stundu "PBS" televīzijas īpašajā raidījumā: "Būvējot sapņus" (Building Dreams), kura pamatā bija viņas grāmata Building Your Field of Dreams. She appeared on a two-hour television PBS special: Building Dreams, which was based on her book Building Your Field of Dreams.[1][115][3] Her programs were on many channels, including NBC television stations.[4][117] In cinema, she was an early supporter of spiritual cinema,[118] and appeared in documentaries.

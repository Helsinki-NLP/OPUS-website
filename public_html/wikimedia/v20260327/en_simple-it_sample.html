Tryparsamide is a medicine made from arsenic that can fight diseases. It was developed in 1915 in the USA by Walter Abraham Jacobs and Michael Heidelberger at the Rockefeller Institute for Medical Research in New York From 1922, the substance was used in Africa as a medicine to treat sleeping sickness caused by Trypanosoma brucei, especially in advanced and long-lasting cases.
La triparsamide è un arsenicale organico appartenente alla categoria degli acidi arsonici pentavalenti (R•C6H4AsO3H2).

From 1934, it was only used together with other drugs like suramin. Because it dissolves well in water, it could be given as a muscle injection or through a vein.
Gli arsenicali organici costituiscono un gruppo importante di farmaci antispirochetici e antiprotozoici.
After World War II, it was replaced by melarsoprol in most countries—except Nigeria, where it was used until the early 1970s—because of growing resistance and serious side effects like blindness and damage to the optic nerve. In the United States and some other countries, tryparsamide was also used for a time to treat neurosyphilis caused by Treponema pallidum
Il principale svantaggio del loro utilizzo in terapia è il basso margine di sicurezza, infatti nel trattamento di un certo numero di patologie, gli antibiotici hanno rimpiazzato gli arsenicali organici, che attualmente non vengono utilizzati né in medicina né per trattamenti farmacologici.

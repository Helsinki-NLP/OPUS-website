Hapón (Haponés: 日本, Nippon or Nihon,[nb 1] yan gi fotmåt 日本国, Nihonkoku)[nb 2] un nasión na isla gi iya Asia.
Japan (Japanese: 日本, Nippon or Nihon,[nb 1] and formally 日本国, Nihonkoku)[nb 2] is an island country in East Asia.

Såsaonao Hapón gi i mafanana'an Aniyon Guåfi (Englés: Ring of Fire), ya kumonsiste nu 14,125 na isla, ya i singko prinsipåt na isla Hokkaido, Honshu (the "mainland"), Shikoku, Kyushu, yan Okinawa.
It is situated in the northwest Pacific Ocean and is bordered on the west by the Sea of Japan, extending from the Sea of Okhotsk in the north toward the East China Sea, Philippine Sea, and Taiwan in the south. Japan is a part of the Ring of Fire, and spans an archipelago of 14,125 islands, with the five main islands being Hokkaido, Honshu (the "mainland"), Shikoku, Kyushu, and Okinawa.
Tokyo i kapitåt Hapón yan i mas dångkulu na siudåt, tinattitiyi ni Yokohama, Osaka, Nagoya, Sapporo, Fukuoka, Kobe yan Kyoto.
Tokyo is the nation's capital and largest city, followed by Yokohama, Osaka, Nagoya, Sapporo, Fukuoka, Kobe, and Kyoto.
Hapón i mina' onse mas mítaotao na nasión gi enteru mundo, yan lokkue' unu gi i mas motmot taotao.
Japan is the eleventh most populous country in the world, as well as one of the most densely populated.
Kasi tres-kuåtto gi i tirenon i tano' sumabåna, ya ayu muna' manggaige i populasión gi kanifes na lugåt gi uriya.
About three-fourths of the country's terrain is mountainous, concentrating its highly urbanized population on narrow coastal plains. Japan is divided into 47 administrative prefectures and eight traditional regions.
Madibidi Hapón entre 47 na distrito, pat "prefecture" gi fino'Englés, yan ochu na lugåt tradisionåt.
The Greater Tokyo Area is the most populous metropolitan area in the world. Japan has the world's highest life expectancy, though it is experiencing a population decline.

A, or a, is the first letter and the first vowel letter of the Latin alphabet,[1][2] used in the modern English alphabet, and others worldwide.
I na'an-ña gi fino' Engles a (ma'a'agang /ˈ eɪ / AY ), plural aes .[nb 1][2]
Its name in English is a (pronounced /ˈeɪ/ AY), plural aes.[nb 1][2]
Parehu i fina'tinas-ña yan i letra alpha gi antigu na Griegu, ni' ginen este na tinige'. [3] I letra ni' takkilo' ha konsiste i dos na bandan triångko ni' manma'a'ñao, ya ma'a'ñao gi i talo' ni' un båndan åpaka'.
It is similar in shape to the Ancient Greek letter alpha, from which it derives.[3] The uppercase version consists of the two slanting sides of a triangle, crossed in the middle by a horizontal bar.
I dikike' na letra ma tuge' gi unu gi dos na manera: i dos-eskalera | a | yan un-eskalera | ɑ | .
The lowercase version is often written in one of two forms: the double-storey |a| and single-storey |ɑ|.
I uttimo na tinige' ma'usa gi i tinige' yan i letra siha ni' ma'usa gi i letra siha, espesiåtmente i letra siha ni' para u ma taitai ni' famagu'on, yan ma sodda' lokkue' gi i italic type .
The latter is commonly used in handwriting and fonts based on it, especially fonts intended to be read by children, and is also found in italic type.
Gi fino' Engles, a i artikulo ni' ti ma'aidentifika, yan i otro na manera an .
In English, a is the indefinite article, with the alternative form an.

"Stealin' Love" is a song by English singer and songwriter Leigh-Anne.
Ma na'huyong gi diha 28 gi i mes Marso 2024 ginen Warner Records komo i fine'nina na kanta ginen i fine'nina na kanta-ña, No Hard Feelings . [ 1 ] [ 2 ] [ 3 ]
It was released on 28 March 2024 through Warner Records as the lead single from her debut extended play, No Hard Feelings.[1][2][3]

A song is a musical composition performed by the human voice.
I bos ha na'guaha i melodia (un series ni' diferentes yan fihu na tono siha) ni' ha usa i håfa na siñat yan i silensiu.
The voice often carries the melody (a series of distinct and fixed pitches) using patterns of sound and silence.
Guaha estruktura- ña i kanta siha, taiguihi i ABA form, ya ma fa'tinas ginen seksion siha ni' ma ripiti pat ma cho'gue yan diferentes despues.
Songs have a structure to them, such as the common ABA form, and are usually made of sections that are repeated or performed with variation later.

I Sadog Ohio 981-mile-long (1,579 km) na hånom gi i Estådos Unidos .
The Ohio River is a 981-mile-long (1,579 km) river in the United States.
Gaige gi i sanlagu na bandan i Midwestern yan i Southern United States, ya ha laknos gi i sanlagu na direksion ginen i sanlagu na Pennsylvania asta i pachot-ña gi i Mississippi River gi i sanlagu na puntan Illinois .
It is located at the boundary of the Midwestern and Southern United States, flowing in a southwesterly direction from western Pennsylvania to its mouth on the Mississippi River at the southern tip of Illinois.
Guiya i mina'tres na mås takhilo' na hånom gi i Estådos Unidos yan i mas takhilo' na hånom gi i sanlagu-sanhaya na hånom Mississippi, ni' ha dibidi i sanhaya yan sanlichan i Estådos Unidos. [ 1 ] Guiya lokkue' i mina'sais na mås amko' na hånom gi i sanlagu na tåno'.
It is the third largest river by discharge volume in the United States and the largest tributary by volume of the north-south flowing Mississippi River, which divides the eastern from western United States.[1] It is also the sixth oldest river on the North American continent.
I hånom ha laknos gi i sanlagu gi i sais na estados, ya i hånom-ña ha na'halom 14 na estados.
The river flows through or along the border of six states, and its drainage basin includes parts of 14 states.
Gi i mas takhilo' na hånom-ña, i Tennessee River, i basin ha na'halom meggai na estados gi sanlagu na båndan i Estådos Unidos. Guiya i fuetsan hånom para singko miyon na taotao. [ 2 ]
Through its largest tributary, the Tennessee River, the basin includes several states of the southeastern U.S. It is the source of drinking water for five million people.[2]
I Norderney (Nördernee gi fino' CHamoru) unu gi i siete na songsong gi i Islas Frisian Oriental gi i sanlagu na båndan Alemania.
Norderney (German pronunciation: [ˌnɔʁdɐˈnaɪ̯] ⓘ; Low German: Nördernee) is one of the seven populated East Frisian Islands off the North Sea coast of Germany.
I isla 14 gi 2.5 kilumetro (8.7 gi 1.6 mi), yan guaha 26.3 kilumetro kuadrao (10.2 sq mi) ya put este na rason i nuebi na mas takhilo' na isla giya Alemania.
The island is 14 by 2.5 kilometres (8.7 by 1.6 mi), having a total area of about 26.3 square kilometres (10.2 sq mi) and is therefore Germany's ninth-largest island.
I numeron taotao gi iya Norderney guaha 5,850 na taotao.
Norderney's population amounts to about 5,850[3] people.
Gi 1946, i Norderney ha risibi i estao-ña gi i munisipåt ya gaige gi i Aurich "Kreis" (county).
In 1946 Norderney gained municipal status and belongs to the Aurich "Kreis" (county).
Gi i sanhaya na bandan i isla guaha 14-kilumetro (8.7 mi) na kanton tasi.
On the northern side of the island lies a 14-kilometre (8.7 mi) long sandy beach.

I Saddok Mariana (Mariana Trench) un ' gaige gi sanlichan na Tasin Pasifiku, kåsi 200 kilometres (124 mi) este na islas Marianas ; este i mas takhilo ' na trinchera gi i tano'.
The Mariana Trench is an oceanic trench located in the western Pacific Ocean, about 200 kilometres (124 mi) east of the Mariana Islands; it is the deepest oceanic trench on Earth.
I åpaka' na åpaka'-ña ya guaha 2,550 km, 69 km, gi i lina'la'-ña.
It is crescent-shaped and measures about 2,550 km (1,580 mi) in length and 69 km (43 mi) in width.
I mas takhilo' na prodokto ni' ma tungo', 10,984 ± 25 metros; gi i sanlagu na puntan un dikike' na tåno' gi i sagan-ña ni' ma tungo' komu i Challenger Deep . [1] I mas takhilo' na punto gi i trinchera mas ki (2 km) mas tåtte gi i tasi ki i puntan Mount Everest . [ un ]
The maximum known depth is 10,984 ± 25 metres (36,037 ± 82 ft; 6,006 ± 14 fathoms; 6.825 ± 0.016 mi) at the southern end of a small slot-shaped valley in its floor known as the Challenger Deep.[1] The deepest point of the trench is more than 2 km (1.2 mi) farther from sea level than the peak of Mount Everest.[a]
Historia i sistematiku na inestudia put i pasado, este i manmaloffan na tiempo.
History is the systematic study of the past, focusing primarily on the human past.
Komo un disiplina akademiku, ti mananálisis yan interpreta i ebidensia para u mafa’maolek istoria siha put i mafa’tinas i kosas yan para u maesplica sa’ tatkumi mafa’tinas.
As an academic discipline, it analyses and interprets evidence to construct narratives about what happened and explain why it happened.
Guaha teorista siha ni’ ma’na’guaha i historia komo un sien­sia sosiát, ennao siha ha’ otro siha ha’li’i’ komo parte i humanidát, pat ha’na’guaha komo un disiplina mesklao.
Some theorists categorize history as a social science, while others see it as part of the humanities or consider it a hybrid discipline.
Guaha parehu na diskusión siha ni’ kumubre i propositu i historia — pot ehemplu, pat i prinsipat na meteña-ña teorétiku, para u ma’otñi i bérdat, pat praktiku, para u ma’aprendi leksion siha ginen i pasado.
Similar debates surround the purpose of history—for example, whether its main aim is theoretical, to uncover the truth, or practical, to learn lessons from the past.
Gi mas jenerál na sinetido, i témino na historia ti ha’li’i’ un kampu akademiku, lao i pasado ha’, i manmaloffan na tiempo, pat i espesífiku na teksu siha ni’ kumubre i pasado.
In a more general sense, the term history refers not to an academic field but to the past itself, times in the past, or to individual texts about the past.
I estúdian historiku ma’usa i primáriu yan sekundáriu na fuenti siha para u mafa’nafåli’i’ i mafa’tinas i pasado yan para u ma’prueba i interpretasion siha.
Historical research relies on primary and secondary sources to reconstruct past events and validate interpretations.
I kritikan fuenti ma’usa para u ma’fåñaina estos na fuenti siha, ni’ ma’a’atan i autentisidát, i contenido, yan i konfiansa-ña.
Source criticism is used to evaluate these sources, assessing their authenticity, content, and reliability.
I manhistoriku siha ha’na’lalåhe para u ma’na’halom i perspektiba siha ginen varios na fuenti siha para u ma’fa’maolek un koherénte na istoria.
Historians strive to integrate the perspectives of several sources to develop a coherent narrative.
I diferente na eskuela siha i pinensan, komu i positibismu, i eskuela Annales, i Marksismu, yan i postmodernismu, guaha distinto na métodu siha gi i metodolohia-ñiha.
Different schools of thought, such as positivism, the Annales school, Marxism, and postmodernism, have distinct methodological approaches.
I historia un anchi na disiplina ni’ kumukubre meggai na ramas siha.
History is a broad discipline encompassing many branches.
Guaha na’guaha ni’ manfokus gi espesífiku na periodu siha gi tiempo, komu i historia antíguo, mentras otro siha mankonsetra gi espesífiku na rehion heograpiku siha, komu i historia i África.
Some focus on specific time periods, such as ancient history, while others concentrate on particular geographic regions, such as the history of Africa.
I kategorizasion temátiku siha inklusi i historia politiku, i historia militát, i historia sosiat, yan i historia ekonómiku.
Thematic categorizations include political history, military history, social history, and economic history.
I ramas siha ni’ ma’assosiá yan espesífiku na métodu yan fuenti i estúdian inklusi i historia kuantitátibu, i historia komparátibu, yan i historia oral.
Branches associated with specific research methods and sources include quantitative history, comparative history, and oral history.
I historia guinaha’ na’guaha komo un kampu inestigasion gi i antigüedát para u masuplanta i istoria siha ni’ manma’na’halom mitu, yan guaha inpluénsia na priméru na tradision siha ni’ guinaha’ ginen i Grésia, i Chaina, yan dispues gi i mundo Islámiku.
History emerged as a field of inquiry in antiquity to replace myth-infused narratives, with influential early traditions originating in Greece, China, and later in the Islamic world.
I istorian esturia guinaha’ chule’ lina’la’ throughout i edat siha yan guinaha’ na’guaha mas profesionál, espesialmente gi i siglu diesnuebe, ania’ guinaha’ na’guaha un rigurósu na metodolohia yan varios na institusion akademiku siha.
Historical writing evolved throughout the ages and became increasingly professional, particularly during the 19th century, when a rigorous methodology and various academic institutions were established.
I historia ta’na’halom yan meggai na otro na kampu siha, komu i historiografia, i pilosofía, i edukasion, yan i politiku.
History is related to many fields, including historiography, philosophy, education, and politics.

The Šumava National Park (Czech: Národní park Šumava, usually shortened as NP Šumava), or Bohemian Forest National Park,[1] is a national park in the South Bohemian regions of the Czech Republic along the border with Germany (where the smaller adjacent Bavarian Forest National Park lies) and Austria.
Süümava Paarkõsy Suumi (Tekkeiži: Národní park Šumava, vjur kaartajhüüisjevoniidus NP Šumava), ui Bohemian Forest National Park,[1] Äävi juus paarkõsy suumi ee Süünsaj regi Böheimaa Tekkeiži Räpublikanus eejo grižonii Tüssikkeimaa (kör leimii Bavarimaaži paarkõsy suumi lüübajhu) ejž Avströniimaa.
They protect a little-inhabited area of the mountain range of the same name, the Šumava or Bohemian Forest.

Forest Guerrillas (Finnish: Metsäsissit, Karelian: Meččäpartizuanut) were an East Karelian resistance movement that was created officially on 14 October 1921.[1] There were around 3,000 Forest Guerrillas in total during the East Karelian Uprising as a Karelian and Finnish resistance movement against Bolshevik Russia, aiming for an East Karelian state with independence from Russia, and in some occasions unification or cooperation with Finland.
Meččäpartizuanut (Suomekse: Metsäsissit, Anglekse: Forest Guerillas) oli päivännouzu Karjalaine vastarindu liikeh, mibo luotih virallizesti 14. ligakuutu vuvennu 1921.[1] Liikkehen kuului noin 3 000 meččä partizuanua päivännouzu Karjalan vallankumovukseh aigah. Karjalaizii da suomelaizii vojuitti Bol’ševiekkuje Ven'aa vastah. Hyö pürgivät suaha Päivännouzu Karjalan itččenäizekse Ven'asta tai liittiä Karjalan osakse Suomeh tai ühteisruadoho Suomenke.
Most of the soldiers of the Forest Guerrillas were from White Karelia, Repola & Porajärvi and Olonets Karelia.[1] The heraldry of the East Karelian Forest Guerrillas was created by Akseli Gallen-Kallela.[2]
Moni soldattu oli lähtözin Vienan Karjalas, Rebol'as da Porarvis da Aunuksen Karjalas.[1] Päivännouzu Karjalan Meččäpartizuanujen gerbun on luadinuh Akseli Gallen-Kallela.[2]
Origins
Roinduperä
East Karelian Forest Guerrilla headquarters at Kiimasjärvi, with Jalmari Takkinen (Ilmarinen) on the right side.
Päivännouzu Karjalan Meččäpartizuanut tukikohdu Kiimasjärvis, Jalmari Takkizen (Ilmarinen) oigiel puolel ker.
Near the end of 1921, company-sized militia groups were being formed in East Karelia to fight against the Bolsheviks, due to the official abandonment of Finnish support for the cause of Karelian Independence following the signing of the Tartu Peace Treaty between the Finns and Russians.[3] These militias would be united into the East Karelian Forest Guerrilla Regiment on 14 October 1921, following a meeting between around 200 representatives from all around Karelia at Koivuniemi [fi].[3][4] The Forest Guerrilla Regiment was sized around 3,000 men strong.[4] In that very meeting held, Ukki Väinämöinen was chosen as the spiritual leader of the Forest Guerrillas, as he was a major proponent and a key figure in Karelian Nationalism, with Ossippa Borissainen [fi] also serving a commanding role in the East Karelian Forest Guerrilla Regiment, with Jalmari Takkinen [fi] (nom de guerre: Ilmarinen) being chosen as the commander-in-chief of the Forest Guerrillas.[5][6]
Miltei vuvennun 1921 lopus, kompuanii suurus militsi joukot alettih perehtütäh Päivännouzu Karjalas vojuittih Bol’ševiekkui vastah. Suomen Karjalan iččenäizyyvele andama viralline tuki loppu Tartun rauhansobimukse jälgeh. Sobimus kirjutettih suomelaiste da Ven'alaiste välil 14. ligakuudu 1920.[1] Militsi joukot ühististü Päivännouzu Karjalan Meččäpartizuanuoihi 14. ligakuudu 1921 sen jälgeh kun noin 200 deputuattua ümbäri Karjaloa kerävüi Koivuniemes.[1][2] Meččäpartizuanuvoloin miärü kazvoi noin 3 000 soldatah.[2] Samas kerähmös Ukki Väinämöinen valittih Meččäpartizuanun hengellizekse johtajakse konzu hiä oli tärgies azemas Karjalan natsionalizmus Ossippa Borissaizen[fi] ker joga palvelii kommandiirun rouliis Jalmari Takkizen (Ilmarinen) ker [fi] joga oli vallittu Meččäpartizuanun ülipiälikökse.[3][4]
Structure
Struktuuru
The political body of the Forest Guerrillas was the Karelian United Government. The body dealing with foreign affairs of East Karelia, mainly asking for help and aid from the Western powers, such as Finland, the United States, Estonia and Poland, was called the Foreign Commission of Karelia.
Mečäpartizuanun politiekkune struktuuru oli Karjalan ühtenäine hallindo, joga vastai ulgopolitiekkusist dieleloist Päivännouzu Karjalas, kui abuun küzümizest Suomelt, Ühtüsvalloilt, Estouniilt da Pol'šult sit vastai Karjalan ulgomuan komissie.
The Forest Guerrillas were divided into three separate units, which were respectively called: the East Karelian Forest Guerrilla Regiment, the Viena Regiment and the Repola Battalion.[1]
Mečäpartizuanut oli javottu kolmeh üksikköh net oldih: Päivännouzu Karjalan Mečäpartizuanu polku, Vienan polku da Repolan battalion.[1]
Forest Guerrillas and Finns at Tsolmo, going towards Kiimasjärvi [fi].
Meččäpartizuanut da suomelaiset Tsolmos, menemäs kohti Kiimasjärvi.
On 11 December 1921, the Repola Battalion launched an offensive attack, capturing Repola and forcing the Bolshevik Russians to retreat.
11. ligakuudu 1921 Repolan battalion piästi hüökkäykse, valtakseh Rebol'an da pakoittah Bol’ševiekut müöstüöh.
With the Forest Guerrillas following closely in pursuit, capturing Lentiera [fi] on 15 December 1921, and the pursuit continued until Lupasalmi [fi], where the Bolsheviks were encircled and beaten with significant losses.
Mečäpartizuanut lähti taga-ajoh hüö valdazivat Lentieran 15. talvikuutu 1921 da taga-ajon jatkues hüö piäsi Lupasalmeh, kusbo Bol’ševiekut kierrettih da tuhottih.
The pursuit tactics which the Forest Guerrillas were using were efficient, until they were forced to battle the Bolsheviks at Klyyssinvaara [fi] and Kuutamalahti [fi], until a tough battle on Christmas night when Porajärvi was captured for the East Karelians.[1]
Mečäpartizuanuvoloin käüttämä taga-ajo taktiekku oli mennüh hÿvi, kuni hüö puututtih voinuh Bol’ševiekui vastah Klüüssinvaaras da Kuutamalahtes kuni Porajärveh voinu joga piättüi Mečäpartizuanuh voittoh.[1]
The Forest Guerrillas had captured around 60,000 square kilometers from the Bolshevik Russians at their highest extent, and there was lots of public support behind them, as Karelian nationalism and Pan-Finnicism were increasingly popular in the rural areas of Karelia.[1]
Mečäpartizuanujil oli hallus noin 60 000 kvadrattukilometrih muata Bol’ševiekul. hyö oli popul’aroi konzu heimoideju kannattamine kazvoi Karjalan küläalovehes.[1]
The Red Army had begun a counter-offensive against the Forest Guerrillas on 5 November, beginning an attack towards the village of Uskela [fi].
Puna-armie alottih vasta hüökkäyksen Mečäpartizuanui vastah 5. külmükuudu Uskelan küläh.
The Forest Guerrillas had set up defensive positions upon hearing the news and opened fire over the Red Army group traveling over the frozen lake at Uskela.
Mečäpartizuanut ottih puolistus azemat da alottih ambuo kohti puna-armieh joga meni Muüezero järveh piäl.
The Forest Guerrillas had defeated the Bolsheviks in their first major encounter.[1]
Voinuh piättyh Mečäpartizuanu voittoh.[1]
Forest Guerrilla retreat & dissolution
Meččäpartizuanuloin müöstüö & murendumine
On 22 January 1922, the Bolsheviks had begun an all-out counter-offensive against the Forest Guerrillas, moving towards Kokkosalmi. The ensuing battle between the Forest Guerrillas and Bolsheviks was fierce, with both sides taking major casualties.
22. pakkaskuutu 1922 Bol’ševiekut alottih suuret hükkäükset Mečäpartizuani vastah ketbo meni kohti Kokkosalmih.
The Forest Guerrillas only had around 800 men at their disposal to defend the village, and they had been blockaded by the Bolsheviks.
Voinuh joukkoloin välil oli kova mollembien suadua menetüstüh.
The Karelian Forest Guerrillas retreated towards Sohjana [fi] through a minor gap in the enemy blockade.
Mečäpartizuanut rubei perävüö kohti Sohjana.
The Viena Regiment reorganized with help from the Oulanga Company at Sohjananvirta, and on 26 January they came into contact with the Bolsheviks, and a battle across a frozen river ensued with the Forest Guerrillas holding off the Bolshevik forces until the next day, when they once again retreated, this time retreating to Pistojärvi [fi].
Vienan polku organizui uudelleh Oulanga kompuanii avul Sohjanvillas, da 26. pakkaskuutu 1922 hüö jouvvuttih voinuh Bol’ševiekkui vastah jiävüttenel jovel. Tulijah päivyn Mečäpartizuanut perävüö jalleh, täl kertah kohti Pistojärveh.
On 5 February 1922, they were forced into a defensive battle with the Bolsheviks, where they ultimately retreated across the Finnish-Russian Border into Kainuu, due to the vast Red Army superiority in manpower and the reason that the Forest Guerrillas were lacking ammunition and food supplies.[1]
5. tuhukuutu 1922 hÿö puuttuo puolistusvoinuh Bol’ševiekkui vastah, minkä jälgeh hüö perävüät Suomen da Ven'an rajan üle Kainuuzeh.[1]

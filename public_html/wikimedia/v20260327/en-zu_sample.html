Crumbly pap is a food that south African prefer to eat.
uPhuthu lungukudla okuvame ukudliwa eNingizimu Afrika.
Crumbly papis made out from drying maize meal, crushed or chopped starchy plants, typically maize meal to make it fine and dry maize meal.it is cooked by adding maize meal on boiling water and continuously stirring. you can eat it together with milk, soup, beans or any other meat or vegetables for your likings.
Uphuthu lwenziwa ngempuphu esuselwa kummbila owomisiwe, bese wagayiwa waphendulwa impuphu. Luphekwa ngamanzi abilisiwe. Ludliwa kakhulu nobhontshisi, amasi, kanti futhi luyadleka nanoma yisiphi isishebo.
Recipe for cooking Crumbly pap (porridge)IZINDLELA ZOKUPHEKA UPHUTHU
IZINDLELA ZOKUPHEKA UPHUTHU
Measure the amount of water for the porridge you want to be cooked. ￼ Boil the water in a pot, once boiled pour in your preferable amount of maize meal inside the pot.
Kala amanzi anele uphuthu ofisa ukulipheka. Linda amanzi abile ebese ufaka impuphu yesikalo sakho. Yekela kubile noma imizuzu emi-5 lapho isitofu sakho siphezulu.
Wait for it to be boiled for 5minutes and your stove must be on maximum. After you open a pot, you stir until maize meal clumps are formed then you lower your stove to medium.
Lapho sekugxabha khona, goqoza kuze kwakheke amageqela ebese wehlisa isitofu usibeke maphakathi.. okusuka lapha usuzoligoqoza kahlanu njalo emva kwemizuzu eyi-10.
After you will continuously stirring the pap after each and every 10minutes. Once is all mixed up, you no longer see the maize meal but a cooked Crumbly pap porridge, then it means that you are finished cooking.

King Edward Masinga (1904-1990) also known as Shobane ka Mangethe and also known as Umfana weKheshi was a teacher who became the first African to present radio programmes in the history of South Africa.
King Edward Masinga (1904- 1990) owayebuye aziwe ngokuthi Shobane ka Mangethe abuye aziwe ngokuthi Umfana weKheshi wabe enguthisha owaphenduka owom-Afrika wokuqala ukwethula izinhlelo emsakazweni emlandweni waseNingizimu Afrika.
Masinga was one of the first broadcasters to broadcast on the Zulu station, which later became known as Radio Zulu.
UMasinga ungomunye wabasakazi bokuqala ukusakaza esiteshini sesiZulu asabe saziwa ngokuthi i-Radio Zulu.
Apart from broadcasting, he was also a composer of songs which he wrote in the Zulu language. [1]He was also the conductor of a musical group (choir) which he founded to sing songs which he composed. [2][3] K.E Masinga is the first Zulu radio presenter.
Ngaphandle kokusakaza wayebuye abe ngumqambi wamaculo ayewabhala ngolimi lwesiZulu.Wayebuye abe ngumbhidisi weqembu lomculo (ikwaya) alabe lisungulwe nguye ukuze licule amaculo ayewaqambile.
History
Isiqalo sempilo nemfundo
Masinga was born in the Mzumbe area in 1904.
UMasinga wazalelwa endaweni yasendaweni yaseMzumbe ngonyaka wezi-1904.
He was born to Christian parents.
Wazalwa ngabazali abangamakholwa, amaKhrestu.
Due to the conflict between Zulu culture and Christianity, his father, who was a bricklayer, forced him to flee his home to become a missionary.
Ngenxa yokugejana kwamsiko esiZulu kanye nenkolo yobu Khrestu ubaba wakhe owabe engumakhi wezindelu kwamphoqa ukuba abaleke endweni ayehlala kuyo ukuze abe yisithunywa senkolo.
He settled in Mzumbe where he preached and taught about Christianity [1].
Wazinza eMzumbe lapho ayeshumayela aphinde afundise ngenkolo yobuKhrestu [1].
His family moved to the Inanda, and Masinga began attending a school founded by Rev. John Dube and his wife Nokutela Dube called Ohlange Institute.
Umndeni usuthuthele endaweni yaseNanda, uMasinga waqala ukufunda esikoleni esasungulwa ngu Mfu John Dube nowakwakhe uNokutela Dube esibizwa ngokuthi Ohlange Institute.
His mother worked part-time to pay for his education.
Umama wakhe wayebaba amatoho ukuze akwazi ukuthi akahokhele imfundo yakhe.
Masinga completed Ohlange Institute and then proceeded to Adams College where he matriculated and obtained a teaching qualification.
Umama wakhe owaneminyaka engama-60 wabemaba amatoho okuwasha izingubo abuye athole amatoho owkulima emasimini, yena wadlula emhlabeni eseneminyaka engama-83. UMasinga waqeda Ohlange Institute wabe sedlulela e-Adams College lapho afunda khona umatikuletsheni kanye neziqu zobuthisha .

Freedom Day is a public holiday in South Africa, observed on 21 March each year.
Usuku lwaMalungelo Esintu (eng:Freedom Day)yiholide lomphakathi elisemthethweni lase Ningizimu Afrika eligujwa mhla zingama-21 uNdasa minyaka yonke.
It is a day to celebrate the progress South Africa has made in promoting and protecting human rights since independence.[1] It also commemorates the Sharpville Massacre, a peaceful march by the apartheid police on 21 March 1960.[2] The holiday was first observed on 21 March 1994.
Ngalolu suku kusuke kubungazwa amagxathu asethathwe izwe lase Ningizimu Afrika ekuthuthekiseni nasekuvikeleni Amalungelo Esintu kusukela lel langabadi lathola inkululeko [1] Ngalolu suku kubuye kukhunjulwe bonke abaqothwa eSharpvile ngamaphoyisa obandlululo bemasha ngoxolo, mhla zingama-21 uNdasa wezi-1960 (Sharpville Massacre)[2].
The holiday is celebrated annually in various locations across South Africa, with the President of the country delivering a national address to the nation on the significance of the day.
Leli holide laba semthethweni ngonyaka we-1994 lagujwa okokuqala mhla ziyi-21 uNdasa we-1994. Minyaka yonke leli holide lugujwa ezindaweni ezahlukahlukene eNingizimu Afrika lapho uMongameli wezwe ethula inkulumo eyibhekise esizweni sonke emayelana nokubaluleka kwalolusuku.

Ouma Katrina Esau (born 1933), popularly known as Ouma Katrina or Queen Katrina, is a South African teacher and language activist, born in the Rosedale area near Upington, Northern Cape.
U-Ouma Katrina Esau (owazalwa ngo-1933), owaziwa kakhulu njenge Ouma Katrina noma Queen Katrina, unguthisha waseNingizimu Afrika futhi usolwazi lwezilimi zomdabu—wazalelwa endaweni yaseRosedale eduze kwe-Upington, eNorthern Cape.
She is considered the last living native speaker of N|uu, an indigenous San language and a branch of Tuu languages.
Uthathwa njengomuntu wokugcina okhuluma kahle ulimi lwesiNǀuu, ulimi lwendabuko lwamaSan olulodwa kweziphambuka kwe-Tuu.
The language is on the verge of extinction.
Lolu limi selusegugini kakhulu yokuphela, kodwa u-Katrina ugcine ukuthi lube semlandweni ongakaze unyathele ngokwezulu.[1][2]

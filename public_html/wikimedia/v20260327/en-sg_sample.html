Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly (Arabic: محمد صلاح حامد محروس غالي, Egyptian Arabic pronunciation: [mæˈħam.mæd sˤɑˈlɑːħ ˈɣæːli];[1] born 15 June 1992), known as Mohamed Salah or Mo Salah, is an Egyptian professional footballer who plays as a right winger or forward for Premier League club Liverpool and captains the Egypt national team.
Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly (Aarabe: محمد صلاح حامد محروس غالي, [1] yeke iri ti lo Mohamed Salah wala Mo Salah, lo yeke mbeni zo ti poto ti kodoro ti Egypte so ayeke sara kua tongana mbeni zo ti maboko ti maboko ti koli wala mbeni zo ti mbele ti Premier League na Liverpool.

Widely regarded as one of the best players of his generation and one of the greatest African players of all time, he is known for his clinical finishing, dribbling and speed.[2][3][4][note 1]
A bâ lo tongana mbeni oko ti akode ti nzoni ahon ti azo ti ngoi ti lo nga mbeni oko ti akota akode ti Afrika ti ngoi kue, a hinga lo na lege ti fini ti lo, driblage nga na vitesse ti lo.[2][3][4][note 1]
Salah started his senior career in 2010 playing for Al Mokawloon, departing in 2012 to join Basel, where he won two Swiss Super League titles.
Salah akomanse na lo na ngu 2010 na Al Mokawloon, na lo zia na ngu 2012 ti gue na Bâle, na ndo so lo wara na lo titre use ti Super Ligue ti Suisse.
In 2014, Salah joined Chelsea for a reported fee of £11 million, but limited gametime led to successive loans to Fiorentina and Roma, who later signed him permanently for €15 million.
Na ngu 2014, Salah ague na Chelsea na mbeni futa ti £11 million, me kete ngoi ti lo ti sara ngia ni ague na lo ti mû nginza na Fiorentina nga na Roma, so na pekoni ala mû lo na mbeni nginza ti €15 million.
In the 2016–17 season, Salah was a key figure in Roma's unsuccessful title bid, reaching double figures in both goals and assists.[1] In 2017, Salah signed for Liverpool for a then-club record transfer of £36.9 million.
Na yâ ti ngu 2016–17, Salah ayeke mbeni kota zo na yâ ti kota ye so azo ti Roma asara ti tene a wara kota kota bungbi ti ala, na lo wara gbâ ti abango ndo ti kota bungbi ti Roma na yâ ti angangu bungbi ti abango zo ti sala kota bungbi ti Rome.
In his first season, he set the record for most Premier League goals scored (32) in a 38-game season and helped Liverpool to the 2018 UEFA Champions League Final.
[1] Na ngu 2017, Salah akomanse ti sala contract na Liverpool teti mbeni transfer ti million 36.9 ti £.
Having formed a formidable attacking trio with Roberto Firmino and Sadio Mané after the departure of Philippe Coutinho, Salah went on to be an integral player in the club's Champions League and Premier League title successes in the following two seasons, and has since also won the FA Cup, League Cup and FA Community Shield.
Na yâ ti kozo ngoi ti lo, lo zia record ti azo mingi so apika gbagba ti Premier League (32) na yâ ti mbeni ngoi ti ngia 38 nga lo mû maboko na Liverpool ti gue na Finale ti Champions League ti 2018. Na peko ti so lo sara mbeni kota bungbi ti azo ti bira na Roberto Firmino nga na Sadio Mané na peko ti so Philippe Coutinho azia lege ti lo, Salah aga mbeni kota zo ti kua na yâ ti Champions League nga na Premier League na yâ ti angu use so aga na pekoni.
Salah has achieved numerous individual accolades, including two PFA Players' Player of the Year awards, three Premier League Golden Boots, the Premier League Player of the Season, the Premier League Playmaker of the Season, finished third for the Best FIFA Men's Player in 2018 and 2021, and finished fifth in the 2019 and 2022 FIFA Ballon d'Or.
Salah awara gbâ ti akungba ti lo, so andu nga akungba use ti PFA Player ti Ngu, akungba ota ti Golden Boots ti Premier League, akungbi ti Premier League ti Ngu ni, akungbu ti Premier League na ngu ni, lo hunzi na ota place ti akungbi na ngu 2018 na ngu 2021, na lo hunzi ti oku place na ngu 2019 na ngu 2022 na FIFA Ballon d'Or.
He received the 2018 FIFA Puskás Award for his winning strike in the first Merseyside derby of the 2017–18 season.
Lo wara matabisi ti FIFA Puskás ti ngu 2017–18 teti ngangu ti lo ti sö benda na yâ ti kozo derby ti Merseyside ti ngu 2017-18.
In 2023, Salah became Liverpool's top Premier League goalscorer, surpassing Robbie Fowler, and became the fifth player to score 200 goals for the club.[2][3]
Na ngu 2023, Salah aga kota zo ti pika gbagba ti Premier League ti Liverpool, na lo hon Robbie Fowler, na lo ga ota zo ti pika na gbagba ti gbagba ti Gbagba ni gbagba ti ga gbagba ti 200.[2][3]
At international level, Salah represented Egypt at youth level before making his senior debut in 2011.
Na yâ ti dunia, Salah ayeke sara kua ti Egypte na yâ ti amaseka kozo ti tene lo sara li ti lo na senior na ngu 2011.
Following his performances at the 2012 Summer Olympics, he was named CAF Most Promising African Talent of the Year.
Na peko ti apendere ye so lo sara na ngoi ti Amérique du Sud, a iri lo na CAF ti tene lo yeke mbeni zo ti nzoni mingi na Afrika.
Since then, he finished as runner-up in the 2017 and 2021 Africa Cup of Nations and was top scorer during CAF qualification as Egypt qualified for the 2018 FIFA World Cup.
Na pekoni, lo hunzi na place ti use na Coupe des Nations de l'Afrique ti ngu 2017 na ngu 2021 na lo ga kota zo ti score na ngoi ti qualification ti CAF tongana Egypte aga na place ti Coupe du Monde ti FIFA ti ngu 2018.
Salah was named CAF African Footballer of the Year (2017 and 2018), BBC African Footballer of the Year (2017 and 2018), and was selected in the 2017 Africa Cup of Nations Team of the Tournament, 2021 Africa Cup of Nations Team of the Tournament and the CAF Team of the Year on several occasions.
A iri Salah Footballer ti Afrika ti ngu ti CAF (2017 na 2018), BBC African Footballer of the Year (2017 na 2018) nga a soro lo na yâ team ti Coupe ti Afrika ti Tournoi ti ngu ti 2017, team ti Coupa ti Afrique ti Tournoi ni ti ngu ti 2021 nga na team ti CAF ti ngu ti ngu ni na ambeni ngoi mingi.
Salah is regarded as a symbol of national pride in Egypt for his achievements[1][2] and was named in Time magazine’s list of the 100 most influential people in 2019.[3] Salah has also been credited for raising the profile of Liverpool among Egyptians.[4][5] To Egyptians, Salah is known as the "Fourth Pyramid".[6][7] Favoured across the Arab world, Salah is often known as the "Pride of the Arabs".[8][9]
A bâ Salah tongana mbeni fä ti kota gonda ti kodoro ti Egypte ndali ti aye so lo sara[1][2] na a zia lo na yâ ti liste ti azo 100 so ayeke na ngangu mingi na yâ ti ngu 2019 ti Time magazine.[3] A yeke nga na Salah ti tene lo sara si Liverpool aga nzoni mingi na popo ti azo ti Egypte.[4][5] [7][6] popo ti azo ti Egypte, a hinga Salah tongana "Chapitre ti osio ti Pyramide". Alexandre Reis, gbë ti 22 août 2004, lo na fama minganga ti "Alee" lo nginza ti Angôlà, lo na rappeur, musisi na entrepreneur.

A cup-bearer was an officer of high rank in royal courts whose duty it was to serve the drinks at the royal table.
Un porteur ëd cope a l'era n'ufissial d'àut rangh ant le cort real, ch'a l'avìa l'ancombensa 'd serve le beivande a la tàula real.
On account of the constant fear of plots and intrigues, a person must be regarded as thoroughly trustworthy to hold the position.

He must guard against poison in the king's cup and was sometimes required to swallow some of the wine before serving it. His confidential relations with the king often gave him a position of great influence.

Freeze drying, also known as lyophilization or cryodesiccation, is a low temperature dehydration process[1] that involves freezing the product and lowering pressure, removing the ice by sublimation.[2] This is in contrast to dehydration by most conventional methods that evaporate water using heat.[3]
This method of dehydration is different from conventional methods that use heat to evaporate water. Freeze drying helps preserve the quality and integrity of the product, as it retains the original structure, flavor, and nutrients. It is commonly used for preserving perishable foods, pharmaceuticals, and other sensitive materials.[1]

Modern freeze drying began as early as 1890 by Richard Altmann who devised a method to freeze dry tissues (either plant or animal), but went virtually unnoticed until the 1930s.[5] In 1909, L. F. Political party in Italy
Ora! (stylized ORA!, English: Now!), previously named Drin Drin (English: Ring Ring) movement,[1] is a centrist[5] political party in Italy.[8][9]
Ora! ( scrivù dcò ORA!, an piemontèis "àura, dsadess" ), s-ciamà an prinsipi Drin Drin ( com ël son ësclint d'un ciochin ) a l'é 'n partì polìtich sentrista an Italia.
History
Stòria
On 7 July 2024, entrepreneur Alberto Forchielli, during a co-hosted YouTube livestream called Drin Drin, discussed an article published in Il Sole 24 Ore reporting that between 2008 and 2022, 525,000 young Italians fled abroad in search of higher wages and better job opportunities.[10] When economist Michele Boldrin remarked on the lack of reaction among youngsters, Forchielli argued that a new party inspired by the ideas of Act to Stop the Decline (the party Boldrin co-founded in 2012) could be successful and offer new hope to young people in Italy.[11] Boldrin was skeptical but challenged their viewers to find 1,000 people willing to volunteer for a new party by sending them an email within 15 days.[12][13][14] The goal was reached overnight and fifteen days later, during a livestream, they announced that 9,100 people had sent an email.[15] As a result, they decided to launch an association, provisionally called Drin Drin, as a political incubator for a new party, since both of them initially refused to become the leaders of the new party.[16][17] On 6 September, the statute was filed,[18] and on 15 September, the official website was launched, allowing people to register to the association.[19][20][15][21]

La neuit apress mila mëssagi a j'ero già ruvà, e quìndes dì apress a l'han contane 'n total ëd 9100.

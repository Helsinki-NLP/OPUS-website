Olga Alkalaj (1907, Belgrade — 1942, Banjica Concentration Camp), was a yugoslav lawyer, activist for women's rights and a member of the Yugoslav Partisans during World War II.
Olga Alkalaj (1907 — 1942), jugoslavenski advokat, aktivistica predratnog ženskog pokreta i učesnica pokreta otpora u Drugom svetskom ratu.
Alkalaj was born on November 23, 1907 in Belgrade.
Rođena je 23. novembra 1907. godine u Beogradu.
When she was still a high school student, she joined the youth communist movement and became a member of the League of Communist Youth of Yugoslavia.
Još kao učenica gimnazije pristupila je omladinskom reolucionarnom pokretu i postala članica Saveza komunističke omladine Jugoslavije (SKOJ).
While a student at the Belgrade Law School, she was active in the student revolutionary movement and in 1923 became a member of the League of Communists of Yugoslavia (LCY).
Kao studentkinja Pravnog fakulteta u Beogradu, bila je aktivna u studentskom revolucionarnom pokretu i 1923. godine je primljena u članstvo Komunističke partije Jugoslavije (KPJ).
She was one of the most active female members of the Communist League of Yugoslavia, in the Women's Movement in Belgrade.
Bila je jedna od najaktivnijih žena, članova KPJ, u Ženskom pokretu u Beogradu.
In 1938, she became a member of the Commision for the Advance of Women of the LCY in Serbia.
Od 1938. godine je bila član Komisije za rad sa ženama pri Pokrajinskom komitetu KPJ za Srbiju.
Also, instructed by the party, she also worked in the editorial office of the feminist newspaper Žena danas (Woman of Today).
Takođe, po nalogu Partije, radila je i u redakciji lista „Žena danas“.
Before World War II, she was the secretary of the party in Belgrade, and also worked as a lawyer, defending fellow communists in court.
Pred početak Drugog svetskog rata, bila je sekretar partijske organizacije Petog rejona u Beogradu. Kao advokat branila je komuniste na sudu.
After the Axis invasion of Yugoslavia and the persecution of Jews in 1941, she lived undercover in Belgrade under a false identity, Sofija Aleksić, working as a maid.
Posle okupacije Kraljevine Jugoslavije i početka progona Jevreja, 1941. godine, nasatvila je da živi u okupiranom Beogradu pod lažnim indentitetom kao Sofija Aleksić, kućna pomoćnica kod Zorice Veljković.
She also continued working for the party and participated in the preparations the uprising.
Nastavila je i sa partijskim radom i učestvovala u pripremama za organizovanje ustanka.
She was behind the organization of the rescue of Aleksandar Ranković, member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of Yugoslavia, from the prison hospital in Vidinskoj street in Belgrade in July 1941.
Bila je učesnica u organizovanju spasavanja Aleksandra Rankovića, člana Centralnog komiteta KPJ, iz zatvorske bolnice u Vidinskoj ulici u Beogradu, jula 1941. godine.
In September 1941, she was appointed as a temporary member of the Local Committee of the Communist Party of Yugoslavia in Belgrade. In November 1941, she was arrested by the Gestapo.
Septembra 1941. godine imenovana je za člana privremenog Mesnog komiteta KPJ u Beogradu. Prilikom jedne partijske provale, novembra 1941. godine, bila je uhapšena od starne Gestapoa.
Taken to the Banjica concentration camp, she was tortured in order to obtain information about other members of the yugoslav resistance.
Najpre se nalazila u logoru na Banjici, gde je bila podvrgnuta strašnim mučenjima u nameri da oda druge članove organizacije.
Since nothing was obtained by the Nazis, she was transferred to the Sajmište concentration camp, but due to the serious injuries sustained during interrogation in Banjica, she had to be transferred to the Jewish hospital.
Pošto ništa nije priznala, prebačena je u sabrini logor za Jevreje na Sajmištu. Zbog teških povreda, koje je zadobila prilikom saslušavanja na Banjici, morala je biti prebačena u Jevrejsku bolnicu.
The partisans in Belgrade organized her rescue, but she declined to escape for fear of the reprisals against the other jewish patients already badly injured at the hospital.
Partijska organizacija u Beogradu, organizovala je njeno bekstvo, ali ona nije htela da pobegne jer se plašila da svojim begom dovede u smrtnu opasnost ostale bolesnike, koji bi mogli stradati kao taoci.
Following instructions of the Gestapo, she was taken from the hospital to a Gaswagen in Jajinci, where she was poisoned to death.
Po nalogu Gestapoa odvedena je iz bolnice 15. marta 1942. godine i u kamionu (zvanom „dušegupka“), zajeno da grupom drugih Jevreja, na putu do Jajinaca ugušena otrovnim gasom.
One street in the Belgrade suburb of Konjarnik is named after her.
Jedna ulica u beogradskom naselju Konjarnik nosi njeno ime.

Prof. MD, DM, MS Svetomir Bojanin (Bolman, 1932), is one of the founders of neuropsychology of the developmental period in Serbia and former Yugoslavia and a renowned child psychiatrist.
Prof. dr. Svetomir Bojanin (Bolman, 1932), jedan od utemeljivača neuropsihologije razvojnog perioda i znameniti dječji psihijatar.
MS Svetomir Bojanin, a child psychiatrist, was born in 1932 in Bolman, Baranja (Croatia).
Dr Svetomir Bojanin, dečiji psihijatar, rođen je 1932. godine u Bolmanu, u Baranji.
The scientific community knows him as the professor who introduced neuropsychology into the Belgrade University Curriculum and taught it at The Faculty of Medicine and the Faculty for Special Education and Rehabilitation (FSER).
Stručna javnost ga poznaje, pored ostalog, i kao profesora koji je uveo predmet neuropsihologija na Univerzitet u Beogradu i predavao ga na Defektološkom i Medicinskom fakultetu.
He is the author and co-author of 2 university textbooks and several specialized monographs in the field of developmental psychiatry.
Autor je i koautor dva univerzitetska udžbenika i više stručnih monografija iz oblasti psihijatrije razvojnog doba.
Wider Serbian public got acquainted with him when he wrote the book “School as a disease” (1991), in which he expressed his opinions about the inappropriate attitude of contemporary schools towards the mental health of children and adolescents who attend them.
Šira javnost ga je upoznala kao autora knjige “Škola kao bolest” (1991.) u kojoj je iskazao svoje stavove o neprikladnom odnosu savremene škole prema mentalnom zdravlju dece i omladine koja ih pohađa.
After analyzing the changes in this area of interest over the past decade, and discerning that the crisis of contemporary school is rapidly intensifying, prof. Bojanin, in his book “The Secret of School”, as an answer to this challenge, presented to a wider public a model of school as it should be, as well as a complete analysis of the condition in contemporary Serbian schooling.
Posle analize promena u ovoj oblasti proteklih deset godina, i uvida da se kriza savremene škole ubrzano produbljuje, profesor Bojanin u knjizi “Tajna škole”, kao odgovor na ovaj izazov, predstavlja široj javnosti jedan model škole kakva bi ona trebala da bude, kao i celovitu analizu stanja u savremenom, pre svega domaćem, školstvu.
He thinks it is nowadays essential to develop extracurricular activities and thus make the institution of school the hub of social life for pupils who attend it.
On smatra da je u sadašnjem trenutku neophodno razvijati vannastavne aktivnosti u školi i time ovu instituciju učiniti središtem društvenog života učenika koji je pohađaju.
Prof. Bojanin has introduced a broad range of novelties into the field of child and adolescent psychiatry: socio-psychiatric approach in developmental period psychiatry, expert fields “Psycho-motor Reeducation” or “Locomotion Therapy”; he has also drawn attention to the disharmonious development and has given the clinical picture of “developmental constructive dyspraxia”, and in the field of work with autistic children he has laid the foundations of modern approach.
Profesor Bojanin je u oblast psihijatrije dece i omladine uneo čitav niz novina: Socijalno- psihijatrijski pristup u psihijatriji razvojnog doba, stručnu oblast "Reedukacija psihomotorike" ili "Tretman pokretom", ukazao je na oblast disharmoničnog razvoja i opisao kliničku sliku "razvojne konstruktivne dispraksije", a u polju rada sa decom sa autizmom je postavio temelje savremenog pristupa.
As of 2012 The Day Hospital for Children in The Mental Health Institute (IMZ) in Belgrade bears Prof. Bojanin’s name.
Dnevna bolnica za decu u Institutu za mentalno zdravlje (IMZ) u Beogradu od 2012. godine nosi ime našeg čuvenog dečjeg psihijatra prof. dr Svetomira Bojanina.
This hospital opened in 1998 and it is intended for diagnostics and treatment of children aged 3 to 12 who experience complex developmental and emotional problems.
Ova bolnica počela je rad 1998. godine i namenjena je dijagnostici i tretmanu dece uzrasta od 3 do 12 godina koja ima problem sa složenim razvojnim i emocionalnim problemima.
After his retirement at IMZ and FSER in 1998 prof. MS Svetomir Bojanin continues to cooperate with his colleagues from other institutions.
Prof. dr Svetomir Bojanin je nakon penzionisanja na Institutu za mentalno zdravlje i na Defektološkom fakultetu (1998) nastavio da sarađuje sa kolegama iz drugih ustanova.
He is the member of one of the oldest NGOs in Serbia - “Family Care” "Pomoć porodici" – where he is the chairman of the professional council and the early childhood development consultant.
Od osnivanja je član jedne od najstarijih nevladinih organizacija "Pomoć porodici" gde je predsednik stručnog saveta i konsultant za rani razvoj.
For years prof. Svetomir Bojanin has been active in humanitarian-charitable work, and to that purpose he offers his expert advice at the Orthodox Pastoral-counseling Centre in Archbishopric of Belgrade and Karlovci of Serbian Orthodox Church.
Profesor Bojanin je godinama aktivan i u polju humanitarnog- milosrdnog rada pa sa tim ciljem svoje stručne savete pruža i u Pravoslavnom pastirsko-savetodavnom centru Srpske Pravoslavne Crkve Arhiepiskopije beogradsko-karlovačke.

Fonetik nopo nga kawo do linguistik it monoriuk kokomoi woyo tulun popolombus do tuni sabaagi komunikasi.
Phonetics is a branch of linguistics that studies how humans produce and perceive sounds or, in the case of sign languages, the equivalent aspects of sign.[1] Linguists who specialize in studying the physical properties of speech are phoneticians.
Pakar linguistik it monoriuk kokomoi gana diti nopo nga ointutunan sabaagi Phoneticians.
The field of phonetics is traditionally divided into three sub-disciplines based on the research questions involved such as how humans plan and execute movements to produce speech (articulatory phonetics), how various movements affect the properties of the resulting sound (acoustic phonetics) or how humans convert sound waves to linguistic information (auditory phonetics). Traditionally, the minimal linguistic unit of phonetics is the phone—a speech sound in a language which differs from the phonological unit of phoneme; the phoneme is an abstract categorization of phones and it is also defined as the smallest unit that discerns meaning between sounds in any given language.[2]

Boros maan (mantad do verbum Latin 'boros') nopo nga boros (sobohogiaan mantad pason) id suang do sintaksis paparamit do gura (mambasa, mimpanau, manangkus, balajal), aantakan (kaantaakan, dumadi) toi ko' aantakan dumadi (jadi, nokoulai, mingkakat).Suang pongintalangan do boros Inggilis, bontuk tuwou, miampai toi ko' poinsuai zarah kumaa, nopo nga infinitif.
A verb (from Latin verbum 'word') is a word (part of speech) that in syntax generally conveys an action (bring, read, walk, run, learn), an occurrence (happen, become), or a state of being (be, exist, stand). In the usual description of English, the basic form, with or without the particle to, is the infinitive.
Id suang ogumu boros, boros maan sinimbanan (sinimbanan id suang bontuk) montok mengekod apatat, sabab, mood om rolou.
In many languages, verbs are inflected (modified in form) to encode tense, aspect, mood, and voice.
Boros maan nogi doti' kaakun miampai tulun, jantina toi ko' ginumu' piipiro banta dau, irad subjek toi ko' objek dau.Haro i boros maan: kaampayat, montok popokito do gura daamot pinapanau maso darapas, montok popokito do isoiso tindakan minaan timpu dumontol, montok popokito tindakan i mangan wonsoion.
A verb may also agree with the person, gender or number of some of its arguments, such as its subject, or object. Verbs have tenses: present, to indicate that an action is being carried out; past, to indicate that an action has been done; future, to indicate that an action will be done.

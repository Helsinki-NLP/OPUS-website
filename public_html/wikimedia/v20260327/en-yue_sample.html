Open data activism

Wong advocates that public bodies not only need to make content available to the general public, but also make them available in machine-readable formats. He criticized the complacency of civil servants who think they have complied with open data regulations but only post scan images of documents. He supports legislating a Public Records Act to codify the requirements for public bodies to disclose information, because the existing Code on Access to Information lacks enforceability.[1][2]
黃浩華認爲推動開放數據有兩個層面，首先係公共機構肯公開啲內容俾人睇，滿足公衆知情權；跟住，公開嘅資訊內容要可以用機器處理，否則左一個 PDF 右一張圖片，就「得個睇字」。佢提倡香港設立《檔案法》，監管公共機構保存檔案同埋公開資訊，因爲既有嘅《公開資料守則》雖然有個機制俾公民索取政府資訊，但冇監管要點畀資訊，亦冇執法權力[1][2]。
Ahead of the 2019 Hong Kong local elections, Wong led the g0vhk project Vote4.hk, which collated public data about candidates and compiled voter guides.[1][2] The pro-democratic camp won a landslide victory in the elections, but Wong criticized that the district councils continued to lag behind the trend in terms of open data, citing the difficulty for the general public to obtain data about council budgets, and even where data were available often only scans of paper documents would be provided, making it difficult for citizens to monitor their council's performance.[3]
2019年香港區議會選舉，佢帶領 g0vhk 社羣建立《Vote 4 Hong Kong》（網址、簡稱：Vote4.hk；直譯「投票爲香港」），整理候選人資料，製作投票指南[13][2]。雖然非建制派喺2019年大選由建制派手上贏走大部分區議會嘅主導權，但係黃浩華表示新一屆區議會喺數據開放方面仍然落後，例如好多工程撥款數據好難搵到，有啲財政文件公開咗出嚟竟然係打印完再掃描返嘅紙本文件，令到市民好難監察議會[14]。
In January 2020, Wong Ho Wa and Vote4.hk colleagues Brian Leung and Nandi Wong saw that public information about the Covid-19 pandemic in Hong Kong was disorganised, so they created the COVID-19 in HK dashboard to collate information about confirmed cases, disease transmission hotspots, and surgical mask market prices. The dashboard attracted 400,000 page views per day during the peak of the pandemic and was maintained by a team of some 20 volunteers assisted by automatic web crawlers.[1] Wong Ho Wa said that the hardest part of maintaining the dashboard was finding committed volunteers to fact-check reports of unscrupulous mask merchants.[2] Wong criticized the Centre for Health Protection's (CHP) practice of withholding data about new Covid-19 cases from their website for many hours after their daily press conference, which forced data reusers to watch CHP's press conferences in order to obtain the latest data.[3] Nevertheless, Wong commended the CHP for providing an API for its coronavirus case data and that the Hong Kong government's open data practices had improved significantly in the late 2010s, even though Hong Kong still fell short of the Open Knowledge Foundation's standards, lagging behind nearby regions like Shanghai and Taiwan.[4][5]
2020年武漢肺炎大流行，香港疫情爆發初期抗疫資訊好混亂。黃浩華同 Vote4.hk 拍檔梁逸風、黃漢斌就用兩日時間揼咗個《武漢肺炎民間資訊》網站出嚟，收集確診人數、高危地點、口罩價格等等抗疫資訊俾公衆睇。民間資訊團隊後來擴展到有大概廿個義工，用網絡爬蟲工具配合人手更新，定期更新資訊[15]。黃浩華認爲搞呢個網站最辛苦嘅係，調查黑心口罩同口罩炒家要搵人核實（fact check），需要好多有心義工投入支持[16]。佢又話政府官方網站上載疫症統計數據慢手慢腳，衛生防護中心開完每日記者會之後幾個鐘先更新網站數據，要追得貼最新數據只能夠靠義工人手更新[17]。不過，黃浩華亦讚賞香港政府之前幾年喺開放數據方面一路進步緊，有啲數據已經有得機讀，只不過仍然輸俾上海、臺灣，相比《開放數據指數》嘅標準仍然有唔少改善空間[18][19]。

The best Pineapple Bun with Butter is made with fresh, hot buns just out of the oven and butter that is chilled with ice. During Republic of China, he was the research assistant to Otto Franke at Hamburg Colonial Institute (now University of Hamburg) and participated in the establishment of Seminar on Chinese language and culture (Seminar für Sprache und Kultur Chinas) and the first Chinese studies center. After returning to China, he served as an Advisor of the Vice President of the Republic of China in Beijing, the Secretary of the Jiangsu governor's Office, a Counselor of the Presidential Palace in Nanjing, and the Financial Commissioner of Jiangxi Province. During People's Republic of China, he served as Jiangsu Provincial Committee for the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference and Jiangsu Provincial Literature and History Research Center.

The Flag of Batak, otherwise known as the Bonang Manalu is the flag of the Batak people of North Sumatra, Indonesia.
Menira Batak, tau dikumbai enggau nama Bonang Manalu nya menira ungkup bansa Batak ari Sumatera Utara, Indonesia.
It is an equal horizontal tricolour flag, which consists of the colour white, red, and black, the Batak Flag itself has many variations and it does not have an official order of colour, namely:[1][2]
Menira tu menira tiga chura melintang, ti nyengkaum chura burak, mirah, enggau chelum. Menira Batak tu ngembuan tebal variasyen lalu nadai ngembuan tusun chura ke rasmi, iya nya:[1][2]
Red, white, and black.
Mirah, burak, enggau chelum.
Black, white, and red.
Chura, burak enggau mirah.
White, red, and black.
Burak, mirah enggau chelum.
Symbolism
Simbolisme
The Flag of Batak as a symbol is an implementation of the values of the Batak mythology that states: "The world consists of three worlds, the Upper World (Banua Ginjang) where the Gods live which is resembled by white, the Middle World (Banua Tonga) where mortals live which is resembled by red, and the Lower World (Banua Toru) where the spirits and ghosts live which is resembled by black.
Simbol menira Batak tu pengereja mitologi Batak ke bemunyi: "Dunya tu ngembuan tiga tikas dunya, Dunya Tikas Atas (Banua Ginjang) ke alai Petara nguan ti diayanka enggau chura burak, Dunya Tengah (Banua Tonga) ke alai orang ke tau parai diayan enggau chura mirah, enggau Dunya Baruh (Banua Toru) ke alai semengat tauka antu diau diayan enggau chura chelum.
The Batak Flag also has philosophical meanings which states, white resembles truth, red resembles bravery, and black resembles leadership.[1][2]
Menira Batak mega ngembuan reti filosofi ke madahka, burak ngayanka pemendar, mirah ngayanka pemerani, lalu chelum ngayanka tuai.[1][2]

The pacu jawi (Minangkabau pronunciation: [ˈpatʃu ˈdʒawi]) is a traditional bull race in Tanah Datar, West Sumatra, Indonesia.
Pacu jawi ( ) nya siti lumba chapi ba Tanah Datar, Sumatera Barat, Indonesia.
In the race, a jockey stands on a wooden plough loosely tied to a pair of bulls and holds them by their tails while the bulls cover about 60–250 metres (200–820 ft) of muddy track in a rice field.
Dalam lumba nya, a jockey stands on a wooden plough loosely tied to a pair of bulls and holds them by their tails while the bulls cover about 60–250 meter (200–820 ft) of muddy track in a rice field.
Hotel Ambacang was a hotel located in Padang, West Sumatra, Indonesia that was destroyed, with significant loss of life, by the major earthquake that struck West Sumatra.
Hotel Ambacang nya siti hotel ke bepalan di Padang, Sumatera Barat, Indonesia ke udah rusak, enggau pengelenyau nyawa ke besai, ketegal tanah berenyang besai ke nuntung Sumatera Barat.
In the Dutch colonial era, the building was used by the Central Trading Company and the Handelsvereeniging Harmsen Verwey & Dunlop N.V. When it became a hotel, it retained the architectural style and atmosphere of the Dutch colonial period.
Maya jeman kolonial Belanda, begunan tu dikena Kompeni Dagang Pusat enggau Handelsvereeniging Harmsen Verwey & Dunlop N.V. Lebuh iya nyadi hotel, iya mengkang ngetanka gaya arkitektur enggau atmosfera jeman kolonial Belanda.
Following its destruction in 2009, the ruins were demolished and a new hotel, now called The Axana Hotel, was built on the old site.[1]
Berindik ari pengerusak iya dalam taun 2009, redak nya direrak lalu sebuah hotel baru, ke diatu dikumbai The Axana Hotel, digaga ba palan lama nya.[1]

Ethnographic museum in Padang, Indonesia
Adityawarman Museum is a State Museum (Museum Negeri) located in Padang, Western Sumatra.
Muzium Adityawarman nya siti muzium negeri (Museum Negeri) bepalan ba Padang, Sumatera Barat.
As a State Museum, Adityawarman Museum is officially known as the State Museum of West Sumatra (Museum Negeri Sumatera Barat).
Muzium tu dikelala rasmi enggau nama Muzium Negeri Sumatera Barat (Museum Negeri Sumatera Barat).
The museum displays ethnographic collections of items related to the culture of the Province of West Sumatra, particularly the culture of Minangkabau and Mentawai.
Muzium tu ngayanka gumpul utai etnografik ke bekaul enggau adat ba Provinsi Sumatera Barat, kelebih agi adat bansa Minangkabau enggau Mentawai.
The idea for a museum dedicated to the culture of West Sumatra was first proposed by Amir Ali, the Head of the Department of Education of Culture of the Province of West Sumatra.
Penemu muzium ungkup adat Sumatera Barat keterubah kali diperambu Amir Ali, Pala Opis Pelajar Adat Sumatera Barat.
The original proposal was to build a "Minangkabau Culture Hall".
Perambu ke asal iya deka nirika siti "Dewan Adat Minangkabau".
The idea was proposed to the Governor of West Sumatra at that time, Harun Al Rasyid Zain.
Penemu tu dipadah ngagai Gabenor Sumatera Barat maya nya, Harun Al Rasyid Zain.
This idea was responded to positively by national authorities, so the construction of the State Museum for the Province of West Sumatra commenced.
Penemu tu ditimbal enggau manah ari piak bekuasa menua, nya alai pengawa ngaga Muzium Negeri ungkup Provinsi Sumatera Barat udah berengkah.
Construction started in 1974 over a plot of land of about 2.6 hectares (6.4 acres).
Pengaga berengkah maya taun 1974 ba plot tanah sepemesai 2.6 hektar (6.4 ekar).
Construction took about 3 years.
Pengaga muzium tu ngambi 3 taun.
The museum was inaugurated on March 16, 1977, by the Ministry of Education and Culture of Indonesia at that time, Syarief Thayeb.
Muzium tu dipenyadika kena 16 Mac 1977, ulih Opis Menteri Pelajar enggau Main Asal Indonesia maya nya, Syarief Thayeb.
On May 28, 1979, a State Museum was established under the name 'State Museum of West Sumatra' (Indonesian Museum Negeri Sumatera Barat) and was given the name Adityawarman.
Kena 28 Mei 1979, sebuah Muzium Negeri ditumbuhka ngena nama ‘Muzium Negeri Sumatera Barat’. (Jaku Indonesia: Museum Negeri Sumatera Barat) lalu diberi nama Adityawarman.
The name Adityawarman was derived from the 14th-century founder and ruler of Malayapura in the Minangkabau Highlands.[1]
Nama Adityawarman datai ari pemungkal enggau pemerintah Malayapura ba Tanah Tinggi Minangkabau ba abad ke-14.[1]
The proposal for the name Adityawarman was filled with controversy, especially among the West Sumatrans.
Perambu meri nama Adityawarman diisi enggau kontroversi, kelebih agi ba raban bansa Sumatra Barat.
Despite the important historic role of Adityawarman in bringing glory to the Minangkabau kingdom and the fact that he was a Minangkabau through the matrilineal order, Adityawarman's affiliation with the Javanese rulers rendered him controversial.
Taja pan sejarah Adityawarman ti beguna dalam mai mulia ngagai perintah Minangkabau sereta iya nya orang Minangkabau nengah atur matrilineal, tang kaul Adityawarman enggau pemerintah Jawa ngasuh iya nyadi kontroversi.
One example of a controversy surrounding the name of Adityawarman was when the Minister of Education Mohammad Yamin wanted to give the name to a university in West Sumatra, but the people rejected the name and changed it to Andalas University in 1956.
Siti chunto kontroversi bekaul enggau nama Adityawarman iya nya lebuh Menteri Pelajar Mohammad Yamin deka meri nama nya ngagai sebuah universiti di Sumatera Barat, tang rayat nulak nama nya lalu nukar nama nya ngagai Universiti Andalas dalam taun 1956.
In 1975, Governor Harun Zain refused to allow the museum to be called Adityawarman Museum.
Dalam taun 1975, Gabenor Harun . Zain enggai ngemendarka muzium nya dikumbai Muzium Adityawarman.
However, his refusal was reversed by Azwar Anas, his successor.[2]
Taja pia, pengawa iya ti enggai nya diputarka Azwar Anas, penganti iya.
Following the regional autonomy scheme in Indonesia, in 2001 the management of the museum was transferred to the West Sumatran Regional Government, directly under the Department of Culture and Tourism of West Sumatera Province.
Berindik ari skim autonomi pelilih menua di Indonesia, dalam taun 2001 pengawa ngatur muzium nya dipindahka ngagai Perintah Wilayah Sumatera Barat, terus ari baruh Jabatan Budaya dan Dagang Temuai Provinsi Sumatera Barat.
The museum was damaged in the Padang earthquake.
Muzium nya rusak ketegal tanah berenyang Padang.
More than 80% of the museum's collection was destroyed.[3]
Lebih 80% ari koleksyen muzium nya udah rusak.[1]
The museum is housed in a traditional Minangkabau building known as the Rumah Gadang.
Muzium tu diengkah ba sebuah begunan asal Minangkabau ti dikelala enggau nama Rumah Gadang.
Two rangkiangs (Minangkabau rice granaries) flank the museum at the front yard.[1]
Dua iti rangkiang (durung Minangkabau) ngapit muzium ba laman mua rumah.[1]

Collection
Koleksyen
The museum houses historic and cultural objects found in the province of West Sumatra, especially those related to the culture and history of Minangkabau and Mentawai.
Muzium tu endur nyimpan kereban sejarah enggau budaya ke ditemu ba pelilih menua Sumatera Barat, kelebih agi ke bekaul enggau budaya sereta sejarah Minangkabau enggau Mentawai.
As of 2006, the museum houses 5,781 items.
Naka taun 2006, muzium tu bisi nyimpan 5,781 iti barang.
The most notable collection in the Adityawarman Museum are relics from the time of the 11th-century Malay-Buddhist kingdom of Dharmasraya, e.g. duplicates of the statue of Bhairawa and Amoghapasa inscription (the original is kept in the National Museum).[1]
Koleksi ke pemadu tebilang ba Muzium Adityawarman iya nya relik ari jeman perintah Melayu-Buddha Dharmasraya abad ke-11, e.g. duplikat patung Bhairawa enggau inskripsyen Amoghapasa (asal iya disimpan ba Muzium Nasional).[1]

Place in Selangor, Malaysia
Sungai Buloh, or Sungei Buloh, is a town, a mukim (commune) and a parliamentary constituency in the northern part of Petaling District, Selangor, Malaysia.
Sungai Buloh, tauka Sungei Buloh, nya siti mengeri, mukim enggau sitak parlimen ba kandang menua utara Pelilih Menua Petaling Jaya, Selangor, Malaysia.
The name itself means bamboo river in the Malay language. It is located 16 km NW of downtown Kuala Lumpur and 8 km north of the Subang Airport, along the Kuala Selangor highway.
Mengeri tu bepalan 16 km barat laut Kuala Lumpur sereta 8 km utara Padang Bilun Subang, sepemanjai jalai alun Kuala Selangor.
Bordering Damansara, Kepong, Kuang and Kuala Selangor, Sungai Buloh is notable for its colonial-era leper colony, one of the largest in the country.
Bekunsi garis entara menua enggau Damansara, Kepong, Kuang enggau Kuala Selangor, Sungai Buloh tebilang enggau koloni orang kusta jeman kolonial, siti ari ke pemadu besai di menua tu.
No clear historical records regarding Sungai Buloh's founding and settlement were available, though railway services had reached Sungai Buloh by the 1890s.
Nadai rikut sejarah ti terang bekaul enggau penumbuh enggau palan pengentap pendiau Sungai Buloh, taja pan servis kereta api udah datai ba Sungai Buloh dalam taun 1890-an.

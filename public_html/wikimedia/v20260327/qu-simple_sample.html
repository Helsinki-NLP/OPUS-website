They usually eat hay or grasses, but they actually try to chew most things, even an empty bottle. However, there are many plants that are poisonous to an Alpaca.

Reproduction

The Alpaca takes about 355 days until the babies are born (11-12 months). They may live up to 20 years (up to 25 years in a zoo). They are as much as 3 or 4 times the opportunity to be mowed fiber in their life.

There they live together with Sheep and pygmy goats. [2] They were selectively bred for household goods like clothes, because they have such good wool. Since the wool made from alpaca is very warm, the wool is popular in the world.
Uywayninmanta
Domestication
Yaqa 5.000 watamanta 6.000 watakamam Puna ayllu runakunaqa wik'uña nisqataqa kunan pacha paqucha nisqaman uywayta qallarirqanku, aycha hinallataq willma ruruchiq uywa hina.
About 5,000 to 6,000 years ago the natives began the domestication of the vicuna into the present day alpaca as a fiber producing animal. Alpaca are also commonly used as herd guardians. Two or more guardians are sent to live in a paddock of sheep, goats or chickens, where it is their job to chase away foxes and other predators.
Paquchakunaqa iskay hatun t'aqakuyniyuqmi, wakaya, suri. Iskayninkumanta ashwan t'aqanakuyqa willma chaninmi, aycha wiranmi, hinallataq willma rikch'ayninmi.
There are two main types of alpacas, the huacaya and the suri (sometimes spelled suriya depending on where you are). The main difference between the two is price and appearance.
Wakaya ashwan mana sasa uywana chaymi allinta miraykun hinantin llaqtakunapi.
Huacayas are more common which makes them cheaper to buy.
Suri ashwan sasa uywana, uñancuna ratu wañurparinku, hinallataq willman sasa k'antinan.
They are also easier to take care of because their coats require less maintenance.
Surikunaqa ashwan chaninniyuqmi, pisilla kasqanrayku, willmanku ashwan munasqa kasqanrayku, ashwan ñañu kasqanrayku ima.
Suris are more expensive due to their rarity and the fact that their wool can be sold for more because it is more luxurious and thicker. Their coats require more daily maintenance.

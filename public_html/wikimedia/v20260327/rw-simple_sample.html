Annick Kayitesi-Jozan (wavutse mu 1979 u Rwanda) ni umwanditsi w’u Rwanda akaba yararokotse jenoside yakorewe abatutsi .
Annick Kayitesi-Jozan (born in 1979 Rwanda) is a Rwandan author and survivor of the Tutsi genocide .

Muri Mata 1994, Annick Kayitesi-Jozan yabuze abanyamuryango bishwe muri Jenoside yakorewe abatutsi.
In April 1994, Annick Kayitesi-Jozan lost part of her family who were massacred.
[1] mu batutsi bari i Butare, kandi bari bahungiye mu kigo cy’ishuri, aho nyina yakoreraga. [2] Interahamwe zishe nyina muriryo shuri.
[1] Her family, Tutsi , were in Butare, and had taken refuge in the infirmary of a school, the mother's workplace.[2] The militiamen assassinated her mother in the schoolyard .
Murumuna we Aimé, ufite imyaka 9, mushiki we Aline, ufite imyaka 16, babyara be bombi, bajyanywe mu gikamyo bajyanwa aho batewe imipanga baricwa.
Her brother Aimé, aged 9, her sister Aline, aged 16, her two cousins, were taken in a truck to a site where they were attacked with machetes and killed.
Annick ntabwo yajyanywe hamwe n'abandi bagize umuryango we, kubera impamvu zidasobanutse neza, ahari kubera ko abaturanyi bashakaga kumugumana nk'umukozi. [3]
Annick was not taken with the rest of her family, for poorly understood reasons, probably because the neighbors who wanted to keep her as a servant .[3]
We na mushiki we Aline, bajyanywe n’umuryango utegamiye kuri Leta Terre des Hommes.
She and her sister Aline , were taken in by the NGO Terre des Hommes.
Yimukiye mu Bufaransa . [1] Yabanje kubana nimiryango yakiriye abanyamahanga ..
She immigrated to France.[4] She first lived with host families..
Yiga siyanse ya politiki, abona DEA, nyuma atangira kwiga psychologiya. yatangiye kwitwa impunzi , mu 2005,aza kubona ubwenegihugu bw'Ubufaransa. [2]
She studied political science, obtained a DEA, then began studying psychology. She obtained refugee status and, in 2005, French nationality .[2]
Yashinze ishyirahamwe Études sans frontières kugira ngo ateze imbere ishuri ry’imfubyi za jenoside. [1] Yitabiriye kwibuka Kunshuro ya 30 jenoside yakorewe Abatutsi. [2]
She founded the association Études sans frontières to promote access to school for orphans of genocides.[5] She participated in 30 year anniversary commemorations of the genocide.[6]
Afite umuhungu n'umukobwa Yabyaranye n'umufaransa. Kuva mu 2015 yabaga muri Uzubekisitani hamwe n'umugabo we, Raphaël Jozan uyobora ikigo gishinzwe iterambere ry'Ubufaransa (AFD). [1]
She has a son and a daughter of a Frenchman. Since 2015 she has lived in Uzbekistan with her husband, Raphaël Jozan, who heads the French Development Agency (AFD) .[1]

Crocodiles (family Crocodylidae) or true crocodiles are large, semiaquatic reptiles that live throughout the tropics in Africa, Asia, the Americas and Australia.
Ol pukpuk ( famili Crocodylidae ) o ol trupela krokodail em ol bikpela, 'semiaquatic reptiles' we i save stap long olgeta hap bilong Afrika, Esia, Amerika na Ostrelia.
The term "crocodile" is sometimes used more loosely to include all extant members of the order Crocodilia, which includes the alligators and caimans (both members of the family Alligatoridae), the gharial and false gharial (both members of the family Gavialidae) as well as other extinct taxa.
Sampela taim ol i save yusim dispela haptok "pukpuk" long makim olgeta memba bilong lain Crocodilia, we i gat ol ' alligator' na 'caiman' (tupela i memba bilong famili Alligatoridae ), ol gharial na giaman gharial (tupela i memba bilong famili Gavialidae ) na tu ol narapela 'extinct taxa'.
Crocodile size, morphology, behaviour and ecology differ among species.

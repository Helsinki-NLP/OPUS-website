Gangelt is a municipality in the district of Heinsberg, in North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany. It is on the border with the Netherlands and about 10 km east of Sittard and 10 km south-west of Heinsberg. Gangelt was the town in which Gerard Kremer, better known as Gerardus Mercator, lived for the first five or six years of his life.

Těrlicko (Czech pronunciation: [ˈcɛrlɪtsko]; Polish: Cierlickoⓘ, German: Tierlitzko) is a municipality in Karviná District in the Moravian-Silesian Region of the Czech Republic.
Cierlicko (po czesku: Těrlicko ; po polsku: Cierlicko, po mymiecku: Tierlitzko ) je gmina w ôkresie Karwińskim we Morawsko-Ślōnskim regiōnie Republiki Czeskij.
It has about 4,800 inhabitants.
Mŏ kole 4800 ôbywatelōw.
Těrlicko is twinned with:[19]
Cierlicko je bliźniynty z:[1]
Těrlicko is made up of villages of Dolní Těrlicko, Horní Těrlicko and Hradiště.
Cierlicko skłŏdŏ sie z tajli Dolne Cierlicko, Gōrne Cierlicko i Grōdziszcze.
The name could be derived from the so-called cierlice, which was a tool used to comb flax.
Nazwa mogła być wywziōnŏ z tzw. cierlice, kerego było narzyndziym używanym do szczyraniŏ lnu.
Another theory derives the name from Cierla or Cierlava, which were older names of the Stonávka River.[2]
Inkszo teoryjo wywodzi miano ôd Cierli abo Cierłwy, co były starsze nazwy rzeki Stōnawki.[1]
Geography
Geografijo
Těrlicko is located next to Havířov, about 13 kilometres (8 mi) southeast of Ostrava.
Cierlicko leży obok Hawiyrzowa, kole 13 kilometrōw na połednie-wschōd ôd Ôstrawy.
It lies in the historical region of Cieszyn Silesia, in the western part of the Moravian-Silesian Foothills.
Leży we historycznym regiōnie Ślōnska Cieszyńskigo, w zachodnij tajli podgōrōw Morawsko-Ślōnskich.
The highest point is Babí hora Hill at 423 m (1,388 ft) above sea level.
Nojwyższy szczyt je kopiec Babiŏ Gōra, we wysokości 423 m nad poziōmym morza.
The municipality is situated on the shores of Těrlicko Reservoir, which was built on the Stonávka River in 1962.
Gmina leży na brzegach zbiōrniku Cierlickigo, kery bōł zbudowany na rzyce Stōnawce w 1962 roku.
It has an area of 227 ha (560 acres).[1]
Ôgrōmno je 227 ha (560 akrōw).[1]
Polish minority makes up 7.4% of the population.[9]
Mniyjszość ślōnskŏ-polskŏ stanowi 7,4% populacyje.[1]
After the reservoir was built, Těrlicko gradually became a centre of tourism.
Po budowie zbiōrnika Cierlickigo stopniowo stało sie cyntrum turystyki.
Public beaches, recreational facilities and private cottages have been set up here.[1]
Ôpubliczne plaże, ôbrōne rekreacyje i prywatne chałpy tu sōm ôstawiōne.[1]
The reservoir is a popular spot for water sports.[12]
Zbiornik Cierlicki je popularnym miyjscym do sportōw wodnych.[1]

In C-Dogs, players play through a number of campaigns, made of a variable number of missions.
W C-Dogs gracze grajōm bez szyrok kampanjōw, skłŏdnych z zmiynnyj liczby misji.
Each mission has a selection of weapons, and different objectives, such as killing enemies, collecting items, destroying objects, or rescuing a hostages.

The campaigns can be played by a single player or with one cooperative player. Other features include color-coded keys to access locked rooms, friendly characters, and neutral civilians that the players are penalized if attacked.

C-Dogs also includes a 2-player, split-screen deathmatch mode called "dogfight": players attempt to kill each other for a fixed number of rounds, and the player winning the most rounds wins.

A playground, playpark, or play area is a place designed to provide an environment for children that facilitates play, typically outdoors.
Szpilplac, szpilpark abo plac ôd szpilu to plac przeznaczōny do zapewniynio bajtlōm strzodowiska, kere pozwala do szpilu, zwykle na zewnōntrz.
While a playground is usually designed for children, some are designed for other age groups, or people with disabilities.
Chociŏż szpilplac je ajnfachowo przeznaczōny do bajtli, niykere sōm przeznaczōne do inkszych grup wiekowych abo perzōnōw ze niypełnosprawnościōm.
A playground might exclude children below (or above) a certain age.
Szpilpark może wykluczać bajtle poniżyj (abo powyżyj) ôkreślōnego wieku.
Modern playgrounds often have recreational equipment such as the seesaw, merry-go-round, swingset, slide, jungle gym, chin-up bars, sandbox, spring rider, trapeze rings, playhouses, and mazes, many of which help children develop physical coordination, strength, and flexibility, as well as providing recreation and enjoyment and supporting social and emotional development.
Moderne szpilplace czynsto majōm wihajstry rekreacyjne, choby kołyska, kaśla, huśtawki, zjeżdżalnia, pakerhala s dżungli, bary s podbrōdkiym, pioskowiczka, spring rider, pierstrzynie trapezōw, chałpy ôd szpilu a labirynty, s kerych mocka pōmoga bajtlōm rozwijać koôrdynacyjŏ fizycznŏ a rekreacyjnŏ, szpas a spōmoganie rozwoju społecznego i emocjōnalnego.
Common in modern playgrounds are play structures that link many different pieces of equipment.
Wszeôbecne na teroźnych szpilplacach sōm kōnstrukcyje szpilu, kere ônaczōm moc roztōmajtych sprzyntōw.

Nineteenth-century playgrounds
XIX-wieczne szpilplace
Through history, children played in their villages and neighbourhoods, especially in the streets and lanes near their homes.[1][2][3] The painting Children's Games (1560) shows children playing in multiple ways in the streets and fields of an imaginary Dutch townscape.
Bez gyszichta, bajtle szpilały we swojich wsiach a ôsiedlach, ôsobliwie na sztrekach a ôsiedlach kole swych chałp [1] [2] [3] Ôbrŏz Bajtlōw Szpil (1560) pokazuje bajtle bawiōnce sie na moc sposobōw na cestach a landwirtszaftach wyimaginowanego niderlandzkiygo landszaftu mjŏsta.
In the 19th century, developmental psychologists such as Friedrich Fröbel proposed playgrounds as a developmental aid, to imbue children with a sense of fair play and good manners.
We XIX stoleciu psychologi rozwojowi, kej Friedrich Fröbel, zapropōnowali szpilplace za pōmoc rozwojowo, coby nasycić bajtle poczuciym radownygo szpasu a dobrymi maniyrami.

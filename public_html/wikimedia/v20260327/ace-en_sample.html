Sobhuza II (1899-1982) nakeuh sidroe ratu keurajeuen Swaziland jinoe.
Sobhuza II, KBE (also known as Nkhotfotjeni,[1] Mona[2][3]) (22 July 1899 – 21 August 1982) was the Paramount Chief and later King of Swaziland for 82 years, the longest verifiable reign of any monarch in recorded history. Sobhuza was born on 22 July 1899 at Zombodze Royal Residence, the son of Inkhosikati Lomawa Ndwandwe and King Ngwane V. When he was only four months old, his father died suddenly while dancing incwala. Sobhuza was chosen King soon after that and his grandmother Labotsibeni and his uncle Prince Malunge led the Swazi nation until his maturity in 1921.[4] Sobhuza led Swaziland through independence until his death in 1982.

South Australia (commonly abbreviated as SA) is a state in the southern central part of Australia. It covers some of the most arid parts of the country.
Nanggroë nyoë na padum-padum boh wilayah nyang paleng that tho di nanggroe nyan.
With a total land area of 984,321 square kilometres (380,048 sq mi),[5] it is the fourth-largest of Australia's states and territories by area, and second smallest state by population.

It has a total of 1.8 million people.[2] Its population is the second most highly centralised in Australia, after Western Australia, with more than 77 percent of South Australians living in the capital Adelaide, or its environs. Other population centres in the state are relatively small; Mount Gambier, the second-largest centre, has a population of 33,233.

South Australia shares borders with all of the other mainland states, as well as the Northern Territory; it is bordered to the west by Western Australia, to the north by the Northern Territory, to the north-east by Queensland, to the east by New South Wales, to the south-east by Victoria, and to the south by the Great Australian Bight.[6] The state comprises less than 8 percent of the Australian population and ranks fifth in population among the six states and two territories.

Colonial government commenced on 28 December 1836, when the members of the council were sworn in near the Old Gum Tree.[8]

As with the rest of the continent, the region has a long history of human occupation by numerous tribes and languages.
Bak thôn 1836, Perusahaan Australia Seulatan geupeudong saboh teumpat tinggai seucara sementara di Kingscote, Pulo Kangaroo, limong buleuen sigohlom geupeudong banda Adelaide. Peuduëk nyan geupeugot nibak saboh prinsip peududukan nyang teulaksana, nyang meukeusut jih keu geupeugöt saboh masyarakat nyang meugah keu ureuëng meugalak droe, deungon geupeugöt keubebasan sipil ngon toleransi agama.
The South Australian Company established a temporary settlement at Kingscote, Kangaroo Island, on 26 July 1836, five months before Adelaide was founded.[9] The guiding principle behind settlement was that of systematic colonisation, a theory espoused by Edward Gibbon Wakefield that was later employed by the New Zealand Company.[10] The goal was to establish the province as a centre of civilisation for free immigrants, promising civil liberties and religious tolerance. Although its history has been marked by periods of economic hardship, South Australia has remained politically innovative and culturally vibrant.

Today, it is known for its fine wine and numerous cultural festivals. The state's economy is dominated by the agricultural, manufacturing and mining industries.

Tasmania (/tæzˈmeɪniə/; Palawa kani: lutruwita[1]) nakeuh saboh propinsi pulo di Australia.[2] Jih geuduek 240 kilometer (150 mil) di seulatan daratan Australia, dipeucre nibak jih le Selat Bass, ngen buleueng pulau nyang na titik paleng selatan nanggroe Australia.
Tasmania (/tæzˈmeɪniə/; Palawa kani: lutruwita[1]) is an island state of Australia.[2] It is located 240 kilometres (150 miles) to the south of the Australian mainland, separated from it by the Bass Strait, with the archipelago containing the southernmost point of the country.
Negara bagian Tasmania nyan mencakup pulo utama Tasmania, pulo nyang keu 26 terbesar di donya, ngen 1000 pulo-pulo di sekitar jih.[1] Jih nakeuh nanggroë Australia nyang hana padum-padum ureuëng duek, Ngon 569.825 ureueng duek bak thôn 2021 Nang nanggroe dan kota rayek jih nakeuh Hobart, ngon sekitar 40 persen populasi udep di wilayah Hobart Raya.[1] Nyan jeut keu nanggroe Australia yang paleng hana pusat.[1]
The state encompasses the main island of Tasmania, the 26th-largest island in the world, and the surrounding 1000 islands.[3] It is Australia's least populous state, with 569,825 residents as of December 2021[update]. The state capital and largest city is Hobart, with around 40 percent of the population living in the Greater Hobart area.[4] This makes it Australia's most decentralised state.[5]
Pulo utama Tasmania dihuni lé ureuëng Duëk Aseuli[1] Dipike bahwa ureuëng duëk aseuli Tasmania dipeucre nibak kelompok Aborigin daratan sekitar 11.700 thon nyang ka lheueh, lheuh tingkat laot nyang ji peuget Selat Bass.[1] Pulo nyan dipeuduek secara permanen le ureung Eropa bak thon 1803 seubagai peuneuduek pidana dari Keurajeuën Britania untuk mencegah klaim bak tanoh le Keurajeuë Prancis phon bak masa Perang Napoleon.[1] Penduduk Aborigin dipeuget antara 3.000 dan 7.000 bak wate pemukiman Inggris, teutapi hampir dipeubinasa lam 30 thon lam periode konflik ngen peuseutu yang dikenal sebagai "Prang Itam" ngen penyebaran peunyakét peularan.
Tasmania's main island was inhabited by Aboriginal peoples.[1] It is thought that Aboriginal Tasmanians became separated from the mainland Aboriginal groups about 11,700 years ago, after rising sea levels formed Bass Strait.[2] The island was permanently settled by Europeans in 1803 as a penal settlement of the British Empire to prevent claims to the land by the First French Empire during the Napoleonic Wars.[3] The Aboriginal population is estimated to have been between 3,000 and 7,000 at the time of British settlement, but was almost wiped out within 30 years during a period of conflicts with settlers known as the "Black War" and the spread of infectious diseases.
Konflik, nyang peukeuh antara 1825 dan 1831 dan meuakibat keu leubeh dari lhee thon undang-undang militer, meupeumioh nyawa hampir 1.100 ureung duëk aselui an pemukim.
The conflict, which peaked between 1825 and 1831 and led to more than three years of martial law, cost the lives of almost 1,100 Aboriginal people and settlers.
Dipeurintah Inggris pulo nyoe awai jih jeut keu bagian dari Koloni Wales Seulatan Baroe tapi jeut keu koloni yang terpisah di bawah nama Van Diemen's Land (ngon nama Anthony van Diemen) bak thon 1825.[1] Kira-kira 80.000 taboh dihukum dijak u Tanoh Van Diemen seugolom praktek nyoe, yang dikenal sebagai transportasi, berhenti bak thon 1853.[1] Bak thon 1855 konstitusi Tasmania jinoe geupeugot, dan thon ukeue koloni nyan seucara resmi meu ubah nan jih jeut Tasmania.
Under British rule the island was initially part of the Colony of New South Wales but became a separate colony under the name Van Diemen's Land (named after Anthony van Diemen) in 1825.[1] Approximately 80,000 convicts were sent to Van Diemen's Land before this practice, known as transportation, ceased in 1853.[2] In 1855 the present Constitution of Tasmania was enacted, and the following year the colony formally changed its name to Tasmania.
Bak thon 1901 jeut keu saboh nanggroe Australia meulalui proses federasi Australia.
In 1901 it became a state of Australia through the process of the federation of Australia.
Jinoe, Tasmania na ekonomi yeng paleng ubit dari negara bagian dan wilayah Australia, yeng dipeuget le pariwisata, pertanian ngen akuakultur, pendidikan ngen peurawatan kesehatan.[1] Tasmania nakeuh saboh eksportir pertanian nyang rayek, meunan cit tujuan rayek keu ekoturisme.
Today, Tasmania has the smallest economy of the Australian states and territories, which is generated by of tourism, agriculture and aquaculture, education and healthcare.[1] Tasmania is a significant agricultural exporter, as well as a significant destination for eco-tourism.
Kira-kira 42 persen wilayah jih, termasuk taman nasional ngen Tanoh Warisan donya (21%) dipeulindong dalam bentuk cadangan.[1] Partai politik lingkungan phon di donya geudirian di Tasmania.[1]
About 42 percent of its land area, including national parks and World Heritage Sites (21%) is protected in some form of reserve.[2] The first environmental political party in the world was founded in Tasmania.[3]

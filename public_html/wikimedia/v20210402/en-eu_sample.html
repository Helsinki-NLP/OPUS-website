Aurelio Gonzato (Chiasso, December 30th, 1914 – Massagno, May 12th, 2014) was a Swiss mosaicist and painter.
Aurelio Gonzato (Chiasso,1914ko abenduaren 30a, – Massagno, 2014ko maiatzaren 12a) mosaikogile eta pintore suitzar bat izan zen.
Biography
Biografia
Aurelio Gonzato, son of Carlo Gonzato and Maria Gonzato, was the eldest among ten brothers and sisters.
Aurelio Gonzato, Carlo Gonzato aitaren eta Maria Gonzato amaren semea, hamar seme-alabaren arteko zaharrena zen.
One of his brothers was the painter Guido Gonzato.
Bere anai-arrebetako bat Guido Gonzato pintorea zen.
Aurelio collaborated many times with his brother, who was a fundamental figure in his artistic development.
Aurelio Gonzato-k bere anaiarekin hainbatetan kolaboratu zuen eta, era berean, Guido anaia Aurelio-ren beraren garapen artistikoan oinarrizko figura bat izan zen.
Aurelio was very young when he started to focus on graphical and craft works in a self-taught way.
Aurelio oso gaztetan hasi zen lan grafikoak eta artisautza-lanak gauzatzen, modu autodidakta batean.
When he was 18-years-old he founded the artistic movement Arte decorativa razionale (Rational decorative art). This movement was the first among many other movements and associations he would later found.
18 urte zituela, Arte decorativa razionale (Arte dekoratibo razionala) mugimendua fundatu zuen, beranduago fundatuko zituen hainbat mugimendu eta asoziazioren arteko lehena.
At that time, he added a research on form and space to his figurative pictorial practice.
Aldi hartan, bere praktika piktoriko figuratiboari formaren eta espazioaren inguruko ikerketa bat gehitu zion.

Early life
Bizitza
His grandfather was imprisoned after the coup of 1936.[1] He is the son of Jose Mari Hualde and the txistulari Margari Iglesias. He was born in Altsasu, and he has two younger sisters. He holds a degree in Law from the University of Deusto, as well as a Master's Degree in Staff Management.
Aitona kartzelaratua izan zen 1936ko estatu-kolpearen ondoren.[1] Jose Mari Hualderen (Altsasuko udaleko eraikuntza lanen brigadako buru izandakoa) eta Margari Iglesias txistulariaren semea da, eta bi arreba gazteago ditu.[2] Zuzenbidean lizentziatu zen Deustuko Unibertsitatean eta gerora Langileen Zuzendaritza Masterra gauzatu zuen.
Before entering politics he worked in the private sector as law counselor in the area of Public and Administrative Law.
Politikan sartu aurretik enpresa pribatuan aritu zen lanean, aholkulari juridiko gisa zuzenbide publikoaren esparruan.
Since 2007 he is on unpaid leave.[…] He became a member of the Basque National Party in 1999.
2007tik eszedentzian dago. 1999an egin zen EAJko kide, 23 urte zituela.[3]
Political career
Ibilbide politikoa
Altsasu town council
Udalean
He was elected as a town councilor for Altsasu in 2003, as a member of the Altsasu Group (Agrupación Altsasuarra / Altsasuko Taldea), an independent group linked to the Basque National Party.
2003ko udal hauteskundeen ondoren Altsasuko udaleko zinegotzi jardun zuen 2007 bitartean, "Agrupación Altsasuarra / Altsasuko Taldea" aliantzaren baitan. 2003-2007 legealdian Aralar, EA eta IUrekin batera udal-gobernua osatu zuten.
The group became second, winning three councilors, as many as the Socialist Party of Navarre, who became first.[…] They formed and alliance with Aralar, and Hualde served as deputy mayor from 2003 to 2007.[2]
Hauteskundeak PSNk irabazi zituen eta "Agrupación Altsasuarra / Altsasuko Taldea" bigarren izan zen, hiruna zinegotzirekin, baina Aralarren alkategaia babestu zuten, Asunción Fernández de Garaialde, hirugarren geratu zena 2 zinegotzirekin.[4][5] Legealdi honetan Unai Hualde alkateordea izan zen.
In 2007 he was the leading candidate of the coalition Nafarroa Bai.
Hurrengo legealdian Unai Hualde Nafarroa Bai koalizioko zerrendaburu gisa aurkeztu zen hauteskundeetara.
The alliance won the election with four councilors, and Hualde was the mayor of Altsasu from 2007 to 2011.
Aliantza hau lehen indarra izan zen, 4 zinegotzi eskuratuz, eta beraz Unai Hualdek alkatetza eskuratu zuen. Herriko alkatea izan zen 2007-2011 legealdian.
He seeked re-election in 2011, but Nafarroa Bai was beaten by Bildu.
2011ko udal hauteskundeetan berriz ere alkatetzarako aurkeztu zen, kasu honetan Nafarroa Bai 2011 zerrendan.
Although both parties obtained four councilors, Bildu received more votes.
4 zinegotziak mantendu zituzten, baina Bilduk boto gehiago eskuratu zituen, beste lau zinegotzirekin lehen indarra izatera iritsiz.
Garazi Urrestarazu succeeded Hualde as the mayor, and from 2011 to 2015 he stayed in the opposition as councilor.
Era horretan Garazi Urrestarazuk eskuratu zuen alkatetza. 2011-2015 legealdian zehar Unai Hualdek udaleko ziengotzi gisa jardun zuen.
In 2015 Geroa Bai, de facto successor of Nafarroa Bai, was again the most voted party and Javier Ollo became the mayor.
2015eko udal hauteskundeetan Geroa Bai izan zen lehen indarra, eta Javier Ollo izendatu zuten alkate.
Hualde continued as town councilor until 2016, when he resigned in order to focus on his work as Member of the Parliament of Navarre. In 2019 Geroa Bai won an overall majority in the town council. Hualde went on a simbolic place from the list.[…]
Unai Hualdek zinegotzi lanean jarraitu zuen 2016ko otsailera arte, parlamentari lanean zentratzeko karguari uko egin zionean.[6] 2019ko udal hauteskundeetan Geroa Bairen zerrendan aurkeztu zen, era sinbolikoan, azkenaurreko postuan.[7]

Askatasuna (''Freedom'' in Basque) was an anarcho-independentist newspaper, circulating in the Basque Country during the 1970s.
Askatasuna 1970eko hamarkadako Euskal Herriko aldizkari anarkista abertzalea izan zen.
The magazine was made by a collective of the same name, composed of exiles in Brussels who had escaped from the Franco regime. They began publishing in Bilbao in 1976.
1971an Bruselan frankismotik ihes egindako erbesteratu batzuek sortu zuten eta 1976tik aurrera Bilbon argitaratzen hasi zen.
These were initially members of the CNT but their intention to form a specific organization in the Basque Country was frustrated when anarchist unions dismissed them.[1]
Aldizkaria izen bereko kolektiboak egindakoa zen. Hauek hasiera batean CNTko kideak ziren baina Euskal Herriko erakunde espezifiko bat eratzeko beren asmoa zapuztua izan zenean sindikatu anarkistak kaleratu egin zituen.[1]
Freedom advocated a global libertarian struggle, adding to their discourse the conceptions of struggle that were not confined to revolutionary nationalism or to union policy.
Askatasuna kolektiboak borroka libertario globala defendatzen zuen, euren diskurtsora nazionalismo iraultzaileko zein politika sindikalera mugatzen ez ziren borroketako kontzepzioak gehituz.
In December 1977 the group announced an "assembly convergence" featuring other groups in the Basque libertarian-left, but the project was canceled.
1977ko abenduan kolektiboak LAIA (ez), LAK eta OCA-EKA erakundeekin "konbergentzia asanblearioa" iragarri zuen, baina proiektua bertan behera geratu zen.
The collective had a workshop in Bilbao, in which they published the magazine and other materials.
Kolektiboak Bilbon tailer bat zeukan, zeinean aldizkaria eta beste zenbait material argitaratzen zuten.
This workshop suffered an arson attack by the right-wing group Guerrilleros de Cristo Rey on August 24, 1978.
Tailer hau Guerrilleros de Cristo Rey eskuin muturreko taldearen atentatu bat jasan zuen 1978 abuztuaren 24an, Bilboko lehen Aste Nagusiko egunetan hain zuzen.
The resulting fire destroyed the workshop and its archives.
Eragindako suteak tailerra, artxiboak eta aldizkariaren erredakzioa suntsitu zuen.
Losses were estimated at six million pesetas at the time.
Galera ekonomikoak garaiko sei milioi pesetatan ebaluatu ziren.
In September of the same year, Freedom released a special issue in response to the attack, printing 25,000 copies.
Urte bereko irailean Askatasuna atentatuari erantzun nahian ale berezia kaleratu zuen, 25.000 ale inprimatu zituztelarik.
After the issuing of a court order, Freedom moved away from anarchist approaches, instead showing "critical support" to the Herri Batasuna.
Bere bigarren garaian, urtebeteko iraupena izan zuena eta epaitegi aginduz bahiketa batez hasi zena, Askatasuna hasierako planteamendu anarkistengandik urrunduz joan zen, Herri Batasunari "sostengu kritikoa" erakutsi ziolarik.
Over time it became a left-wing magazine with no specific affiliation and released its last issue on August 1980.
Denborarekin adskripzio zehatzik gabeko aldizkari ezkertiar bihurtu zen, bere azken alea 1980ko abuztuan kaleratu zuelarik.

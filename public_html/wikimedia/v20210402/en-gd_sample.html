Christine Primrose (17 February 1950 — ) is a Gaelic singer and music teacher.
Tha Christine Primrose (17 an Gearran 1950 — fhathast beò) na seinneadair Gàidhlig agus na teagasgaiche òrain.

She was born in Carloway, Lewis, but she currently lives on the Isle of Skye.
Rugadh i ann an Càrlabhagh, Eilean Leòdhais, ach tha i a' fuireach anns an Eilean Sgitheanach a-nis.
In interviews Primrose has stated that she has been singing since she was a small child, which is very typical in her family.
Tha i air a bhith a' seinn bhon a bha i na nighean bheag, is mar a thuirt i fhèin ann an agallamh, 's e dìreach rudeigin nàdarrach a bh’ ann san teaghlach aice.
She won a gold medal in sean-nós at the Royal National Mòd in 1974 and an award at the 1978 Pan-Celtic Festival, [1] and, as was not common at the time, she took a degree in traditional Gaelic music, and she has been performing all around the world, especially in North America, Australia, New Zealand and in Europe.
Bhuannaich i Bonn Òir an t-Seann Nòis aig a’ Mhòd Nàiseanta Rìoghail ann an 1974 agus duais aig an Fèis Pan Celtic ann an 1978, [1] agus, ged nach robh e cumanta aig an àm sin, rinn i dreuchd den cheòl Ghàidhealach, agus tha i air a bhith ga riochdachadh air feadh an t-saoghail, gu h-àraid ann an Aimearaga a Tuath, Astràilia, Sealan Nuadh agus anns an Roinn Eòrpa.

The Comunn Gàidhlig Thoronto (Toronto Gaelic Society) began to meet in 1880 and was formally established in 1887.
’S e Comunn Gàidhlig Thoronto an comunn Gàidhealach ann am baile Toronto ann an Canada. Chaidh a chur air chois ann an 1880 agus chaidh a stéidheachadh gu foirmeil leis an riaghaltas ann an 1887.
Its primary purpose was to give assistance to immigrants from the Gàidhealtachd, but it also aimed to preserve the Scottish Gaelic language and culture.
Am prìomh amas a bh’ aige an toiseach b’ e sochair a thoirt do in-imircich bho Ghàidhealtachd na h-Albann, ach bha e cuideachd a’ toirt taic don chànan agus don chultar.
Through the years, as the country itself established a social safety net and more benefits, the society took on a cultural role, preserving Gaelic traditions such as cèilidhs.
Thar nam bliadhnaichean, mar a bha an riaghaltas fhéin a’ gabhail cùram air sochairean, mar a bha tuill nan in-imirceach a’ tiormachadh, lughdaich e agus thionndaich e barrachd dhan chultar agus céilidhean.
As its membership aged, the club began to wane and finally ended at the end of the 1980s.
Ach mar am ballrachd a’ fàs nas aosta, dh’ fhàs e lag agus cha mhór nach do bhàsaich e aig deireadh nan 1980an.
It was revived in 2012, however, by young learners interested in the language.
Thàinig ath-bheòthachadh sunndach bhon bhliadhna 2012, ge-tà, ga bhrosnachadh le luchd-ionnsachaidh òg a bha dìleas don chànan.

Dàimh is a band.
'S e còmhlan-ciùil a tha ann an Dàimh.
Its members are Aonghas MacCoinnich (whistle/bagpipes), Gabe McVarish (fiddle), Griogair Labhruidh (voice/bagpipes), Murdo Cameron (mandolin/accordion) and Ross Martin (giotàr).[1] In addition, Calum Ailig Mac a' Mhaoilein sang with them on their album Crossing Point.
'S iad buill a' chòmhlain Aonghas MacCoinnich (feadan/pìob), Gabe McVarish (fìdheall), Griogar Labhruidh (seinn/pìob), Murdo Cameron (mandolin/bòcsa-ciùil) agus Ross Martin (giotàr).[1] Roimhe seo, sheinn Calum Ailig Mac a' Mhaoilein còmhla riutha air a' chlàr Crossing Point.
The group was established in the western Gàidhealtachd as this was the homeland of the original members.[1] Aonghas MacCoinnich is from Màbu, Ceap Breatainn; Gabe McVarish is from California; Ross Martin is from Àrasaig; Griogar Labhruidh is from Bhealach and Murdo Cameron from Gleann Eilg.
Tha an còmhlan stèidhichte air taobh an iar na Gàidhealtachd ged is ann à àiteachan eadar-nàiseanta a tha na buill o thùs.[1] 'S ann à Màbu, Ceap Breatainn a tha Aonghas MacCoinnich; 's ann à California a tha Gabe McVarish; 's ann à Àrasaig a tha Ross Martin; tha Griogar Labhruidh às a' Bhealach agus tha Murdo Cameron à Gleann Eilg.
The group has released five albums.

The Comunn Oiseanach Oilthigh Ghlaschu was established in 1831 and is the second-oldest organization on campus.
Chaidh Comunn Oiseanach Oilthigh Ghlaschu a stèidheachadh ann an 1831 agus is e an dara comann as sine air a' champus.
Originally a discussion group in Scottish Gaelic, it has broadened its focus to embrace the revival of interest in the Gaelic language and culture especially among students of the University of Glasgow.
Bhon a bhith na chomann deasbaid Gàidhlig, tha e air leudachadh gu bhith na bhuidheann a tha a' brosnachadh ùidh ann an Gàidhlig agus cultar nan Ceilteach fad is farsaing ach gu h-àraidh a-measg nan oileanach aig Oilthigh Ghlaschu.

Marcas Mac an Tuairneir is an English writer born in York (28th November 1984).
'S e sgrìobhadair Sasannach a th' ann am Marcas Mac an Tuairneir a rugadh ann an Eabhraig (28mh den t-Samhna 1984).
Mac an Tuairneir writes and publishes in Scottish Gaelic and English.
Bidh Mac an Tuairneir a' sgrìobhadh is a' foillseachadh ann an Gàidhlig is Beurla.
He was educated at the University of Aberdeen, earning a master's degree in Gaelic and Spanish in 2008.[1] Cheumnaich e le iar-cheum Litreachas na h-Èireann is na h-Alba bhon aon oilthigh ann an 2010[1] and he wrote his thesis about intentionality in the poetry of Crìsdean Mac Ille-Bhàin and Cathal Ó Searcaigh.
Ghabh e foghlam bho Oilthigh Obar Dheathain, a' crìochnachadh Mgr E Gàidhlig is Spàinntis ann an 2008.[1] Cheumnaich e le iar-cheum Litreachas na h-Èireann is na h-Alba bhon aon oilthigh ann an 2010[1] agus sgrìobh e tràchdas mun tèama aona-ghnèitheachd anns a' bhàrdachd aig Crìsdean Mac Ille-Bhàin is Cathal Ó Searcaigh.
He completed a master's degree in writing fiction for television at the Glasgow Caledonian University between 2011 and 2012, when he began to write for MG Alba.[2][1]
Rinn e Mgr E ann an Sgrìobhadh Ficsean Telebhisein aig Oilthigh Cailleannach Ghlaschu eadar 2011 is 2012, far an do choisinn e sgoilearachd bho MG Alba.[2][1]
Poetry
Bàrdachd
Mac an Tuairneir published poetry in Cabhsair, Irish Pages and Poetry Scotland, among other publications.
Tha bàrdachd Mhic an Tuairneir air a nochdadh ann an Cabhsair, Irish Pages is ann am Poetry Scotland, am measg grunn eile.
He published his first collection, Deò[3], in 2013.[1] His second collection, Lus na Tùise, was published in 2016.
Chaidh a' chiad chruinneachadh aige, 'Deò'[3], fhoillseachadh ann an 2013.[1] Chaidh an dàrna cruinneachadh aige, 'Lus na Tùise' fhoillseachadh ann an 2016.
In 2014, he won a Highland Literary Salon prize for his poetry and both first and second place in the Baker Prize[1].
Ann an 2014 choisinn e duais na bàrdachd aig Salon Litreachas na Gàidhealtachd agus duais Baker na Gàidhlig aig Seòmar Leughadh an Eilein Sgitheanaich[1].
In 2015, he placed second for the William Blake Poetry Prize for his English language poetry.[1] He was shortlisted for the Gaelic award at the literary festival in Wigtown in 2015 and 2016.
Ann an 2015 fhuair e dàrna àite ann am farpais nàiseanta Uilleim Blake, son a chuid bàrdachd ann am Beurla.[1] Bha e air geàrr-liosta duais na Gàidhlig aig Fèis Leabhraichean Bhaile na h-Ùige ann am 2015 agus 2016.
In 2012 he was shortlisted for the Comórtas Filíochta an Chornéil Uí Néill[2] and, in 2016, for the award Dhughlas de hÍde at the Fèis Litreachas Bhaile na mBuillí, both Irish organizations.
Ann am 2012 bha e air geàrr-liosta son Comórtas Filíochta an Chornéil Uí Néill[2] agus, ann an 2016, air geàrr-liosta an farpais Dhughlas de hÍde aig Fèis Litreachas Bhaile na mBuillí, an dà chuid stèidhichte ann an Èirinn.
In 2016, he won the competition 'Write Up North!" at the Scottish Association of Writers.[1]
Ann an 2016 bhuannaich e am farpais 'Write Up North!" aig Cos-nasgadh nan Sgrìobhaichean Albannach.[1]
He contributes literary and artistic criticism and essays to Cothrom Ùr and Dàna.[1] He is the Gaelic editor of the magazine "The Poets' Republic".
Bidh e a' sgrìobhadh critig ealain is chaidh aistidhean aige fhoillseachadh ann an Cothrom Ùr agus Dàna.[1] 'S esan deasaiche na Gàidhlig den iris bàrdachd 'Poblachd nam Bàrd.'
Drama
Dràma
Mac an Tuairneir won the New Gaelic Dramatist award in 2016 at Playwrights' Studio Scotland and Comhairle nan Leabhraichean.
Choisinn Mac an Tuairneir duais Dràmaire Ùr na Gàidhlig ann an 2016 aig Playwrights' Studio Scotland agus Comhairle nan Leabhraichean.
He has written three plays.
Tha e air trì dealbhan-cluiche a sgrìobhadh.
'Diuchdadh' was performed at Eden Court, Inverness, in March 2016, directed by Mac an Tuairneir himself.
Nochd 'Diuchdadh' ann an Cùirt an Aodainn, Inbhir Nis, ann am Màrt 2016, fo stiùir Mhic an Tuairneir fhèin.
The script, written in verse, is his own work.
'S e dealbh-chluich sgrìobhte ann am bàrdachd a tha san obair seo.
'Tilleadh' and 'Tioradh' are his other dramas.[1]
'S iad 'Tilleadh' is 'Tioradh' na dealbhan-cluiche eile aige.[1]

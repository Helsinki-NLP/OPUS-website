The Tambo Island is located at the bottom of the Pontevedra bay, and belongs to the municipality of Poio, from which it is separated by a 1.1 km long but shallow channel.
A illa de Tambo atópase no fondo da ría de Pontevedra, e pertence ao concello de Poio do que esta separado por unha canle de 1,1 km pero de pouca profundidade.
Its surface area is 0.28 km², with a maximum height of 80 m, although its entire central part is over 50 metres high.
A súa superficie é de 0,28 km², cunha altura máxima de 80 m, aínda que toda a súa parte central supera os 50 metros de altitude.
Its perimeter reaches 4 km.
O seu perímetro acada os 4 km.

Among the various prehistoric remains on the island is a castro, which occupies the entire central part of the island and is listed by the General Direction of Heritage with the code GA36041043.
Entre os diversos restos prehistóricos da illa destaca un castro, que ocupa toda a parte central da illa e está catalogado pola Dirección Xeral de Patrimonio co código GA36041043.
A chapel was built in the 6th century, founded by Martín de Dumio.
No século VI construíuse unha capela, fundada por Martiño de Dumio.
Fructuosus of Braga turned it into a monastery, linked to the convent of San Juan de Poyo.
Froitoso de Braga converteuna en mosteiro, vinculado ao convento de San Xoán de Poio.
It later became a priory, under the patronage of Santa María de Gracia.
Despois transformouse en priorado, baixo a advocación de Santa María de Graza.
In the 12th century, the island of Tambo belonged to Queen Urraca and was donated to the convent of San Juan de Poyo.
No século XII a illa de Tambo pertencía á raíña Dona Urraca e foi doada ao convento de San Xoán de Poio.
The inhabitants of Combarro used the island for centuries, where they worked their lands and celebrated pilgrimages.
Os veciños de Combarro usaron a illa durante séculos, onde traballaban as súas terras e celebraban romarías.
In 1589 the chapel was plundered by the corsair Francis Drake, who threw the figure of Santa María de Gracia into the sea.
En 1589 a capela foi vítima do saqueo do corsario Francis Drake, o cal botou a figura de Santa María de Graza ao mar.
In 1846, with the Spanish confiscation it ceased to belong to the monks of Poyo and years later it was bought by the Minister of Justice, Eugenio Montero Ríos.
En 1846, coa desamortización de Mendizábal, deixou de pertencer aos monxes de Poio e anos despois foi mercada polo ministro de Xustiza, Eugenio Montero Ríos.
In the same century, a lazaretto was built on the island, which between 1865 and 1879 was used to quarantine sailors arriving at the port of Marín-Pontevedra.
Nese mesmo século construíuse un lazareto na illa, que entre os anos 1865 e 1879 foi usado para poñer en corentena os mariñeiros que chegaban ao porto de Marín.
Due to protests from the citizens of Pontevedra, the infirmary was moved to the island of San Simón, in the Vigo estuary, and in 1889 the island became a military penitentiary.
Debido ás protestas dos cidadáns de Pontevedra, o lazareto foi trasladado á illa de San Simón, na ría de Vigo, e en 1889 a illa converteuse en penitenciaría militar.
With the creation of the Marín Naval Military School in 1943, the island was militarised and came to be used as an arsenal.
Coa creación da Escola Naval Militar de Marín, en 1943 a illa militarizouse e pasou a ser utilizada como arsenal.
It was in that year that the heirs of Montero Ríos sold the island to the Navy.
Foi nese ano cando os herdeiros de Montero Ríos venderon a illa á Armada.
Until 2005, the heirs continued to be listed as owners of one fifth of the island.
Ata o ano 2005, os herdeiros seguiron figurando como propietarios dunha quinta parte da illa.
The Navy ceased to classify the island as a strategic enclave in 2002, which raised expectations that it could be ceded to the Xunta de Galicia and incorporated into the Atlantic Islands of Galicia National Park, for which it would first have to be declared a natural park.[1] However, the island remains under military jurisdiction.[2]
A Armada deixou de cualificar a illa como enclave estratéxico no ano 2002, o que disparou as expectativas de que puidese ser cedida á Xunta e incorporada ao Parque nacional das Illas Atlánticas, para o que antes debería ser declarada parque natural.[1] Porén, a illa continúa baixo xurisdición militar.[2]
On 14 April 2013, a group of Combarro residents made a symbolic declaration of independence of the island as an act of protest against the state of neglect it suffered after demilitarisation.[1] In October 2018, after a period of closure of the island that lasted two years, the Naval School began again to allow organised visits to the island.[2]
O 14 de abril de 2013, un grupo de veciños e veciñas de Combarro fixeron unha declaración de independencia simbólica na illa como acto de denuncia polo estado de abandono que sufriu logo da desmilitarización.[3] En outubro de 2018, logo dun período de peche da illa que durou dous anos, a Escola Naval comezou de novo a permitir visitas organizadas á illa.[4]

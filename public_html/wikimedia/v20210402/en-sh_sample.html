"Veliki Tepčija" took care of the royal estates and had office at the ruler's (Ban, later King) court.
Veliki tepčija je bio na vladarevom dvoru, a tepčija je bio na službi u župi ili pri oblasnom gospodaru.
''Tepčija'', if there was "Lower Tepčija", cared of all major feudal estates bar that which belonged to the Court, where "Lower Tepčija" cared about rest of the land.<ref>{{cite book|title=Историски гласник: орган Историского друштва НР Србије|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Mo5pAAAAMAAJ|year=1975|publisher=Научна књига|page=25|quote=<!--Добравца-->}}</ref>
Bez obzira na rang, tepčije su vodile brigu o zemljoposjedima svojih gospodara (bilo vladara, bilo oblasnih gospodara ili župljana). Ovi dostojanstvenici se pojavljuju tokom razgraničenja posjeda (vladarskih, gospodarevih ili župskih). U Srbiji se titula velikog tepčije ugasila prije Dušanovog krunisanja za cara, a u Bosni krajem XIV i početkom XV stoljeća
Batalo belonged to medieval Šantić family.
Batalo je ripadao je rodu Šantića.
He married Resa of Hrvatinići, sister of Bosnian Grand Duke, Hrvoje Vukčić Hrvatinić, which elevated his status and gave him lordship over medieval Sana region, or at least over some of its parts.
Bio je oženjen Resom iz plemićkog roda Hrvatinića, te je tako zagospodario i župom Sanom.
His seat was fortress of Toričan, above contemporary Varošluk village.[1]
Iznad današnjeg sela Varošluk podigao je dobro utvrđeni grad Toričan.[1]
The first mention of Batalo is found in issue by King Stjepan Dabiša, from July 17, 1392, to the government of Dubrovnik.
Prve podatke o Batalu nalazimo u ispravi kralja Stjepana Dabiše, koju je kralj 17. jula 1392. godine izdao dubrovačkoj vladi.
In it Batalo figure in a role of King Court's "Tepčija" and a witness to the validity of issue.
U ovome dokumentu Batalo se spominje u ulozi dvorskog tepčije i ulozi svjedoka u validnosti isprave.
He was also, among others, major influencer at Bosnian Court and adviser to bosnian rulers from late 14th century, as well as confidant of Hrvoje Vukčić.
Bio je zajedno sa ostalim velmožama među glavnim savjetnicima bosanskih vladara iz kraja 14. vijeka.
Batalo was also recognized by historians as one of noblemen close and /or supportive of Bosnian Church.
Poznat je i po svojim vezama sa Crkvom bosanskom.

Batalo's Gospel is dated to 1393.
Nastalo je 1393. godine.
Scribe, in medieval Bosnia called dijak, local Stanko Kromirijanin, was identified as a writer, and Bosnian nobleman and Tepčija, Batalo Šantić was his patron and customer.
Pisar (dijak) Stanko Kromirijanin prepisao je ovo evanđelje za bosanskog velmožu tepčiju Batala Šantića.
Four pages still exist, and are held in National Library of Russia in Sankt Petersburgu.
Očuvana su četiri lista koja se nalaze u Nacionalnoj biblioteci u Sankt Petersburgu.
On the third page dijak Stanko (scribe) states that he was writing ornate gospel for Batalo, dating its completion to 1393, two years after Tvrtko I death, during the reign of Dabiša.
Na trećem listu pisac navodi da je prepisao evanđelje za tepčiju Batala, koje je bilo ukrašeno, te vrijeme njegovog završetka koje se desilo 1393. godine dvije godine poslije smrti kralja Tvrtka a u doba kralja Dabiše.
[2] Here we also learn about his fortress Toričan, and his dominion over Sana, as well as name of his wife, Resa, sister of Grand Duke of Bosnia, Hrvoje Vukčić.
[2] Iz očuvanog dijela teksta saznaje se da je tepčija Batalo držao grad Toričan, župu Sanu i da je bip oženjen Resom, sestrom velikog vojvode bosanskog Hrvoja Vukčića Hrvatinića.
On page two there is a two column list of names of all contemporary Djeds of Bosnian Church as well as those before and after Rastudije.
Na drugoj stranici dopisana su dva niza imena onih „koji su se narekli u red Crkve“ prije i poslije Rastudija.
Researchers call this list „Red gospodina Rastudija“ (Order of Bishop Rastudije), and is understood as a list of names of all Bosnian Church bishops before and after him.
To je tzv. „Red gospodina Rastudija“ koji se shvata kao popis imena onih koji su upravljali Crkvom bosanskom prije i poslije Rastudija.
Batalo's grave and mausoleum was discovered in 1915 by captain of Austro-Hungarian army, Teplý, on the hill called Crkvine.
Batalovu grobnicu (mauzolej) je 1915. godine otkrio kapetan Teplý na uzvišenju Crkvine.
Crkvine locality is multi-layered archaeological site, with cultural-historical continuity dating back from neolithic, to early Roman and late antiquity, to end of the 14th century.
Crkvine predstavljaju višeslojno arheološko područje koje datira u kulturno-povijesnom kontinuitetu od neolita i eneolita, pa preko ranorimske nekropole i kasnoantičkog mauzoleja do kraja 14. st.
At the entrance large block of limestone carried stećak.
Na gornjoj ploči je stajao stećak u obliku sljemenjaka.
Frontal stone plate, dimension 17 x 24 cm, carried inscription in Bosančica :
Čeona kamena ploča je sa natpisom u bosanskoj ćirilici, dimenzija 17 x 24 cm. Ploča je oštećena u gornjem dijelu na kojem se nalazi natpis:
Ase leži] uzmo [žni] muž' t [ep']čija Batal[o] bosan'ski a pisa Radomil' dijak'. Here lies mighty man bosnian tepčija Batalo so is writing dijak Radomil.
Ase leži] uzmo [žni] muž' t [ep']čija Batal[o] bosan'ski a pisa Radomil' dijak'.

This is a List of djed (Eng. bishop) of Bosnian Church. The source is historical document known as Batalo's Gospel.
Spisak djedova crkve bosanske, kao nalazi se istorijskom dokumentu poznatom kao Batalovo jevanđelje.
Batalo was Tepčija at Bosnian Court from the late 14th century.
Batalo je tepčija na bosanskom dvoru s kraja XIV vijekabo.
The list contains 28 names of Bosnian Church bishops called "djed" (literally, Eng. singular, "grandfather"), from middle of the 11th century until 1393.
U spisaku se nalazi spisak 28 poglavara, od sredine XI vijekaa, pa sve do 1393. godine.
Note: years in parenthesis indicating assumed period of governance.
Napomena: u zagradama se nalazi pretpostavljeno vrijeme upravljanja crkvom.

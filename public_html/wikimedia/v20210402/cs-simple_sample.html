Fidget spinner je hračka, o které si někteří lidé myslí, že pomáhá dětem, které potřebují rozptýlit (s ADHD, autismem a podobně.)
A fidget spinner is a toy that some people believe helps children who can become distracted.
Společnost Gadget World [1] na svém blogu tyto údajné výhody popisuje.
Gadget World [1]have published a blog to explain the alleged benefits.
Naopak jiní lidé, hlavně učitelé[2], hovoří o tom, že hračka děti rozptyluje.
However, other people such as teachers [2] believe that fidget spinners are a distraction.
Catherine Hettingerová, chemická inženýrka, je některými novináři označována za vynálezkyni fidget spinnerů. Její příběh se objevil i v novinách jako The Guardian, The New York Times nebo New York Post.
Catherine Hettinger, a chemical engineer, was first credited by some news stories to have been the inventor of the fidget spinner, including by media outlets such as The Guardian, The New York Times and the New York Post.
V roce 1993 podala společnost Hettinger patentovou přihlášku pro "spinningovou hračku".
Hettinger filed a patent application for a "spinning toy" in 1993.
Hlavním cílem fidget spinners je zábava.
Fidget spinners are made to entertain people.
Některé kousky jsou vyráběny hlavně s důrazem na cool vzhled, jiné hlavně pro "fidgering".
Some are made to look cool and some are made for fidgeting.
Nejpopulárnější jsou svítící fidger spinners s LED diodami, vyráběné v Londýně.
The most popular fidget spinners are the glowing ones and the LED ones in London.
Existuje mnoho druhů fidget spinnerů, jako třeba svítící, s LED diodami nebo kovově lesklé.
There are many kinds of fidget spinners like the glowing ones, the LED ones, and the shiny metallic ones.

Brett E. Crozier je americký námořní důstojník.
Brett E. Crozier is an American naval officer.

When he was captain of the aircraft carrier Theodore Roosevelt, he noticed that some of his crew had caught COVID-19, a serious disease that affects the lungs and breathing.
Koncem března 2020 napsal dopisy, ve kterých žádal o povolení k evakuaci nemocných na ostrov Guam.Tyto dopisy unikly do médií.
Late in March, 2020, he wrote letters asking for permission to send his sick sailors off his ship to Guam. These letters were found and printed in a newspaper.
O několik dní později byl Crozier odvolán z pozice kapitána lodi, ale nebyl propuštěn z Námořnictva.
A few days later, the Navy fired Crozier as the captain of the Theodore Roosevelt but not kicked out of the Navy.

Li čen-šeng (čínsky: 22. září 1940 - 23. června 2020) byl čínský fotožurnalista .
Li Zhensheng (Chinese: 李振盛; pinyin: Lǐ Zhènshèng; 22 September 1940 – 23 June 2020) was a Chinese photojournalist.
Zachytil některé z nejrozsáhlejších obrazů z Velké proletářské kulturní revoluce, známější jako čínská kulturní revoluce. [1] Li se narodil v Ta-lienu .
He captured some of the most telling images from the Great Proletarian Cultural Revolution, better known as the Chinese Cultural Revolution.[1] Li was born in Dalian.
Byl známý spoluprací s Heilongjiang Daily .
He was known for working with Heilongjiang Daily.
V roce 2017 bylo v provincii S'-čchuan v rámci muzea Jianchuan otevřeno soukromé muzeum věnované životu a práci Li. [1]
A private museum, dedicated to Li's life and work, was opened in 2017 in Sichuan Province as a part of the Jianchuan Museum Cluster.[2]

She worked with National Film Board of Canada.

Many of her works were seen at the National Gallery of Canada.

The inauguration marked the beginning of the four-year term of Donald Trump as President and of Mike Pence as Vice President.

The main theme in the inauguration speech of President Trump was his focus on the interests of the United States ("America first!", "Make America Great Again").

Presidents Jimmy Carter, Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, and Barack Obama, along with their respective wives, the First Ladies (including Hillary Clinton),[1] had all confirmed their attendance at Trump's inauguration,[2] and were all present.

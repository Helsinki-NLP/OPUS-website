Dragan Mektić (*24. decembra 1956. godine u Prnjavoru) je bosanskohercegovački kriminalista i političar.
Dragan Mektić (born in Prnjavor on 24 December 1956) is a Bosnian politician and criminal investigator.
Od 31. marta 2015. godine je na funkciji ministra sigurnosti Bosne i Hercegovine, te je član Srpske demokratske stranke (SDS)[1].
He is a member of the Serb Democratic Party (SDS) and since 31 March 2015 serves as the Minister of Security of Bosnia and Herzegovina[1].
Mektić je završio srednju mašinsku školu u bosanskohercegovačkom gradu Banja Luka, nakon čega je završio višu upravnu školu, te diplomirao kriminalistiku na pravnom fakultetu Univerziteta u Zagrebu, glavnom gradu Hrvatske.
Mektić completed an engineering high school in the Bosnian city of Banja Luka, after which he completed a higher administrative school, and graduated in Criminal Justice at the University of Zagreb.
Dragan Mektić je od 1980. do 1997. godine rukovodio policijskoj stanici, gdje je se bavio na suzbijanju općeg i finansijskog kriminala.
From 1980 to 1997 Mektić directed a police station, where he was engaged in suppressing general and financial crime.
Nakon toga, od 1997. do 2003. godine, Mektić je bio zaposlen u obavještajnom sektoru Bosne i Hercegovine.
Then, from 1997 to 2003, Mektic was employed in the intelligence sector of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
Od 2003. do 2006. godine je bio zamjenik tadašnjeg ministra sigurnosti BiH Borisa Čolak[2], nakon čega je devet godina, od 2006. do 2015. godine, bio direktor Službe za poslove sa strancima Bosne i Hercegovine.
From 2003 to 2006 he was deputy of the then security minister Boris Čolak[2], followed by nine years, from 2006 to 2015, as director of the Service for Foreigners' Affairs of Bosnia and Herzegovina.
31. marta 2015. godine Mektić je imenovan za ministra sigurnosti Bosne i Hercegovine u sklopu vlade između Srpske demokratske zajednice (SDS), Hrvatske demokratske zajednice BiH (HDZ BiH) i Stranke demokratske akcije (SDA), na čijem je čelu Denis Zvizdić[3].
On 31 March 2015 Mektic was appointed Minister of Security of Bosnia and Herzegovina as part of the government led by Denis Zvizdić[3].

Josip Grubeša
Josip Grubeša je bosankohercegovački političar.
Josip Grubeša is a Bosnian politician.
Od 31. marta 2015. godine je na funkciji ministra pravde Bosne i Hercegovine, a od 2011. godine Grubeša je član predsjedništva Hrvatske demokratske zajednice Bosne i Hercegovine (HDZ BiH)[1].
Since March 31, 2015 he has been serving as Minister of Justice of Bosnia and Herzegovina. Since 2011 Grubeša is a member of the Presidency of the Croatian Democratic Union of Bosnia and Herzegovina (HDZ BiH).[1]
Josip Grubeša je rođen 4. novembra 1978. godine u bosanskohercegovačkom gradu Prozor-Rama.
Josip Grubeša was born on 4 November 1978 in Prozor-Rama, Bosnia and Herzegovina.
1997. godine je završio nadbiskupsku gimnaziju Zadru, nakon čega je diplomirao na filozofskom fakultetu na Univertziteta u Splitu.
In 1997 he graduated from the Archdiocese's Gymnasium of Zadar, after which he graduated from the Faculty of Philosophy at the University of Split.
Nakon što je diplomirao, bio je 2003. godine zaposlen u filozofskom fakultetu Univerziteta u Mostaru.
He was then employed in the Faculty of Philosophy of the University of Mostar in 2003.
Sedam godina nakon toga, Grubeša je 2010. godine počeo raditi kao gostujući profesor na Medicinskom i Pravnom fakultetu Univerziteta u Mostaru, što i danas radi.
Seven years later, Grubeša started working as a visiting professor at the Medical and Law Faculty of the University of Mostar, which he still does today.
2011. godine je ponovo diplomirao, sada na filozofskom fakultetu Univerziteta u Mostaru.
In 2011 he graduated again, now at the Faculty of Philosophy of the University of Mostar.
Od te godine je i član predsjedništva Hrvatske demokratske zajednice Bosne i Hercegovine (HDZ BiH).
Since that year, he is a member of the Presidency of the Croatian Democratic Union of Bosnia and Herzegovina (HDZ BiH).
31. marta 2015. godine, Josip Grubeša je imenovan za ministra pravde Bosne i Hercegovine u sklopu Vlade između Srpske demokratske stranke (SDS), Stranke demokratske akcije (SDA) i Hrvatske demokratske zajednice BiH (HDZ BiH), na čijem je čelu Denis Zvizdić.
On March 31, 2015, Josip Grubeša was appointed Minister of Justice of Bosnia and Herzegovina within the SDA/SDS/HDZ coalition government led by Denis Zvizdić.
Osim hrvatskog i bosanskog, Grubeša govori i njemački jezik.
Grubeša is marred with three children. In addition to Croatian and Bosnian, he speaks German.

Atik Behram-begova (Šarena) džamija je najstarija džamija u Tuzli.
The Šarena džamija (colourful mosque), formally Atik Behram Bey mosque, is the oldest mosque in Tuzla, Bosnia and Herzegovina.
Ime Atik ova džamija je dobila zbog toga što je ona, vjerovatno, najstarija tuzlanska džamija, a ime Behram-begova dobila je po tome što je preko puta nje bila smještena Behram-begova medresa, i što je vjerovatno sam ovaj vakif dao da se ona obnovi i održava.
The name of Atik was given to this mosque because it is probably the oldest mosque in Tuzla. It also acquired the name of Behram Bey due to the Behram-begova medresa being placed in front of it, and because the mosque was probably renewed and maintained as part of the same waqf (vakif).
Također je zbog svoje ukrašene unutrašnjosti nazivaju i Šarena džamija.
Due to the decorated interiors, it is called the Colorful Mosque (Šarena džamija).
Džamija je sagrađena na jednom manjem brežuljku, veličine je 10x10m i dominira okolinom.
The mosque was built on a small hill, the size is 10x10m and dominates the environment.
Prije požara 1871. godine bila je zidana od ćerpića, s drvenom kupolom.
Before the fire of 1871, it was built of clay, with a wooden cupola.
Imala je nakon ponovne izgradnje 1888. godine i kupolu od čvrstog materijala, ali je ona brzo zamijenjena krovom od crijepa.
After its rebuilding in 1888, it had a dome of solid materials, but this was quickly replaced by a roof of a tile.
U prizemlju ima deset prozora pravljenih od željeza sa ukrštenim rešetkama.
On the ground floor there are ten windows made of iron with cross bars.
Unutrašnjost džamije je ukrašena raznim mustrama.
The interior of the mosque is decorated with various furniture.
Minber je izrađen u arapskom stilu.
The minbar is built in Arabic style.
Munara se sastoji iz više djelova, pa je tako od podnožja do vrha zida džamije četvrtasta sa uskim krovom sa tri strane.
The minaret consists of several parts, so it is squared from the base to the top of the mosque wall with a narrow roof on three sides.
Džamija kao i sami harem je osiguran potpornim zidom od kamena sa betonskom pločom.
The mosque, as well as the harem itself, is secured by a rock-wall support wall with a concrete slab.

Mustafa Busuladžić
Mustafa Busuladžić (1. april 1914. - 29. juni 1945.) bio je pisac, naučnik i jedan od istaknutih muslimanskih i bošnjačkih intelektualaca u periodu između dva svjetska rata.
Mustafa Busuladžić (1. april 1914. - 29. juni 1945.) was a writer, scientist and one of the prominent Muslim and Bosniak intellectuals in the period between the two world wars.
Bio je nosilac ideje nacionalističkog i panislamskog pokreta Mladi muslimani,[1][2][3][4][5][6][7] antikomunist i antisemitist.[8][9][10] Podržavao je stavove nacističke propagande i progona Židova u Sarajevu od strane ustaša, te je svojim pisanim djelima i esejima u novinama Osvit podržavao Ustaški pokret.[9] Pored maternjeg bosanskog jezika govorio je i arapski, turski, njemački, francuski i italijanski jezik.[11]
He was the bearer of the idea of the nationalist and pan-Islamic movement Young Muslims (Mladi muslimani),[1][2][3][4][5][6][7] anti-Communist and anti-Semitist.[8][9][10] He supported the views of the Nazi propaganda and persecution of the Jews in Sarajevo by the Ustashas, and he supported the Ustasha Movement with his written works and essays in the Osvit newspaper.[9] In addition to his native Bosnian, he also spoke Arabic, Turkish, German, French and Italian.[11]
Rođen je 1. aprila 1914. godine u Gorici kod Trebinja, od oca Smaila i majke Emine.[12] Mekteb i osnovnu školu pohađao je u rodnom mjestu nakon čega odlazi u Travnik gdje se upisuje u Elči Ibrahim-pašinu medresu.
Busuladžić was born on April 1, 1914 in Gorica near Trebinje, son of Smail and Emina. He attended the mekteb and elementary school in his hometown, after which he went to Travnik where he enrolled in the Elca Ibrahim Pasha Medrese.
Poslije jedne ili dvije godine provedene u travničkoj medresi, prebacuje se na dalje školovanje u Gazi Husrev-begovu medresu u Sarajevu gdje i maturira 1936. godine.
After one or two years spent in the Travnik medrese, he moved to further education at Gazi Husrev-beg's medrese in Sarajevo where he graduated in 1936.
Kao učenik medrese objavljivao je svoje tekstove u Islamskom glasu, Novom Beharu, Obzoru, Svijesti, El-Hidaji, Glasniku VIS-a, Našoj domovini i drugim publikacijama.
As a medrese student, he published his texts in Islamskom glasu, Novom Beharu, Obzoru, Svijesti, El-Hidaji, Glasniku VIS-a, Našoj domovini and other publications. .[12]
Nakon završene medrese upisao se na Višu islamsku šerijatsku školu u Sarajevu gdje je diplomirao 1941. godine.
After completing the Madrasa, he enrolled at the Islamic Sharia School in Sarajevo, where he graduated in 1941.

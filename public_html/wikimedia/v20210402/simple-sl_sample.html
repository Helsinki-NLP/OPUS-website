Taklamakan Desert
Puščava Takla Makan
The Taklamakan Desert, also known as Taklimakan and Teklimakan, is a desert in northwest China, in the southwest part of the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region.
Puščava Takla Makan, poznana tudi pod imenom Taklimakan in Teklimakan, je puščava na severozahodu Kitajske.
It is bounded by the Kunlun Mountains to the south, and the desert Pamir Mountains and Tian Shan to the west and north. The Taklamakan Desert Ecoregion is a Chinese ecoregion of the Deserts and xeric shrublands Biome.
Na jugu se dotika gorovja Kunlun, na severu in zahodu pa gorovja Pamir in Tian Shan.
There is very little water in the desert and it is dangerous to cross.
V puščavi Takla Makan je zelo malo vode in nevarno jo je prečkati. Trgovci, ki so jo prečkali po Svilni cesti, so se pogosto ustavljali ob cvetočih mestih ob oazah.
Merchant caravans on the Silk Road stopped for relief at the thriving oasis towns.[1] The key oasis towns, watered by rainfall from the mountains, were Kashgar, Marin, Niya, Yarkand, and Khotan to the south, Kuqa and Turpan in the north, and Loulan and Dunhuang in the east.[2] Some of these are now ruined cities in sparsely inhabited areas in the Xinjiang Autonomous Region.[3] The present population consists largely of Turkic Uyghur people.
Najpomembnejša mesta ob oazah, ki jih je napajal dež z gora, so bila Kashgar, Marin, Niya, Yarkand, Khotan, Kuqa, Turpan, Loulan in Dunhuang. Ruševine od nekaterih izmed njih so še vedno vidne v dokaj poseljenih krajih v avtonomni pokrajini Xinjiang. Ljudje, ki tam živijo danes, so večinoma turško ljudstvo Ujgurcev.

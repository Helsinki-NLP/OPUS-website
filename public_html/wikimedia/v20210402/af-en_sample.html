Die Eugène Maraisprys, vantevore bekend as die "Aanmoedigingsprys" en later die "Debuutprys", is 'n toekenning van die Suid-Afrikaanse Akademie vir Wetenskap en Kuns vir 'n eerste of vroeë publikasie in Afrikaans.
The Eugène Marais Prize is a South African literary prize awarded by the Suid-Afrikaanse Akademie vir Wetenskap en Kuns for a first or early publication in Afrikaans.

Dit is in 1971 hernoem na die Afrikaanse digter en navorser Eugène Marais.
In 1971 it was renamed after the Afrikaans poet and researcher Eugène Marais.

Die Verdragboom (ook bekend as die Traktaatboom) is 'n ou melkhoutboom in Treaty Road (langs Springstraat) en suid van die spoorlyn in Woodstock waarby op 10 Januarie 1806 die vredesverdrag na die Slag van Blaauwberg onderteken is.
The Treaty Tree (Afrikaans: Verdragboom or Traktaatboom ) is a 500 year old White Milkwood tree on Treaty Road and south of the rail line in Woodstock, Cape Town. Peace was made under the tree on the 10 January 1806 after the Battle of Blaauwberg.
Die terrein is die eiendom van die Stad Kaapstad en die melkhoutboom daarop is in 1967 tot gedenkwaardigheid verklaar.
The City of Cape Town owns the property and the tree was declared a monument in 1967.
Ander melkhoutbome wat tot monumente verklaar is, is die Poskantoorboom by Mosselbaai, die Fingo-melkhoutboom en die Renosterfontein-melkhoutboom.
There are several other White Milkwood trees that have been declared monuments in South Africa including: The Post Office Tree in Mosselbay The Fingo-White Milkwood The Renosterfontein-White Milkwood.

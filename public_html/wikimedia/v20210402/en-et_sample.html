An Italian Woman with Children by a Stream is a painting by Johann Köleri from 1862. The work has dimensions 82.5 cm x 53.5 cm.
"Itaallanna lastega ojal" on Johann Köleri maal aastast 1862.

The work belongs to the Art Museum of Estonia's collection and are exhibited in Kumu Art museum. The painting is a realistic view with a warm color palette.
Maal kuulub Eesti Kunstimuuseumi kollektsiooni ja seda eksponeeritakse Kumu kunstimuuseumis.

Volli Kalm (10 February 1953, Aluste – 23 December 2017, Kääriku) was an Estonian geologist who, from 1 July 2012 to his death on 23 December 2017, was the rector of the University of Tartu.
Volli Kalm (10. veebruar 1953 Aluste, Vändra rajoon – 23. detsember 2017 Kääriku) oli eesti geoloog, alates 1. juulist 2012 kuni surmani Tartu Ülikooli rektor.
Kalm graduated from Vändra Secondary School in 1971 and began studying geology at the University of Tartu, graduating in 1976.
Volli Kalm lõpetas 1971. aastal Vändra Keskkooli ning asus õppima Tartu Riikliku Ülikooli geoloogia erialale, mille lõpetas 1976. aastal.
From 1980 to 1984, he was a graduate student at the Institute of Geology at the Estonian Academy of Sciences, defending his degree in geology with a dissertation "Formation, composition and use of glaciofluvial deposits in Estonia" (his supervisor was Anto Raukas) in 1984.
Aastatel 1980–1984 oli ta aspirant Eesti NSV Teaduste Akadeemia Geoloogia Instituudis, kaitstes 1984. aastal geoloogiakandidaadi kraadi väitekirjaga "Formation, composition and usage of glaciofluvial deposits in Estonia" (juhendaja Anto Raukas).
Between 1988 and 1989, he was a postdoctoral student at the Department of Geology at the University of Alberta.[1]
Aastatel 1988–1989 oli ta järeldoktor Alberta Ülikooli geoloogiaosakonnas.[1]
Kalm had worked since 1981 in the Institute of Geology at the Faculty of Biology and Geography of the University of Tartu (since 2008 at the Institute of Ecology and Earth Sciences at the Faculty of Science and Technology), first as an assistant, from 1986 to 1988 as a lecturer, from 1988 to 1992 as a docent, and, from 1992 until his appointment as rector, as a professor of applied geology.[1]
Kalm töötas alates 1981. aastast Tartu Ülikooli bioloogia-geograafiateaduskonna geoloogia instituudis (alates 2008. aastast loodus- ja tehnoloogiateaduskonna ökoloogia- ja maateaduste instituudis), esmalt assistendina, 1986–1988 lektorina, 1988–1992 dotsendina ning alates 1992. aastast rakendusgeoloogia professorina.[1]
From 1998 to 2003, he was the Vice-Rector for the University of Tartu.
Aastatel 1998–2003 oli ta Tartu Ülikooli õppeprorektor.
In 2007, He applied twice to be the rector of the university; the first time in 2007 was a failure, losing the position to Alar Karis was ahead of him in other elections.
2007. aastal kandideeris ta kahel korral Tartu Ülikooli rektoriks, ent esimesel korral jäi rektor valimata, teistel valimistel edestas teda Alar Karis.
Kalm was chosen rector of the University of Tartu on 31 May 2012, ahead of Mart Ustav and Toivo Maimets.
31. mail 2012 valiti Kalm Tartu Ülikooli rektoriks, valimistel edestas ta Mart Ustavit ja Toivo Maimetsa.

Kalm has published studies on the mineralogy of lithology and stratigraphy, clay and archaeological ceramics in Estonia and the paleogeography of the Baltic, Scandinavian and Andean glaciers.[2]
Kalm on avaldanud uurimusi Eesti liustikusette litoloogia ja stratigraafia, savide ja arheoloogilise keraamika mineraloogia ning Baltikumi, Skandinaavia ja Andide jäätumise paleogeograafia alalt.[2]

Märt Rask (born 19 December 1950) is an Estonian jurist and poltician.
Märt Rask (sündinud 19. detsembril 1950) on Eesti jurist ja poliitik.
Rask graduated from the Faculty of Law of the University of Tartu in 1978.
Rask lõpetas 1978. aastal Tartu Riikliku Ülikooli õigusteaduskonna.
Starting 1 August 1978, From August 1, 1978, he was a member of the Estonian SSR Lawyers' College.
Alates 1. augustist 1978 kuulus ta Eesti NSV Advokaatide kolleegiumi.
From 1978 to 1979, he was a legal adviser in Pärnu and Tallinn, and later became the Deputy Legal Director and Head of Legal Advice from 1979 to 1990, as well as the first Deputy Minister of Justice of the Estonian SSR from 1990 to 1992.[1]
Aastatel 1978–1979 oli ta Pärnu õigusnõuandla stazöör ja Tallinna õigusnõuandla stazöör, aastatel 1979–1990 Tallinna I õigusnõuandla advokaat, õigusnõuandla juhataja asetäitja ja -juhataja, 1990–1992 Eesti NSV justiitsministri esimene asetäitja[1].
From 1995 to 1996, Rask was the Estonian Minister of the Interior, as well as being the Justice Minister in 1992 and from 1999 to 2003.
Aastatel 1995–1996 oli ta Eesti siseminister ning aastatel 1992 ja 1999–2003 justiitsminister.
From 2004 to 2013, Rask was the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court of Estonia.
Aastatel 2004 kuni 2013 oli Märt Rask Riigikohtu esimees.
Rask was the son of lawyer Valdeko Leeto (1927–1992).
Märt Rask on advokaat Valdeko Leeto (1927–1992) väimees.
Rask's son, Rasmus Rask (born 26 February 1977) was a member of the Estonian Bar Association from 2000 to 2001.[2]
Märt Raski poeg Rasmus Rask (sündinud 26.02.1977) oli 2000–2001 Eesti Advokatuuri liige.[2]
Rask is a member of the Estonian Reform Party.
Märt Rask on Reformierakonna liige.

The Metsakalmistu (Forest Cemetery) is a cemetery in the Pirita district in Tallinn.
Metsakalmistu on surnuaed Tallinnas Pirita linnaosas Kloostrimetsa asumis Kloostrimetsa tee 36.
The forest cemetery was originally planned to be an open city medieval cemetery.
Metsakalmistu kavandati avalikuks linna keskkalmistuks.
Eduard Vilde was the first to be buried in 1933.
Esimesena maeti Metsakalmistule 1933. aastal Eduard Vilde.
The original area of the cemetery was 24.2 hectares, but since then, it has expanded to be 48.3 hectares.
Algul oli pindala 24,2 ha, praegu on see kasvanud 48,3 hektarini.
Metsakalmistu was officially unveiled in 1939.
Metsakalmistu avati ametlikult 1939. aastal.
In the same year, 15 people were buried in the cemetery.[1] In 1939, the nearby Kloostrimetsa Farm cemetery was created, which eventually, through expansion, became part of an expanded Metsakalmistu.
Samal aastal maeti kalmistule 15 inimest.[1] 1939. aastal rajati Metsakalmistu lähedal ka Kloostrimetsa talu kalmistu, mis praeguseks on jäänud Metsakalmistu laienenud ala sisse.
At first, the designers of the cemetery were unanimous about the general design requirements of the cemetery, but the area still dominated by the appearance of a wild forest.
Algul poldud kalmistu üldiste kujundusnõuete suhtes üksmeelsed, kuid domineerima jäi ikkagi loodusliku metsa ilme.
Initially, the placement of crosses, girders, ranks, and the largest size of the calcareous stone were 80 x 50 cm.
Esialgu keelati ka ristide, piirdeaedade, rantide paigutus, kalmukivi suurimad mõõdud olid 80×50 cm.

(src)="82781"> Zika virus ( ZIKV ) is a member of the Flaviviridae virus family and the Flavivirus genus , transmitted by daytime-active Aedes mosquitoes , such as A. aegypti .
(trg)="560"> Zika virus ( ZIKV ) je član obitelji  Flaviviridae virusa i Flavivirus  rod , prenosi dana-aktivni  Aedes  komarci , kao  A. aegypti .
(src)="82782"> In humans , the virus causes a mild illness known as Zika fever , Zika , or Zika disease , which since the 1950s has been known to occur within a narrow equatorial belt from Africa to Asia .
(src)="82783"> In 2014 , the virus spread eastward across the Pacific Ocean to French Polynesia , then to Easter Island and in 2015 to Mexico , Central America , the Caribbean , and South America , where the Zika outbreak has reached pandemic levels.[1 ]

(src)="83196"> Because a tachyon would always move faster than light , it would not be possible to see it approaching .
(src)="83197"> After a tachyon has passed nearby , we would be able to see two images of it , appearing and departing in opposite directions .
(src)="83198"> The black line is the shock wave of Cherenkov radiation , shown only in one moment of time .
(src)="83199"> This double image effect is most prominent for an observer located directly in the path of a superluminal object ( in this example a sphere , shown in grey ) .
(src)="83200"> The right hand bluish shape is the image formed by the blue-doppler shifted light arriving at the observer—who is located at the apex of the black Cherenkov lines—from the sphere as it approaches .
(src)="83201"> The left-hand reddish image is formed from red-shifted light that leaves the sphere after it passes the observer .
(src)="83202"> Because the object arrives before the light , the observer sees nothing until the sphere starts to pass the observer , after which the image-as-seen-by-the-observer splits into two—one of the arriving sphere ( to the right ) and one of the departing sphere ( to the left ) .

(src)="83203"> A tachyon /ˈtæki.ɒn/ or tachyonic particle is a hypothetical particle that always moves faster than light .
(src)="83204"> The word comes from the Greek : ταχύ pronounced tachy /ˈtɑːxi/ , meaning rapid .
(src)="83205"> It was coined in 1967 by Gerald Feinberg.[1 ] The complementary particle types are called luxon ( always moving at the speed of light ) and bradyon ( always moving slower than light ) , which both exist .
(src)="83206"> The possibility of particles moving faster than light was first proposed by O. M. P. Bilaniuk , V. K. Deshpande , and E. C. G. Sudarshan in 1962 , although the term they used for it was " meta-particle".[2 ]

(src)="83413"> The Lambda baryons are a family of subatomic hadron particles that have the symbols Λ0 , Λ+ c , Λ0 b , and Λ+ t and have +1 elementary charge or are neutral .
(src)="83414"> They are baryons containing three different quarks : one up , one down , and one third quark , which can be a strange ( Λ0 ) , a charm ( Λ+ c ) , a bottom ( Λ0 b ) , or a top ( Λ+ t ) quark .

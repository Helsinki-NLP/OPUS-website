(src)="296040"> The Nauruan[1 ] or Nauru[4][5][6 ] language ( dorerin Naoero ) is an Oceanic language , spoken natively by around 6,000 people in the island country of Nauru .
(src)="296041"> Its relationship to the other Micronesian languages is not well understood .
(trg)="86"> T '  Nauruan [ 1 ] o  Nauru [ 4 ] [ 5 ] [ 6 ] laenghwij ( dorerin Naoero ) esa  Oceanic laenghwij , tal ' n nativlii b ' abaut 6 000 piopel i ' t ' ailen kuntrii  o '  Nauru .
(trg)="87"> Ets relayshunship t ' t ' otha Micronesian laenghwijs es nor understood wel .
(src)="296042"> Nauruan has 16–17 consonant phonemes .
(src)="296043"> Nauruan makes phonemic contrasts between velarized and palatalized labial consonants .
(src)="296044"> Velarization is not apparent before long back vowels and palatalization is not apparent before non-low front vowels.[7 ]
(trg)="88"> Nauruan haufs 16 – 17 consonant  fonemes .
(trg)="89"> Nauruan meiks fonemic kontrasts b ' twiin welariz ' d an palataliz ' d labial consonants .
(trg)="90"> Welarizayshun es nor wisibul b ' foor lon bak wowels an palatalizyshun es nor wisibul b ' foor non-lo frunt wowels . [ 7 ]

(src)="296050"> Moriori is an extinct[3 ] Polynesian language most closely related to New Zealand Māori .
(src)="296051"> It is the native language of the Moriori , the indigenous people of the Chatham Islands ( Rēkohu in Moriori ) - an archipelago of New Zealand , south east of New Zealand 's South Island .
(trg)="96"> Moriori  esa [ 3 ] Polynesian laenghwij  mos rilii riilated t '  Nyuu Ziilan Māori .
(trg)="97"> Ets  t ' natiw laenghwij o ' dem  Moriori , t ' endijenus piopel o ' dem  Chatham Ailen  ( Rēkohu  i ' Moriori ) - an arkipelago o '  Nyuu Ziilan , sowth iist o ' Nyuu Ziilan ' s Sowth Ailen .
(src)="296052"> The Chatham Island 's first European contact was with William R. Broughton of Great Britain who landed on November 29 , 1791 and claimed the islands .
(src)="296053"> He renamed his ship , HMS Chatham .
(src)="296054"> Broughton 's crewmen intermarried with the women of Moriori.[4 ]
(trg)="98"> Dem Chatham Ailen ' s fas Uropean kontakt wos wit ' William R. Broughton o ' t ' Yunitid Kingdum huu lan ' d a ' 29 Nowemba 1791 an klaim ' d dem ailen .
(trg)="99"> Hii renam ' d hiis shep , HMS Chatham .
(trg)="100"> Broughton ' s crewmen intermarrii ' d wit ' dem laaidiis o ' Moriori . [ 4 ]
(src)="296055"> The invasion from Taranaki had a heavy impact on Moriori population , culture and language , with only 101 Moriori remaining in 1862,[5 ] and few speaking the language by the 1870s.[6 ] However , Samuel Deighton , Resident Magistrate on the Chathams from 1873 to 1891 , compiled a short vocabulary of Moriori words , with their equivalents in Māori and English .
(src)="296056"> The vocabulary was published as an appendix of Michael King 's Moriori : A People Rediscovered .
(trg)="101"> T ' enwayshun  f ' Taranaki haufd a hewii empakt a ' Moriori popyulayshun , kulchur an laenghwij , wit ' onli 101 Moriori steyin i ' 1862 , [ 5 ] an few tal ' n t ' laenghwij b ' t ' 1870s . [ 6 ]  Tho , Samuel Deighton , Resident Magistrate a ' dem Chathams f ' 1873 t ' 1891 , complii ' d a short wokabyularii o ' dem Moriori word , wit ' deir ekwiwalents i ' Māori an Inglish .
(trg)="102"> T ' wokabyularii wos publish ' d as an appendiks o '  Michael King ' s Moriori : A Piopel Rediiscower ' d .
(src)="296057"> The genocide of the Moriori people occurred during the fall of 1835 .
(src)="296058"> The invasions of the Chatham Islands left the Moriori people and their culture to die off .
(src)="296059"> Those who survived were either kept as slaves or eaten and Moriori were not sanctioned to marry other Moriori or have children within their race .
(src)="296060"> This caused their people and their language to be endangered .
(src)="296061"> There were only 101 Moriori people left out of 2000 who had survived in 1863.[7 ]
(trg)="103"> T ' jenosiid o ' t ' Moriori piopel okkur ' d durin t ' fawl o ' 1835 .
(trg)="104"> T ' enwayshun o ' dem Chatham Ailen left dem Moriori piopel an deir kulchur t ' dy of .
(trg)="105"> Dos huu surwiiw ' d wos iider kept as slaaws o eat ' n an Moriori wos nor saankshun ' d t ' marrii otha Moriori o hauf children wit ' i ' deir raas .
(trg)="106"> Dis  kaus ' d deir piopel an deir laenghwij t ' b ' endanjer ' d .
(trg)="107"> Deir wos onli 101 Moriori piopel left outta 2 000 huu haufd surwiiw ' d i ' 1863 . [ 7 ]
(src)="296062"> The language was reconstructed for Barry Barclay 's 2000 film documentary The Feathers of Peace,[8 ] in a recreation of Moriori contact with Pākehā and Māori .
(trg)="108"> T ' laenghwij wos riimeik ' d f '  Barry Barclay ' s 2000 film dokumentarii  T ' Featas o ' Piis , [ 8 ] i ' a riimeikin o ' Moriori kontakt wit '  Pākehā an Māori .
(src)="296063"> In 2001 , as part of a cultural revival movement , Moriori people began attempts to revive the language , and compiled a database of Moriori words.[9 ] There is a POLLEX ( Polynesian Lexicon Project Online ) database of Moriori words as well.[10 ]
(trg)="109"> I ' 2001 , as part o ' a kulchural riiwiwal moowment , Moriori piopel b ' gan attempts t ' riiwiwe t ' laenghwij , an kompiil ' d a database o ' dem Moriori word . [ 9 ]  Deir esa POLLEX ( Polynesian Lexicon Project Online ) database o ' dem Moriori word tuu . [ 10 ]
(src)="296064"> The 2006 New Zealand census[4 ] showed 945 people choosing to include " Moriori " amongst their tribal affiliations , compared to 35 people in the 1901 census.[11 ]
(trg)="110"> T '  2006 New Zealand census [ 4 ] show ' d 945 piopel choosin t ' include " Moriori " amonst deir triibal affiliayshuns , compar ' d t ' 35 piopel i ' t ' 1901 census . [ 11 ]
(src)="296065"> One hypothesis that was discredited in the 1960s and 1970s stated the Moriori people were distinct from the Māori settlers of New Zealand , and another hypothesis among archaeologists who believed that the Moriori people who settled on the Chatham Islands were originally Māori.[4 ]
(trg)="111"> Wan hipotesis tat wos diskredited i ' t ' 1960s an 1970s staited dem Moriori piopel wos distinkt f ' dem Māori set ' las o ' Nyuu Ziilan , an anotha hipotesis amon dem aarkaeolojists huu biiliew ' d tat dem Moriori piopel huu set ' l ' d a ' dem Chatham Ailen wos orijinalii Māori . [ 4 ]
(src)="296066"> The genealogical history of the Moriori language exposes the Proto-tahitic subgroup that separates into four languages : Tahitian , Rarotongan , Tuamotuan , and Maori .
(src)="296067"> Moriori is considered a dialect of Maori .
(src)="296068"> The two languages share similar phonological structure.[12 ]
(trg)="112"> T ' jiiniaalojikel histrei o ' t ' Moriori laenghwij ekspooses t ' Proto-tahitic subgroup tat separaits i ' t ' dem for laenghwijs : Tahitian , Rarotongan , Tuamotuan , an Māori .
(trg)="113"> Moriori es konsidired a dialekt o ' Maori .

(trg)="52579"> Eluveitie ( /ɛlˈveɪti/ el-VAY-ti)[6 ] is a Swiss folk metal band from Winterthur , Zurich , founded in 2002 by Chrigel Glanzmann .
(trg)="52580"> The project 's first EP , Vên , was released in 2003 .
(trg)="52581"> Vên was a studio project of Glanzmann 's , but its success led to the recruitment of a full band .
(trg)="52582"> The band then released a full-length album , Spirit , in June 2006 .
(trg)="52583"> In November 2007 , Eluveitie was signed by Nuclear Blast.[7 ] The first product of the collaboration , Slania , was released in February 2008 .
(trg)="52584"> The album peaked at number 35 in the Swiss charts and number 72 in the German charts.[8][9 ]

(trg)="52585"> Eluveitie describe themselves as " The new wave of folk metal".[10 ] The band 's style incorporates characteristics of melodic death metal combined with the melodies of traditional Celtic music .
(trg)="52586"> They use traditional European instruments , including the hurdy-gurdy and bagpipes , amidst guitars and both clean and harsh vocals .
(trg)="52587"> Their lyrics include references to Celtic mythology , particularly the Celtic Gaul village .
(trg)="52588"> The lyrics are often in the extinct ancient language Gaulish .
(trg)="52589"> The name of the band comes from graffiti on a vessel from Mantua ( ca .
(trg)="52590"> 300 BC).[11 ] The inscription in Etruscan letters reads eluveitie , which has been interpreted as the Etruscan form of the Celtic (h)elvetios ( “ the Helvetian”),[12 ] presumably referring to a man of Helvetian descent living in Mantua .

(trg)="52625"> Eluveitie infuses traditional Celtic folk melodies with Gothenburg-styled melodic death metal .

(trg)="52627"> The traditional folk tunes in their songs have been drawn from various sources , including traditional Irish reels .
(trg)="52628"> While many of their lyrics are in English , some are in the ancient extinct Gaulish language .
(trg)="52629"> In some instances , the two languages are intertwined throughout a song .

(trg)="52631"> Their lyrics are based on texts written in Gaulish such as prayers , invocations of the gods and other spirits.[32 ]

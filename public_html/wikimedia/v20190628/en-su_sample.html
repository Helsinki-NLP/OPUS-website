(src)="379757"> Slow lorises are a group of several species of nocturnal strepsirrhine primates which make up the genus Nycticebus .
(src)="379758"> Found in Southeast Asia and bordering areas , they range from Bangladesh and Northeast India in the west to the Sulu Archipelago in the Philippines in the east , and from Yunnan province in China in the north to the island of Java in the south .
(src)="379759"> Although many previous classifications recognized as few as a single all-inclusive species , there are now at least eight that are considered valid : the Sunda slow loris ( N.  coucang ) , Bengal slow loris ( N.  bengalensis ) , pygmy slow loris ( N.  pygmaeus ) , Javan slow loris ( N.  javanicus ) , Philippine slow loris ( N.  menagensis ) , Bangka slow loris ( N.  bancanus ) , Bornean slow loris ( N.  borneanus ) , and Kayan River slow loris ( N.  kayan ) .
(src)="379760"> The group 's closest relatives are the slender lorises of southern India and Sri Lanka .
(src)="379761"> Their next closest relatives are the African lorisids , the pottos , false pottos , and angwantibos .
(src)="379762"> They are less closely related to the remaining lorisoids ( the various types of galago ) , and more distantly to the lemurs of Madagascar .
(src)="379763"> Their evolutionary history is uncertain since their fossil record is patchy and molecular clock studies have given inconsistent results .

(src)="379917"> The Sunda slow loris ( Nycticebus coucang ) or greater slow loris is a strepsirrhine primate and a species of slow loris native to Indonesia , western Malaysia , southern Thailand and Singapore .
(src)="379918"> It measures 27 to 38  cm ( 11 to 15  in ) from head to tail and weighs between 599 and 685  g ( 21.1 and 24.2  oz ) .
(src)="379919"> Like other slow lorises , it has a wet nose ( rhinarium ) , a round head , small ears hidden in thick fur , a flat face , large eyes and a vestigial tail .

(src)="379920"> The Sunda slow loris is nocturnal and arboreal , typically occurring in evergreen forests .
(src)="379921"> It prefers rainforests with continuous dense canopies and has an extremely low metabolic rate compared to other mammals of its size .
(src)="379922"> Its diet consists of sap , floral nectar , fruit and arthropods .
(src)="379923"> It will feed on exudates such as gum and sap by licking wounds in trees .
(src)="379924"> The species is generally solitary ; one study showed only 8 % of its active time was spent near other individuals .
(src)="379925"> Social behavior makes up a very small part of the activity budget , though it has monogamous mating system with the offspring living with the parents .
(src)="379926"> It sleeps during the day , rolled up in a ball in hidden parts of trees above the ground , often on branches , twigs , palm fronds , or lianas .
(src)="379927"> The species is polyoestrous , usually giving birth to a single offspring after a gestation period of 192 days .
(src)="379928"> The young disperses between 16 and 27 months , generally when it is sexually mature .

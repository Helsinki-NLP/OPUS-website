(src)="427282"> Lasagne ( /ləˈzænjə/ or /ləˈzɑːnjə/ or /ləˈsɑːnjə/ , Italian pronunciation :  [ laˈzaɲɲe ] , singular lasagna ) are wide , flat-shaped pasta , and possibly one of the oldest types of pasta.[1 ] The word " lasagne " , and , in American English , the singular " lasagna " , can also refer to a dish made with several layers of lasagne sheets alternated with sauces and various other ingredients .
(trg)="5"> Лазанья  ( итальян дылга адаарга : / лазанья / азы  / лазаанья / азы  / ласанья / ) – калбак болгаш каъттарлыг  паста-дыр , ону эң бурунгу пасталарның бирээзи деп санап болур . [ 1 ]  Лазанья ( " lasagne " ) деп сөс-биле калбак каът-каът далганнар аразынга соустар болгаш янзы-бүрү холумактарлыг чемни база адап болур .
(src)="427283"> Lasagne originated in Italy during the Middle Ages and has traditionally been ascribed to the city of Naples ( Campania ) .
(src)="427284"> The first recorded recipe was set down in the early 14th century Liber de Coquina ( The Book of Cookery).[2 ] It bore only a slight resemblance to the later traditional form of lasagne , featuring a fermented dough , flattened into a thin sheet , boiled , sprinkled with cheese and spices , and then eaten with the use of a small pointed stick.[3 ] Other recipes written in the century following the Liber de Coquina recommended boiling the pasta in a chicken broth and dressing it with cheese and chicken fat , or in one case walnuts , in a recipe adapted for the Lenten fast.[3 ]

(src)="427287"> In Ancient Rome , there was a dish similar to the traditional lasagne one called lasana or lasanum ( Latin word for " container " , " pot " ) described in the book De re coquinaria by Marcus Gavius Apicius,[8 ] but the word could have a more ancient origin .
(src)="427288"> The first theory is that lasagne comes from Greek λάγανον ( laganon ) , a flat sheet of pasta dough cut into strips.[9][10][11][12 ] The word λαγάνα ( lagana ) is still used in Greek to mean a flat thin type of unleavened bread baked for the Clean Monday holiday .

(src)="111425"> Tongue-and-groove pliers—also known as water pump pliers , adjustable pliers , groove-joint pliers , arc-joint pliers , Multi-Grips , tap or pipe spanners , gland pliers and Channellocks—are a type of slip-joint pliers .
(src)="111426"> They have serrated jaws generally set 45 to 60 degrees from the handles .
(src)="111427"> The lower jaw can be moved to a number of positions by sliding along a tracking section under the upper jaw .
(src)="111428"> An advantage of this design is that the pliers can adjust to a number of sizes without the distance in the handle growing wider .
(src)="111429"> These pliers often have long handles—commonly 9.5 to 12  inches long—for increased leverage.[1][2][3 ]

(src)="111729"> The Brewster F2A Buffalo[1 ] was an American fighter aircraft which saw service early in World War II .
(src)="111730"> Designed and built by the Brewster Aeronautical Corporation , it was one of the first U.S. monoplanes with an arrestor hook and other modifications for aircraft carriers .
(src)="111731"> The Buffalo won a competition against the Grumman F4F Wildcat in 1939 to become the U.S. Navy 's first monoplane fighter aircraft .
(src)="111732"> Although superior to the Grumman F3F biplane it replaced and the early F4Fs,[2 ] the Buffalo was largely obsolete when the United States entered World War 2 being unstable and overweight , especially when compared to the Japanese Mitsubishi A6M Zero.[3 ]

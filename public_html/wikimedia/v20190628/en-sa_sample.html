(src)="300752"> Arignar Anna Zoological Park ( abbreviated AAZP ) , also known as the Vandalur Zoo , is a zoological garden located in Vandalur , a suburb in the southwestern part of Chennai , Tamil Nadu , about 31 kilometres ( 19  mi ) from the city centre and 15 kilometres ( 9.3  mi ) from Chennai Airport .
(src)="300753"> It previous location was set up in 1855 and was the first public zoo in India .
(src)="300754"> It is affiliated with the Central Zoo Authority of India.[8 ] Spread over an area of 602 hectares ( 1,490 acres ) , including a 92.45-hectare ( 228.4-acre ) rescue and rehabilitation centre , the park is the largest zoological garden in India .
(src)="300755"> The zoo houses 2,553 species of flora and fauna across 1,265 acres ( 512  ha ) .
(src)="300756"> As of 2012 the park houses around 1,500 wild species , including 46 endangered species , in its 160 enclosures.[9 ] As of 2010 , there were about 47 species of mammals , 63 species of birds , 31 species of reptiles , 5 species of amphibians , 25 species of fishes , and 10 species of insects in the park.[10 ] The park , with an objective to be a repository of the state 's fauna , is credited with being the second wildlife sanctuary in Tamil Nadu after Mudumalai National Park.[11 ]

(src)="300943"> Bhojpuri ( /boʊdʒˈpʊəri/ ; [ 5 ] भोजपुरी  ) is an Indo-Aryan language spoken in the Northern-Eastern part of India and the Terai region of Nepal.[2 ] It is chiefly spoken in eastern Uttar Pradesh , western Bihar , and in extreme northwestern part of Jharkhand in India.[6 ] Bhojpuri is sometimes considered one of the seven Hindi languages ( Haryanvi , Braj , Awadhi , Bhojpuri , Bundeli , Bagheli and Kannauji),[7 ] although it officially belongs to the Bihari branch of Eastern Indo-Aryan .
(src)="300944"> Fiji Hindi , an official language of Fiji , has a strong Bhojpuri influence .
(src)="300945"> Bhojpuri is one of the recognized national languages of Nepal and official language of Indian state Jharkhand .
(src)="300946"> It is also a recognized language in Guyana , Suriname , and Mauritius.[8][9 ] Bhojpuri is also spoken by the first generation immigrants who migrated from the UP region which now encompasses parts of Bihar and Uttar Pradesh in India to the newly created Pakistan .
(src)="300947"> It is , however , slowly dying out in Pakistan as the next generation prefers to speak Urdu , Pakistan 's national language and lingua franca .

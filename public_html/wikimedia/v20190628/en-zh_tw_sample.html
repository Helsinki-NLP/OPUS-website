(src)="457563"> Agnotology ( formerly agnatology ) is the study of culturally induced ignorance or doubt , particularly the publication of inaccurate or misleading scientific data .
(src)="457564"> The neologism was coined by Robert N. Proctor,[1][2 ] a Stanford University professor specializing in the history of science and technology.[3 ] Its name derives from the Neoclassical Greek word ἄγνωσις , agnōsis , " not knowing " ( confer Attic Greek ἄγνωτος " unknown"[4 ] ) , and -λογία , -logia.[5 ] More generally , the term also highlights the increasingly common condition where more knowledge of a subject leaves one more uncertain than before .

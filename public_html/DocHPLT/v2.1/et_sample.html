    <s id="4.1">EarCave re-release of I Don&apos;t Have To Cry</s>
  </P>
  <P id="5">
    <s id="5.1">I was positively surprised by Andrew Morgans decision to re-release my and Maryn E.</s>
    <s id="5.2">Coote&apos;s 1984 single &quot;I Don&apos;t Have To Cry&quot;.</s>
    <s id="5.3">He did switch the song on the b-side for &quot;Vision of Estonia&quot; from the same year.</s>
  </P>
  <P id="6">
    <s id="6.1">Trying to remember the production notes of this vinyl..</s>
    <s id="6.2">It was recorded at a rented home-studio in Stockholm.</s>
    <s id="6.3">We did rent (with the generous help from our manager at that time Harry Kask R.I.P.) a small Fostex 8 track studio rack, consisting of the multitrack, mixer, two compressors, a spring reverb and an equalizer..</s>
  </P>
  <P id="7">
    <s id="7.1">We used the Oberheim DX drum-machine.</s>
    <s id="7.2">We couldn&apos;t locate a DMX or afford a Linn..</s>
    <s id="7.3">Keys were played on a Yamaha DX 9 - again, we couln&apos;t get the DX 7..</s>
  </P>
  <P id="8">
    <s id="8.1">Maryn played all keyboards, I did the drum programming, bass (a Fender Mustang) and the vocoder ( Syntovox 222 )..</s>
    <s id="8.2">I did the engineering aswell.</s>
    <s id="8.3">Maryn plays also the flute on the B-side.</s>
    <s id="8.4">We had help on the guitar part.</s>
  </P>
  <P id="9">
    <s id="9.1">An interesting addition was the Roland SDE 1000 delay, that we rigged up along with the Yamaha CS 10 mono synth to do some primitive sampling effects..</s>
  </P>
  <P id="10">
    <s id="10.1">Anyway, I hope this single gets back to the dancefloors where it has always belonged.</s>
    <s id="10.2">You can buy the single at: http://earcave.mybisi.com/product/uq001</s>
  </P>
